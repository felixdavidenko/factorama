Interesting facts about Chile

Interesting facts about Chile presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Chile is a country in the southwest of South America, which occupies a narrow strip of land with a length of more than 6,000 km (3,730 mi), washed by the waters of the Pacific Ocean. The capital of the country, Santiago, is located in a valley surrounded by the Cordillera Coast and the Andes mountains. The city's palm-lined Plaza de Armas houses the neoclassical cathedral and the National History Museum. The country is famous for one of the world's largest swimming pools, the Atacama Desert, numerous glaciers and not only. Fact #1. The world famous Easter Island belongs to Chile

The first one of fun facts about Chile regards the Easter Island, located in the southeastern Pacific Ocean. This island is one of the world's most famous landmarks, renowned for its nearly 1,000 human statues called "Moai". Since 1995 it’s a World Heritage Site.

Fact #2. The Chilean Spanish is not like any other Spanish

The official language in Chile is Spanish. But local Spanish is distinctively accented and quite unlike the "classic" or "common" Spanish. In Chilean version of Spanish final syllables are often dropped and some consonants are usually pronounced softly.

Fact #3. One of the world’s largest swimming pools is located in Chile

Here’s another one of interesting facts about Chile. The city of Algarrobo is home to the world's 2nd largest pool inscribed in the Guinness Book of Records. It covers the area of 8 ha (20 acres), its length is 1 km (0,6 mi) and its max depth is 3,5 m (11,5 ft).

Fact #4. The world's largest bird is a national symbol of Chile

The Andean condor is the world's largest flying bird. The condor is a scavenger that lives generally on inaccessible rock ledges. It plays such an important role in the Chilean folklore and mythology that it was depicted on the country's coat of arms.

Fact #5. The oldest known mummy was found in Chile

The next one of interesting facts about Chile regards mummies. The fact is that the oldest known mummy was found not in Egypt but in Chile. It was one of the totals of 282 Chichorro mummies found and removed from burial sites in the Camarones Valley.

Fact #6. The Chile’s Atacama Desert is one of the driest places on Earth

The Chile's Atacama Desert is covering a 1,600 km (990 mi) strip of land on the Pacific Coast of the country. This desert is considered one of the driest places on Earth. Because of extreme aridity, some parts of the region have never seen a tiny drop of rain.

Fact #7. There is a city in Chile inscribed in the UNESCO World Heritage List

Another one of fun facts about Chile regards the city of Valparaiso. It’s a major industrial seaport and educational center. The city is world famous for its vivid and colorful historical center, which was inscribed in the UNESCO World Heritage List in 2003.

Fact #8. Lapageria is the national flower of Chile

Lapageria is a flowering plant that has only one specie Lapageria rosea, also known as the Chilean bellflower. It grows in forests of the southern part of Chile, but it's very rare. Lapageria is a symbol of national identity that was declared a national flower in 1977.

Fact #9. Chile is a natural habitat for penguins

The next one of interesting facts about Chile regards penguins. Most people assume that Antarctica is the only natural habitat where penguins can be met. But in fact, Chile is home to several colonies of King, Emperor, Magellanic penguins and not only.

Fact #10. Chile is one of the world's largest wine producers

Chile is renowned for its wine. The first wine grape was planted here in 1554 by Spanish Conquistadores. Today, this country is home to over 1,200 km (745 mi) of viticulture valleys, growing in 14 regions and producing about 10 mil hectoliters of wine per year.
