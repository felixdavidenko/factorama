Interesting facts about South Africa

Interesting facts about South Africa presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. South Africa is the southernmost country on the African continent with diverse ecosystems. Large animals such as elephants, hippos, giraffes and lions can be admired on a safari in the Kruger National Park in the hinterland. The Western Cape is famous for its beaches, lush vineyards surrounding the towns of Stellenbosch and Paarl, rocky cliffs at the Cape of Good Hope, forests and lagoons along the Garden Route, and Cape Town, dominated by the flat-topped Table Mountain. The country is also famous for its three capitals, platinum reserves, numerous languages and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about South Africa for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. South Africa has three capitals at the same time

Here goes the first one of fun facts about South Africa. Unlike many other countries, this one has three capitals: legislative Cape Town, executive Pretoria and judicial Bloemfontein. What's also curious is that the largest city is Johannesburg.

Fact #2. South Africa is one of the world's largest platinum producers

South Africa is known to produce more platinum than any other country in the world. It's known that in 2005 the country produced almost 80% of the world's platinum. In 2010 the country produced more than 163,000 kg (5,200,000 oz) of platinum.

Fact #3. South Africa is the southernmost country in the Eastern Hemisphere

Here's another one of interesting facts about South Africa. The country is located at the southern tip of the African continent, washed by the South Atlantic and Indian Oceans to the south and is known as the southernmost country in the Eastern Hemisphere.

Fact #4. The father of modern fantasy literature was born in South Africa

John Ronald Reuel Tolkien was born in 1892 in Bloemfontein, South Africa. On 21 September 1937 was published his fantasy novel "The Hobbit", on 29 July 1954 "The Lord of the Rings". Today he is known as the "Father of modern fantasy literature".

Fact #5. South Africa is the most populated country located south of the equator

The next one of fun facts about South Africa regards population. The country is inhabited by over 59 million people. Thus, South Africa is known as the world's 24th most populated nation and the most populated country entirely located south of the equator.

Fact #6. Barbecue in South Africa is called Braai

In South Africa, there is such a term as a "Braai". This term comes from the Afrikaans language and means barbecue or grill. The word has been adopted by English speaking citizens and also serves as a verb when describing how the food was made.

Fact #7. There are 11 official languages in South Africa

Another one of interesting facts about South Africa regards its languages. The country is known to have 11 official languages. This is the fourth highest number in the world. The most spoken languages are Zulu (22,7%), Xhosa (16%) and Afrikaans (13,5%).

Fact #8. South Africa is home to more than 67,000 animal species

According to the South African National Biodiversity Institute (SANBI), the country is home to more that 67,000 animal species. Almost half of the species of reptiles, amphibians, butterflies and fish are endemic to South Africa and its exclusive economic zone.

Fact #9. South Africa has the most number of UNESCO sites on the continent

The next one of fun facts about South Africa regards UNESCO World Heritage Sites. As of 2020, the country has a total of 10 sites inscribed in the UNESCO World Heritage List. Among them, 5 sites are cultural, 4 sites are natural and 1 site is mixed.

Fact #10. South Africa has one of the world's highest bridge bungee

The Bloukrans is an arch bridge located near Nature's Valley, South Africa. It stands above the Bloukrans River and houses one of the world's highest commercial bungee at 216 m (709 ft). The Bloukrans Bridge Bungy has been operating since 1997.
