Interesting facts about Nicaragua

Interesting facts about Nicaragua presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. Nicaragua is a state in Central America lying between the Pacific Ocean and the Caribbean Sea. Its landscape is formed by lakes, volcanoes and beaches. To the north of the country's capital, Managua, are the vast Lake Managua and the famous Momotombo stratovolcano. To the south lies the city of Granada, known for its Spanish colonial architecture, and several islets that are home to a variety of tropical birds. The country is also famous for its archaeological sites, lake sharks, UNESCO's cathedral and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Nicaragua.

Fact #1. Nicaragua is the largest country in the Central American isthmus

The first one of fun facts about Nicaragua regards its size. The official name of the country is The Republic of Nicaragua. With the total area of 130,375 sq km (50,338 sq mi), Nicaragua is known as the largest country in the Central American isthmus.

Fact #2. The capital of Nicaragua is the third largest city in Central America

Managua is the capital of Nicaragua. With the population of over 1 mil people, Managua is the largest city of the country. With an area of over 267 sq km (103 sq mi), Managua is the third largest city in Central America after Tegucigalpa and Guatemala.

Fact #3. The largest lake in Central America is located in Nicaragua

The next one of fun facts about Nicaragua regards Lake Nicaragua. With an area of 8,264 sq km (3,191 sq mi), it's the largest freshwater lake in Central America. Also, it's the world's 19th largest lake and the 10th largest lake in the Americas.

Fact #4. Nicaragua is home to more than 1,400 animal species

Nicaragua is known as one of the world's most biodiverse countries. The country has more than 1,400 registered animal species. It’s home to 705 bird species, 640 fish species, 248 species of amphibians, 183 species of mammals and not only.

Fact #5. There is an archaeological island in Nicaragua with 1,700 petroglyphs

Another one of interesting facts about Nicaragua regards petroglyphs. Ometepe is a volcanic island rising out of Lake Nicaragua. The island has a total of 73 archaeological sites with more than 1,700 petroglyphs depicted on more than 1,400 boulders.

Fact #6. Both Nicaragua and Costa Rica has the same national dish

Gallo Pinto is a traditional Nicaraguan dish that the country claims to be its national. The dish consists of rice and beans as a base, and has a lot of variations. The origin of the dish is uncertain, but both Nicaragua and Costa Rica claim it as their own.

Fact #7. There is a canyon of national importance in Nicaragua

Here goes the next one of fun facts about Nicaragua. Somoto Canyon National Monument, located in the Madriz Department is a protected area of about 170 h (420 acres). It was discovered only in 2004 and in 2006 it was declared the national monument.

Fact #8. The national bird of Nicaragua is the national bird of El Salvador

The turquoise-browed motmot, known locally as 'Guardabarranco' is a colorful 34 cm (13 in) long bird that weighs up to 65 g (2,3 oz) and lives in forest edges and scrublands. What’s curious, this bird is the national symbol of both Nicaragua and El Salvador.

Fact #9. Nicaragua is home to 2,000 years old footprints

Another one of interesting facts about Nicaragua regards footprints. The Ancient footprints of Acahualinca were first discovered in 1874. The scientific analysis conducted in the 1970s demonstrated that tracks are about 2,120 years old.

Fact #10. Nicaragua is home to 12,000 species of plants

Nicaragua is known to have a total of 78 protected areas, covering over 22,000 sq km (8,500 sq mi) accounting for about 17% of the country's landmass. The country is home to some 12,000 species of plants, half of which are even not yet classified.
