Interesting facts about the Netherlands

Interesting facts about the Netherlands presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. The Netherlands is a flat country in northwestern Europe known for its canals, tulip fields, windmills and bike paths. From the Golden Age of the Netherlands (17th century), mansions along the canals, as well as the famous paintings by Rembrandt and Vermeer, have survived. The country is also famous for its Caribbean municipalities, coffeeshops, flower gardens and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about the Netherlands.

Fact #1. The Netherlands consists of European provinces and Caribbean municipalities

The first one of fun facts about the Netherlands regards its territory. The Netherlands is a constituent country that in Europe consists of twelve provinces while in the Caribbean, it consists of three special municipalities: the islands of Bonaire, Sint Eustatius and Saba.

Fact #2. There are more bicycles in the Netherlands than citizens

Cycling is a common mode of transport in the Netherlands. The country is known to have at least 18 million bicycles, more than one per capita and twice as many as (9 million) motor vehicles. Busy roads have 35,000 km (21,747 mi) of dedicated cycle tracks.

Fact #3. The largest seaport in Europe is located in the Netherlands

Here's the next one of interesting facts about the Netherlands. The Port of Rotterdam, located in the province of South Holland is the largest seaport in Europe. With a total area of over 12,713 h (31,410 acres) it's also the largest seaport outside of Asia.

Fact #4. The national symbol of the Netherlands is the black-tailed godwit

The black-tailed godwit is a long-legged and long-billed shorebird. It measures 42 cm (17 in) from bill to tail, weighs around 280 g (9,9 oz) and has an average wingspan of up to 82 cm (32 in). The black-tailed godwit is the national bird of the Netherlands.

Fact #5. The name of the Netherlands is derived from its topography

Another one of fun facts about the Netherlands regards its name. Netherlands literally means "lower countries" in reference to the country's low elevation with only about 50% of its land exceeding 1 m (3,3 ft) above sea level and 26% falling below sea level.

Fact #6. The Wadden Sea in the Netherlands is the stopover for white-tailed eagles

The Wadden Sea is an intertidal zone part of the North Sea in the northwest of the Netherlands. The Wadden Sea is famous for its rich fauna, especially birds. The area is used as a migration stopover for waders, ducks, gulls, terns, herons and white-tailed eagles.

Fact #7. The Netherlands is one of the founding members of the European Union

Here goes another one of interesting facts about the Netherlands. This country is one of the founding members of the European Union, Eurozone, NATO, OECD, WTO and G10, as well as a part of the Schengen Area and the trilateral Benelux Union.

Fact #8. There are more than 100 km of canals in the Netherlands

Amsterdam, capital of the Netherlands, also known as the "Venice of the North" has more than 100 km (62 mi) of canals, about 90 islands and over 1,500 bridges. In 2010, the 17th century canal ring area was inscribed in the UNESCO World Heritage List.

Fact #9. The Netherlands is one of the world's most densely populated countries

Here goes the next one of fun facts about the Netherlands. With a population of over 17 mil people, all living within an area of roughly 41,800 sq km (16,100 sq mi), the Netherlands is the 2nd most densely populated country in the EU and the 12th in the world.

Fact #10. The Netherlands is home to the triple-point tourist mountain

The Vaalserberg is a 322,4 m (1,057 ft) tall mountain at the south-easternmost edge of the Netherlands. The mount is located at the intersection between Germany, Belgium and the Netherlands and is a well-known tourist attraction.
