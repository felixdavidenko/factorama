Interesting facts about Peru

Interesting facts about Peru presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Peru is a country in South America with the rainforests of the Amazon and Machu Picchu, an ancient Inca city at the top of a mountain range in the Andes. The area around Machu Picchu, where the Urubamba Valley (also known as the Sacred Valley of the Incas), the Inca Trail and the colonial city of Cuzco are located, is famous for its archaeological sites. The country is also famous for its rainbow mountain, the highest dune, Huacachina village and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Peru for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. The Peruvian national animal is not the alpaca

Here’s the first one of fun facts about Peru. The country is home to the three-quarters of the world's population of alpaca. But the national animal is not the alpaca, but similar to it vicuna. It has more than 20 natural colors, and its wool is considered luxurious.

Fact #2. Peru is home to the world-famous city of Machu Picchu

The most famous landmark in Peru is the ancient city of Machu Picchu. The city is located in the province of Urubamba in Cusco. These unique stone constructions are built against a cliff with a spectacular drop of 400 m (1,312 ft) over the Urubamba River.

Fact #3. The world’s highest sand dune is located in Peru

The next one of interesting facts about Peru is that it’s home to the world's highest sand dune. The dune is known as Cerro Blanco. Its height is 1,176 m (3,860 ft) from ground to summit and it can be found in the Sechura Desert in the northwest of the country.

Fact #4. It takes 6 months to weave a traditional Peruvian Poncho

Poncho is a traditional outfit in Peru. Almost every known today weaving technique was invented by Peruvians in about 3,000 B.C. It's known that it takes about 6 months to weave one poncho, and what's more interesting, it's expected to last a lifetime.

Fact #5. The Incan Empire was larger than the Roman Empire

Here’s another one of fun facts about Peru. The Roman Empire was known to be the largest of its time, but in fact, the Incan empire was larger. It had far more developed infrastructure with more than 39,992 km (24,850 mi) of roads, including bridges.

Fact #6. There are about 15 indigenous languages in Peru

The official language in Peru is Spanish, but it's not the only one. In fact, there are 2 more official languages besides Spanish: Quechua and Aymara. Moreover, deep in the Amazon jungles, there are natives who can speak 13 more indigenous languages.

Fact #7. Potato is considered to be originated in Peru

The next one of interesting facts about Peru regards potato. It's considered that potato originated in Peru, what eventually gave rise to the famous local proverb – "Soy mas Peruano que la papa". The translation is – "I am more Peruvian than the potato".

Fact #8. Peruvian shamanism traditions are one of the oldest in the world

The Peruvian shamanism tradition goes back for more than 3,000 years. The tradition is so strong, that it's popular even today. But the explanation is simple. Most people can't afford the proper medical care, so they turn to a shaman's healing procedures.

Fact #9. In Peru there is a tradition of gifting yellow pants for the New Year

The next one of fun facts about Peru regards New Year's gifts. It's believed by Peruvians that good luck in the coming year can be attracted by gifting each other yellow pants. According to the tradition, until midnight they have to be worn inside out.

Fact #10. Peru is home to the world’s largest bird

The Giant Andean Condor is considered the world's largest bird. Its natural habitat is the Colca Canyon in South Peru. Despite its huge weight of 12 kg (27 pounds), size of 1,2 m (4 ft) and wingspan of 4,2 m (14 ft), it can fly for hours without using wings.
