Interesting facts about French Polynesia

Interesting facts about French Polynesia presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. French Polynesia, an overseas territory of France, has more than 100 islands in the South Pacific Ocean, with a total length of more than 2,000 km. They are part of the Tubuai, Gambier, Marquesas, Society Islands and Tuamotu archipelagos and are known for their lagoons, coral reefs and water bungalow hotels. The highlight of the islands are white and black sand beaches, mountains, hilly uninhabited areas and majestic waterfalls. The country is also famous for black pearls, stone deities, Paul Gauguin paintings and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about French Polynesia.

Fact #1. French Polynesia consists of 118 dispersed islands

Here's the first one of fun facts about French Polynesia. The country consists of 118 dispersed islands and atolls stretching over more than 2,000 km (1,200 mi) in the South Pacific Ocean. The total area of French Polynesia is 4,167 sq km (1,609 sq mi).

Fact #2. French Polynesia is divided into five groups of islands

French Polynesia is divided into five groups of islands: the Society Islands archipelago, including the Windward Islands and the Leeward Islands; the Tuamotu Archipelago; the Gambier Islands; the Marquesas Islands; and the Austral Islands.

Fact #3. Almost 70% of the population of French Polynesia lives on one island

The next one of fun facts about French Polynesia regards its population. As of 2017 the total population of the country was 275,918 people. What's curious is that out of 188 islands, almost 68,7% of the population lives on the island of Tahiti alone.

Fact #4. One of the most important export commodities of French Polynesia is black pearls

French Polynesia exports the famous black Tahitian pearls, which accounts for almost 55% of the country's exports. The Tahitian pearl, also known as black pearl is an organic gem formed from the black lip oyster, cultivated around the island of Tahiti.

Fact #5. There is an island in French Polynesia named after the ship wreckage

Here's the next one of fun facts about French Polynesia. Matilda was a whaling ship built in 1779 and wrecked in 1792 on a shoal, later named Matilda Island. The crew of 21 members was saved. Later it turned out that Matilda Island was in fact an atoll Moruroa.

Fact #6. The only official language in French Polynesia is French

A law of 12 April 1996 states that "French is the official language, but Tahitian and other Polynesian languages can also be used". According to the 2017 census, 73,9% of people reported that the language they spoke the most at home was French.

Fact #7. The highest peak of French Polynesia is an extinct volcano

Another one of interesting facts about French Polynesia regards Mont Orohena. This mountain is an extinct volcano, located in the South Pacific, on the island of Tahiti. With an elevation of 2,241 m (7,352 ft) it is the highest peak of French Polynesia.

Fact #8. The flag of French Polynesia has a canoe with a crew of five

The flag of French Polynesia has two red horizontal stripes and one white band, representing traditional Polynesian colors. The disc in the center has the sea, the sun and a Polynesian canoe with a crew of five persons representing the five island groups.

Fact #9. French Polynesia is home to the deities that created humans

Here goes the next one of fun facts about French Polynesia. Hiva Oa is the second largest island in the Marquesas Islands. The island is world famous for the abundant number of tikis - stone carved humanoids representing deities that created humans.

Fact #10. Tahitian vanilla is one of the most in demand variety of vanilla in the world

Vanilla is a crop that was introduced in French Polynesia in 1848. A new variety of vanilla was developed in the area and became known as Tahitian vanilla. Due to its unique taste and scent it has resulted in great demand all around the world.
