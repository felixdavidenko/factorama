Interesting facts about Paraguay

Interesting facts about Paraguay presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. Paraguay is a landlocked country located between Argentina, Brazil and Bolivia. Large areas are occupied by swamps, subtropical forests and Chaco – a semi-desert area where savannah and shrub steppe are combined. The capital is located in the city of Asuncion, which lies on the banks of the Paraguay River. It is home to the Government Palace and the Del Barro Museum, displaying pre-Columbian ceramics and nanduti lace. The country is also famous for its Jesuit missions, rural tourism, one of the world's loudest birds and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Paraguay.

Fact #1. Paraguay is a landlocked country with coasts and beaches

Here's the first one of fun facts about Paraguay. Despite the country being landlocked, it has coasts, beaches and ports. That's because Paraguay has Paraguay and Parana rivers that form the Parana-Paraguay Waterway giving the exit to the Atlantic Ocean.

Fact #2. Paraguay has two Jesuit missions considered a single World Heritage Site

The Jesuit Missions of La Santisima Trinidad de Parana and Jesus de Tavarangue, located in the Itapua Department, Paraguay, were built in 1609. Both were developing for 150 years and in 1993 ruins of both missions were declared UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Fact #3. Climate in Paraguay is both tropical and subtropical

Here's another one of interesting facts about Paraguay. The Tropic of Capricorn divides the Southern Temperate Zone to the south and the tropics to the north. Since Paraguay is crossed by this tropic, the country experiences both tropical and subtropical climates.

Fact #4. Paraguay is one of the world's largest producers of soybeans

The economy of Paraguay is highly dependent on agricultural products. This sector of the economy accounts for 30% of the country's GDP. With an annual rate of over 10 million tons, Paraguay is known as one of the world's largest producers of soybeans.

Fact #5. A spider web is a traditional Paraguayan lace

Here goes the next one of fun facts about Paraguay. Nanduti is a traditional Paraguayan lace made of fabric tightly stretched in a circular frame. The name of the lace is derived from the official indigenous Guarani language and means 'Spider web'.

Fact #6. The national Paraguayan bird has one of the loudest bird calls known

The bare-throated bellbird is a species of bird famous for their shrill cry. These species have white plumage, live in tropical forests, feed with fruit and have one of the loudest bird calls known. In 2004 this bird was forever declared the national bird of Paraguay.

Fact #7. There is a town of national pride in Paraguay

Here's another one of interesting facts about Paraguay. Humaita is a town on the Paraguay river that served as the country's main stronghold and home of the government from 1866 until 1868. Today, Humaita is considered a great symbol of national pride.

Fact #8. The traditional Paraguayan soup is often served with a soup

Sopa paraguaya is a traditional Paraguayan dish often made of corn flour, water, cheese, eggs and milk. Literally meaning 'Paraguayan soup' its consistency resembles more a cheese souffle than a savory cornbread. The dish is often served with beef soup.

Fact #9. Paraguay is one of the world's leading exporters of electrical power

The next one of fun facts about Paraguay regards electricity. The country relies mostly on hydroelectric power. With more than 70 TWh produced and 64 TWh exported, the country is known as one of the world's leading exporters of electrical power.

Fact #10. Paraguay is home to the South America's largest wild mammal

The guanaco is a camelid, native to South America that often lives at high altitudes, up to 4,000 m (13,123 ft) above sea level. Guanacos are 1,3 m (4,3 ft) tall, 2,2 m (7,3 ft) long, weigh up to 140 kg (310 lb) and inhabit mountainous regions of Paraguay.
