Interesting facts about Sudan

Interesting facts about Sudan presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. The Republic of the Sudan is a state in East Africa. In the northeast it is washed by the waters of the Red Sea. The capital is the city of Khartoum. Before the division into Sudan and South Sudan, it was the largest African country in terms of territory, now it ranks third after the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Algeria. The country is also famous for its pyramids, national bird, the oldest football league in Africa and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about the Sudan.

Fact #1. Sudan is the third largest country in Africa

Here goes the first one of fun facts about Sudan. The Republic of the Sudan has a population of 43 million and occupies an area of 1,886,068 sq km (728,215 sq mi), making it Africa's third largest country and also the third largest in the Arab league.

Fact #2. There is the throne hall in Sudan that once was a mosque

The Throne Hall of Dongola is an archaeological site in Old Dongola, Sudan. It's a two storey brick building, originally built in the 9th century as a Makurian kings' residence. In 1317 it was converted into a mosque. In 1969 it became a historic monument.

Fact #3. The name of Sudan is the name of the geographical region

Another one of interesting facts about Sudan regards the name of the country. The country's name is a name given to a geographical region to the south of the Sahara. The name is derived from the Arabic "bilad as-sudan" which means "Land of the Blacks".

Fact #4. The national bird of Sudan is a secretary

The secretary bird is a large eagle-bodied bird endemic to Africa. Its height is up to 1,3 m (4,3 ft), it lives in grasslands, nests at the tops of thorny trees and feeds with insects. The bird is featured on the emblem of Sudan, and both on the presidential flag and seal.

Fact #5. Sudan has twice as many pyramids as Egypt

The next one of fun facts about Egypt regards pyramids. Deeply influenced by the Egyptians, Nubian kings built their own pyramids. There are twice as many Nubian pyramids as there are Egyptian ones. All of them are inscribed in the UNESCO World Heritage List.

Fact #6. The oldest football league in Africa originated in Sudan

The most watched and played sport in Sudan is football. The country was one of the four African nations which formed African football. The nation's capital is home to the Khartoum League, which is considered to be the oldest football league in Africa.

Fact #7. One of the largest irrigation projects in the world is located in Sudan

Another one of interesting facts about Sudan regards the Gezira Scheme. It's one of the largest irrigation projects in the world covering an area of 8,800 sq km (sq mi). The network of canals is 4,300 km (2,700 mi) long and the main crop grown here is cotton.

Fact #8. Sudan is a Muslim country with Christian churches

Despite the fact that more than 97% of the population of Sudan adheres to Islam and places of worship are predominantly Muslim mosques, there are also Christian churches, including the iconic Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Khartoum but not only.

Fact #9. There is a 1 billion years old landmark in Sudan

Here's the next one of fun facts about Sudan. Itbay is a region in northeastern Sudan famous for its Red Sea Hills composed of the volcano-sedimentary rock rising about 3,000 m (9,800 ft) tall. What's curious is that these hills are almost 1 billion years old.

Fact #10. The most important element of the economy of Sudan is cotton

Cotton is the principal export crop and an integral part of the economy of Sudan. Cash crops that include cotton and cottonseed are of primary importance to the country with more than 172,000 tons and 131,000 tons produced annually respectively.
