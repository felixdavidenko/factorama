Interesting facts about Honduras

Interesting facts about Honduras presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, culture, economy, science, nature, sports and of course people. Honduras is a country in Central America, washed by the Caribbean Sea to the north and the Pacific Ocean to the south. In the rainforests near Guatemala, Copan is located – the center of one of the ancient Mayan states, which is known for its hieroglyphic signs and high steles. Popular among diving enthusiasts are the islands of Islas de la Bahia in the Caribbean Sea, which are part of the 1,000 km long Mesoamerican Barrier Reef. The country is also famous for its endemic animals, coral fortress, the world's oldest clock and not only. So, get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Honduras.

Fact #1. Honduras is the second largest country in Central America

Here’s the first one of interesting facts about Honduras. The Republic of Honduras is the second largest country in Central America. It has a total area of 112,890 sq km (43,590 sq mi) and borders with Nicaragua, El Salvador and Guatemala.

Fact #2. One of the greatest Mayan sites is located in Honduras

Copan is an archaeological site located in the western Honduras some 600 m (2,400 ft) above sea level. This site is considered one of the most significant examples of the Maya civilization of the period from the 5th to 9th centuries AD.

Fact #3. Holidays in Honduras are celebrated with sawdust carpets

The next one of fun facts about Honduras regards cultural traditions. Some of the national holidays in Honduras are celebrated with colorful sawdust carpets. This tradition originated in Europe and was brought to the Americas by Spaniards.

Fact #4. Honduras is divided into three geographical regions

Honduras has three different geographical regions: wide highland area and two lowland areas along the country's coasts. Inner part of the country is mountainous for almost 80%. The Caribbean and the Pacific lowlands are flat landforms.

Fact #5. Waters around Honduras are full of the world's largest sharks

Another one of interesting facts about Honduras regards sharks. Waters surrounding the country are a natural habitat for slow, filter-feeding whale sharks known as the world’s largest. The largest found one was 18,8 m (62 ft) in length.

Fact #6. There is a popular drink in Honduras made of cereals and seeds

Agua fresca, also known as "fresh water" is a non-alcoholic drink made of one or several fruits, cereals or seeds mixed with water and sugar. Aguas frescas are very popular in Honduras and are sold by street vendors, juice bars and restaurants.

Fact #7. The highest mountain in Honduras has its own park

Here goes another one of fun facts about Honduras. Cerro Las Minas, located in the Lempira Department, is the country's highest mountain, standing 2,849 m (9,420) tall. It's located in the Celaque National Park, created for the mount in 1987.

Fact #8. The Ara macao is the national bird of Honduras

The Ara macao, also known as the Scarlet macaw is a large parrot, native to humid evergreen forests of Central America. This 80 cm (32 in) long, red, yellow and blue colored bird weighs about 1 kg (2,2 lb). It’s the national bird of Honduras.

Fact #9. Honduras is home to the 18th century coral fortress

Here's the next one of fun facts about Honduras. The Fortaleza de San Fernando is a Spanish fortress located in Omoa. Being built between 1756 and 1775 of brick and coral it withstood several attacks and sieges but remained almost intact.

Fact #10. One of the most popular dishes in Honduras is fried Yojoa fish

Fried Yojoa fish is a Honduran dish popular and served all around the country. This fish has a distinctive savory flavor compared to other fish dishes. It's often salted, spiced, deep fried and served with pickled cabbage and fried plantains.
