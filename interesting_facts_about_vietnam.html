Interesting facts about Vietnam

Interesting facts about Vietnam presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. Vietnam is a country in Southeast Asia, located on the coast of the South China Sea. It is known for its beaches, rivers, Buddhist pagodas and vibrant cities. In the capital, Hanoi, there is a huge marble mausoleum of the famous leader of the country's Communist Party, Ho Chi Minh. Ho Chi Minh City, formerly called Saigon, is home to many French colonial-style buildings as well as museums dedicated to the history of the Vietnam War. The country is also famous for the roof of Indochina, water puppetry, snake wine, turtle tower and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Vietnam.

Fact #1. There is a city with the city inside the city in Vietnam

The Imperial City is an enclosure protected by ramparts 2 by 2 km (1,2 by 1,2 mi) within the citadel of the city of Hue. Within the Imperial City there is the Purple Forbidden City. Access to the innermost enclosure is restricted by the imperial family.

Fact #2. One of the most popular tourist sites in Vietnam is a peaceful meeting place

Hoi An is a city in Vietnam's Quang Nam Province. Old Town, the city's historic district is recognized as an exceptionally well-preserved example of a Southeast Asian trading port dating from the 15th to the 19th century. Hoi An translates as "peaceful meeting place".

Fact #3. There are 7 climatic regions in Vietnam

The next one of fun facts about Vietnam regards its climate. There are as much as 7 climatic regions in Vietnam: Northwest, Northeast, North Delta (Red River Delta), North Central (North Central Coast), South Central, Central Highlands and the South.

Fact #4. Vietnam is the 5th biodiversity hotspot in the world

According to the Conservation International List, Vietnam is the 5th biodiversity hot spot in the world. The country is home to 889 species of birds, 850 species of land mollusks, 310 species of mammals, 296 reptile species and not only.

Fact #5. The roof of Indochina is located in Vietnam

The next one of fun facts about Vietnam regards Fansipan Mountain, located in the Lao Cai Province. The height of the mountain is 3,147 m (10,326 ft). It is the highest mountain in the Indochinese Peninsula, hence its nickname "The Roof of Indochina".

Fact #6. Water puppetry is an ancient element of national identity in Vietnam

Water puppetry is a tradition originated in the villages of the Red River Delta area of northern Vietnam that dates back as far as the 11th century. Modern Vietnamese water puppetry is a unique variation of the ancient Asian puppet tradition.

Fact #7. One of the most popular drinks in Vietnam is a snake wine

Ruou thuoc is a kind of Vietnamese distilled liquor with herbs and snakes. Ginseng and snake are placed into a large jar of alcohol and kept for days to let the substances dissolve in liquor. Distilled liquor must be strong enough with alcoholic content of 45%.

Fact #8. One of the major scenic spots in Vietnam is the Turtle Tower

Hoan Kiem Lake is a freshwater lake measuring some 12 h (29 acres) in the historical center of Hanoi. The islet in the center was first used as a fishing site and in the 1400s on it was erected a Turtle Tower. This site is one of the major scenic spots in the city.

Fact #9. Vietnam is home to 889 species of birds

The next one of fun facts about Vietnam regards birds. According to the BirdLife International records, the country is home to 889 species of birds, which includes 18 endemic species, 44 globally threatened species and 6 introduced species.

Fact #10. The largest solar plant project in South East Asia is located in Vietnam

Dau Tieng Solar Power Project in Tay Ninh Province utilizes 1,4 million photovoltaic modules, 220,000 pile foundations, 2,400 km (1,491 mi) of wires. With a total generating capacity of 600 MWp it's the largest solar plant project in South East Asia.
