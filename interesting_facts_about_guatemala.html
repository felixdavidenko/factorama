Interesting facts about Guatemala

Interesting facts about Guatemala presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Guatemala is a country in Central America south of Mexico famous for its volcanoes, rainforests and ancient Mayan cities. The capital of the same name is home to the majestic National Palace of Culture and the National Museum of Archeology and Ethnology. In the city of Antigua Guatemala to the west of the capital, Spanish buildings from the colonial period are preserved. The country is also famous for the deepest lake in Central America, the Pirate fort, the money-bird and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Guatemala for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. The most populous urban area in Central America is located in Guatemala

Here's the first one of fun facts about Guatemala. Guatemala City is the largest city and also the capital of the country. With a population of about 1 million people, the city of Guatemala is considered the most populous urban area in Central America.

Fact #2. There are more than 30 volcanoes in Guatemala

Guatemala is known to have a lot of volcanoes, the most noteworthy of which is Tajumulco. It's famous because it's the highest peak in Central America standing 4,202 m (13,786 ft) tall. Out of 30 volcanoes, only Fuego, Pacaya and Santiaguito are active.

Fact #3. Guatemala is one of the leading countries in Blue Denim production

Another one of interesting facts about Guatemala regards weaving. Today, textile weaving is still in demand in San Antonio Aguas Calientes municipality. Local women are crafty weavers and the country itself is one of the leading in Blue Denim production.

Fact #4. In Guatemala, the national bird and the national currency are called the same

The national bird of Guatemala is the Quetzal. It's a long-tailed bird that primarily lives in humid forests and rainforests. It's not an easy thing to find one, but locals see it every day. That's because the national currency in Guatemala is also called Quetzal.

Fact #5. Retired American school buses live their second lives in Guatemala

The next one of fun facts about Guatemala regards buses. When the mileage of an American yellow school bus reaches 240,000 km (150,000 mi) it is sold. Then, most often such a bus is driven down to Guatemala, where it’s used as a public transport.

Fact #6. Guatemala is home for one of the largest Mayan archaeological sites

Guatemala is home for Tikal. This is the ruin of an ancient Mayan city, also known as Yax Mutal. It's located in northern part of the country in the archeological region of the Peten Basin. Tikal is part of the National Park and the UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Fact #7. Guatemala is one of the leading countries in chocolate production

Another one of interesting facts about Guatemala regards chocolate. Mayan people worshiped the cacao tree and considered chocolate "the Food of Gods". Guatemala has strong chocolate making traditions and is one of the leading countries in its production.

Fact #8. The hearty stew with chicken meat is the national dish in Guatemala

Chicken Pepian is considered to be the national dish in Guatemala. This dish is present in almost every restaurant menu. It’s a hearty stew with chicken meat and vegs (potato, tomato, onion). The meat is often slow-cooked and the sauce is always spicy.

Fact #9. In Guatemala, traditional clothing is worn along with modern

The next one of fun facts about Guatemala regards clothing. Among the locals there are a lot of Mayan descendants. Since they are very proud of their heritage, they often wear traditional clothing which on the streets can be seen along with modern.

Fact #10. The deepest lake in Central America is located in Guatemala

Lake Atitlan is located in the Guatemalan Highlands of the Sierra Madre mountain range. This is a crater lake, formed from explosive activity during volcanic eruption. The depth of the lake is 340 m (1,120 ft), what makes it the deepest in Central America.
