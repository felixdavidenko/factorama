Interesting facts about Ethiopia

Interesting facts about Ethiopia presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Ethiopia is a landlocked mountainous country in the northeast of the African continent. Archaeological artifacts, which are more than three million years old, have been found on its territory. Ethiopia is home to the world-famous city of Lalibela with its 12th and 13th-century Christian churches carved inside the rocks. The country has three seasons instead of four and uses a 12-hour instead of 24-hour clock system. The country is also famous for its hydrothermal springs, world heritage sites, endemic animals, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Ethiopia for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Ethiopia was once a communist country

The first one of interesting facts about Ethiopia regards communism. Ethiopia was once a People's Democratic Republic. Established in 1987 as a Marxist-Leninist one-party state, it existed till 1991, when the regime fell having no support from the USSR.

Fact #2. Walia ibex is a wild goat endemic only to Ethiopia

Ethiopia is a natural habitat for plenty of endangered species of animals, including the Walia ibex. It's a wild species of ibex endemic only to this country. It is estimated that there are only about 500 individuals that inhabit the Semien Mountains.

Fact #3. Ethiopia is one of the most populated countries in Africa

Another one of interesting facts about Ethiopia regards its population. The total population of the country is over 109 million people. This makes Ethiopia the second most populous country in Africa (after Nigeria) and the 12th most populous in the world.

Fact #4. Ethiopia is home to the 17th century UNESCO fortress-city

The Royal Enclosure, also known as Fasil Ghebbi is the remains of an Emperor's fortress-city located in Gondar, Ethiopia. The city was founded in the 17th century and covers an area of 70 sq km (27 sq mi). It's been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1979.

Fact #5. In Ethiopia, there is a 12-hour instead of a 24-hour clock system

The next one of fun facts about Ethiopia regards time. The clock system in Ethiopia is different from most other countries. To adjust the clock to the local Addis Ababa time, one must add (or subtract) 6 hours, because here 02:00 is called "8 at night".

Fact #6. Ethiopia uses one of the world's oldest alphabets

Ethiopia uses the ancient Ge'ez script. This is one of the world's oldest alphabets, adapted to write other languages, mostly Semitic, such as Amharic, Tigrinya, Harari, etc. It originated in the 9th century BCE and up to date is still in use in the world.

Fact #7. Ethiopia is home to the hydrothermal springs of Dallol

Here’s the next one of interesting facts about Ethiopia. Dallol is a locality in northern Ethiopia, famous for its vivid hydrothermal springs discharging anoxic and hyper-acidic brines. That's why Dallol is used as a site for studies investigating the limits of life.

Fact #8. Ethiopia is known all over the world for its successes in athletics

The most successful sport in Ethiopia is athletics. The New York Times once called this country a "Running Mecca" because of numerous successes in athletics. Ethiopia is home to the Great Ethiopian Run and several Olympic Gold medalists.

Fact #9. The Ethiopian calendar is neither Gregorian nor Julian

Here’s another one of fun facts about Ethiopia. The local calendar here has 12 months of 30 days plus it adds a leap day every 4 years. The start of the New Year falls on 29 or 30 August. Also, this calendar has a gap of 7-8 years compared to the Gregorian.

Fact #10. There are over 90 different languages in Ethiopia

According to the statistical data, there are over 90 different languages in Ethiopia. Despite the fact that Amharic has the official status of working language, it's spoken only by 29% of people. The most prevalent language is Oromo, spoken by 33% of people.
