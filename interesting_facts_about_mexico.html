Interesting facts about Mexico

Interesting facts about Mexico presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, culture, economy, science, nature, sports, and of course people. Mexico is a state located between the United States and several small Central American states. It is famous for its beaches, the Gulf of Mexico, and for its varied landscape. Mountainous terrain, volcanoes, and rainforests meet here. The country is also famous for the world’s smallest volcano, sombrero hats, Mayan sites, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Mexico for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. The official name of Mexico is the same as the official name of America

The first one of interesting facts about Mexico regards its name. The official name of the country is the United States of Mexico. Just like the United States of America, Mexico is also divided into states. The country consists of 31 states, each having its own legislation.

Fact #2. Mexico has the world's largest population of Spanish speakers

Mexico has 68 official linguistic groups and 364 varieties of local indigenous languages. Spanish is the official language in Mexico. Since it's spoken by more than 121 million people, this country has the largest population of Spanish speakers in the world.

Fact #3. There are more U.S. citizens in Mexico than Mexican citizens in the U.S.

Another one of fun facts about Mexico is that there are more U.S. citizens living in Mexico than Mexican citizens living in the U.S. According to the population census, there are more than 750,000 U.S. citizens living here and this figure is growing constantly.

Fact #4. Mexico is home to more than 30 UNESCO World Heritage Sites

There are 34 UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Mexico to be precise. 27 Cultural sites, 6 natural and 2 mixed. This fact makes Mexico one of the leading countries by the number of world heritage sites, and because of that, one of the most attractive for tourists.

Fact #5. Public transport in Mexico has women-only buses and sections

Another one interesting fact about Mexico regards public transport, which has some privileges for women. For instance, there are women-only buses in Mexico City and a special section on a subway. However, women prefer to use public transport along with men.

Fact #6. The Mexican Dia de los Muertos is a celebration of life, not death

Mesoamericans that were living on the territory of modern Mexico believed that death was not an end of life, but a part of it. They considered death as a transfer to the next stage of life, so Dia de los Muertos (Day of the Dead) is a celebration of those, who went on to the next stage.

Fact #7. The world’s smallest volcano in Mexico in fact is not a volcano

Here goes the next one of interesting facts about Mexico. The city of Puebla is home to the world's smallest volcano Cuexcomate. Its height is only 13 m (43 ft), and its depth is 17 m (56 ft). Despite being known as a volcano, in fact, it's literally an inactive geyser.

Fact #8. Mexico is home to the largest native cat species of the New World

The jaguar is the only extant cat species of the Panthera genus endemic to the Americas. It's the largest native cat species of the New World and the 3rd largest in the world. According to the IUCN Red List, this spotted predator cat is listed as Near Threatened.

Fact #9. The name of the Yucatan peninsula was a result of a misunderstanding

Another one of fun facts about Mexico regards the Yucatan peninsula. When the Spanish conquistadors first discovered this land, they asked Mayans "What is the name of this peninsula?" one of them said "Ma'anaatik ka t'ann" - "I don't understand you".

Fact #10. The "tequila fields" in Mexico is a UNESCO World Heritage Site

Mexican tequila is well known all around the world. This alcoholic drink is made of blue agave, which is used for such purposes since the 16th century. Today, this plant is part of national identity and the Agave Landscape in Jalisco state is a World Heritage Site.
