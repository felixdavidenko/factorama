Interesting facts about Antarctica

Interesting facts about Antarctica presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the continent, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Antarctica is a continent located in the very south of the Earth. The center of Antarctica roughly coincides with the geographic South Pole. It is washed by the Atlantic, Indian and Pacific oceans. Antarctica has the lowest relative air humidity, the strongest wind, and the most intense solar radiation. The continent is also known for one of the world's largest mountain ranges, the world's largest subglacial lake, the world's best conditions for astronomical observations, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Antarctica for children, adults, the elderly, and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Antarctica is larger than entire Europe

Here's the first one of fun facts about Antarctica. This continent has a total area of 14,200,000 sq km (5,500,000 sq mi). By this indicator, it is larger than Europe - 10,180,000 sq km (3,930,000 sq mi) and lesser than South America - 17,840,000 sq km (6,890,000 sq mi).

Fact #2. Antarctica holds the record for the lowest measured temperature on earth

Antarctica is the coldest, driest, and windiest of all continents. It is mainly a polar desert, covered with ice sheets, ice fields, and ice caps. This continent holds the record for the lowest measured temperature on earth of -89,2 deg C (-128,6 deg F), registered on 21 July 1983.

Fact #3. The largest subglacial lake in the world is located in Antarctica

The next one of interesting facts about Antarctica regards lake Vostok, located under the central East Antarctic Ice Sheet. Its surface is some 4,000 m (13,100 ft) below sea level. It has an average depth of 432 m (1,417 ft), and measures 250 km (160 mi) long by 50 km (30 mi) wide.

Fact #4. Antarctica is governed by almost 30 countries

While many consider Antarctica as no man's land, in fact, it is governed by almost 30 countries, participants of the 1959 Antarctic Treaty. Seven countries have made territorial claims here and other countries have their research stations within the areas claimed by the others.

Fact #5. The ice sheet of Antarctica weighs approximately 26 gigatons

The next one of fun facts about Antarctica regards its ice sheet. It contains 26,5 million cub km (16,4 million cub mi) of ice. A cub km of ice weighs approximately one metric gigaton, meaning that the ice sheet weighs 26,500,000 gigatons. To clarify, 1 gigaton equals 1 billion tons.

Fact #6. Antarctica is the world's best point for astronomical observations

High elevation above sea level, long polar nights, and lack of water vapor make Antarctica the world's best astronomical observation point. The Amundsen-Scott station is home to the IceCube Neutrino Observatory which is the largest neutrino detector in the world.

Fact #7. Antarctica is home to one of the longest mountain ranges in the world

The next one of interesting facts about Antarctica regards the Transantarctic Mountain Range. With a total length of about 3,500 km (2,174 mi), it's one of the longest ranges in the world. The 100-300 km (62-186 mi) wide range divides the continent into West and East Antarctica.

Fact #8. Antarctica is considered the world's best environment for the study of meteorites

The Antarctic dry polar desert makes meteorites easy to find as the dark stones stand out in the white snow. Thanks to such a unique environment, meteorites older than a million years have been found here. Some of them have been proven to come from Moon and Mars.

Fact #9. The population of Antarctica varies depending on the season

Another one of fun facts about Antarctica regards its population. During the summer months, a total amount of about 5,000 people reside at numerous research bases, stations, and facilities. During the winter season, that figure drops to around 1,000 people.

Fact #10. Antarctica is visited by over 70,000 tourists annually

Antarctica has been open to tourists since 1957. Sea cruises last up to 3 weeks and cost from $6,000. Scenic flights last up to 14 hours and cost from $1,000. Almost 75,000 tourists visited Antarctica in 2020 to enjoy camping, hiking, skiing, mountaineering, and not only.
