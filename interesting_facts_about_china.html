Interesting facts about China

Interesting facts about China presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports, and of course people. China is a densely populated country in East Asia with diverse landscapes and reliefs. Here you can find meadows, deserts, mountains, lakes, rivers, and coasts with a length of more than 14 thousand kilometers. The capital of the country, Beijing, is famous for its modern architecture and ancient monuments, including the Forbidden City palace complex and Tiananmen Square. To the east is the world financial center Shanghai, whose silhouette is shaped by countless skyscrapers. The country is famous for the Great Wall, the Everest Mountain, the Maglev Train, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about China for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. China is the world’s most populated country

The first one of fun facts about China regards its population. China is known to have the largest number of citizens in the world. It’s counted that the population of China exceeds 1,41 billion people. The second closest competitor is India with its 1,40 billion people.

Fact #2. China is the world’s fastest growing country

The Chinese construction industry is the largest in the world. There is no country on Earth to grow as fast as China. They build 120 bridges simultaneously, a new skyscraper every 5 days, plus, they hold a record for building a 57-story building in 19 days.

Fact #3. There are several hundred languages in China

Another one of interesting facts about China regards its languages. The official language in China is Standard Chinese, based on central Mandarin. But outside the borders of Standard Chinese, there are hundreds of other Chinese languages and dialects.

Fact #4. Table tennis is the national sport in China

Table tennis, also known as Ping Pong is the national sport in China. It's the most popular recreational sport in the country with about 300 mil players in total. At the 2008 Beijing, 2012 London, and 2016 Rio Olympic Games, China won all possible gold medals.

Fact #5. China is known to be the homeland of tea

The next one of fun facts about China regards tea. China is known to have the oldest tea traditions and the best tea in the world. In southwest China, there are still can be found wild tea trees from over 3,000 years ago and plantations from over 800 years ago.

Fact #6. China is home to the world’s greatest wall

The Great Wall of China is one of the most famous and recognizable Chinese landmarks. Also, it's the longest wall ever built by humans. What's curious about this wall is that its total length of 21,196,18 km (13,170,70 mi) is equal to half the length of the Equator.

Fact #7. The world-famous Chinese lanterns are over 2,000 years old

Here’s another one of interesting facts about China. It’s believed that Chinese lanterns were invented during the Eastern Han Dynasty as a way to pay respect to Buddha. Emperor Han Mingdi ordered to lit lanterns every fifteenth day of the first lunar month.

Fact #8. Panda is the national animal of China

The Giant Panda is considered to be the national animal of China. Fossils of this animal that were found across the country are between 1 and 2 million years old. Today, the natural habitat of pandas is only a wild nature in remote foothills of China's Tibet.

Fact #9. Dragon is the mythical animal of China

The next one of fun facts about China regards dragon, also known as Lung or Long. This creature is popular in Chinese mythology, folklore, culture, architecture, art, and not only. The dragon in East Asian culture symbolizes power, strength, and good luck.

Fact #10. China is one of the few communist countries in the world

Today, there are only four countries in the world that are ruled by the principles of communism. These are China, Cuba, Laos, and Vietnam. China is the world's only communist country that can surpass capitalist America in terms of economic growth.
