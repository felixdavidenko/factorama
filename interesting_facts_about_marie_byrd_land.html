Interesting facts about Marie Byrd Land

Interesting facts about Marie Byrd Land presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the land, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science and of course people. Marie Byrd Land is part of West Antarctica, lying on the Pacific coast of the Southern Ocean between the Ross Ice Shelf in the east and Ellsworth Land in the west. The land is famous for its mountain ranges named after the motor company, lichens used in astrobiology experiments, research station served as a template to the horror movie and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Marie Byrd Land.

Fact #1. Marie Byrd Land was named after the wife of its explorer

Here's the first one of fun facts about Marie Byrd Land. This land is the portion of West Antarctica lying east of the Ross Sea and the Ross Ice Shelf. The name was initially applied by the explorer of this area, American naval officer Richard Byrd in honor of his wife.

Fact #2. Marie Byrd Land is the largest single unclaimed territory on Earth

Because of its remoteness, most of Marie Byrd Land has not been claimed by any sovereign state. With an area of 1,610,000 sq km (620,000 sq mi, including Eights Coast, east of Marie Byrd Land) it's by far the largest single unclaimed territory on Earth.

Fact #3. There are mountain ranges in Marie Byrd Land named after the motor company

The next one of interesting facts about Marie Byrd Land regards the Ford Ranges. These are mountain ranges, discovered by the Byrd Antarctic Expedition in 1929, and named for Edsel Ford of the Ford Motor Company, who financed the expedition.

Fact #4. Marie Byrd Land is mentioned in the fantasy novel about magicians

Marie Byrd Land is the location of the southern outpost of Brakebill's Academy in Lev Grossman's 2009 fantasy novel "The Magicians''. The Magicians won the 2010 Alex Award and its author won the 2011 John W. Campbell Award for Best New Writer.

Fact #5. The lowest point on any of the Earth's continental plates is located in Marie Byrd Land

The next one of fun facts about Marie Byrd Land regards Byrd Subglacial Basin, which is a major subglacial basin of West Antarctica. With a maximum depth of 2,870 m (9,416 ft) below sea level, it's the lowest point on any of the Earth's continental plates.

Fact #6. Marie Byrd Land is home to over 50,000 pairs of Adelie penguins

There are 6 Adelie penguin colonies in the coastal regions of Mary Bird Land. The size of each colony ranges from a couple of hundred breeding pairs to over 40,000. The total breeding population in this ecoregion is estimated to be something over 50,000 pairs.

Fact #7. The Byrd Station was the template for "The Thing" horror movie

Another one of interesting facts about Marie Byrd Land regards the Byrd Station. It's a former research station, established in 1957 and evacuated in 2005. It was the template for the Antarctic base in the horror movie John Carpenter's "The Thing" (1982).

Fact #8. Marie Byrd Land is home to lichens used in astrobiology experiments

According to the most comprehensive studies conducted in 1989, Marie Byrd Land is home to 6 species of moss and 23 species of lichen. One of the lichens, Buellia frigida, is often used in astrobiology experiments that simulate space conditions.

Fact #9. The first ice core to fully penetrate the Antarctic Ice Sheet was drilled in Marie Byrd Land

Here's the next one of fun facts about Marie Byrd Land. In 1968, the first ice core to fully penetrate the Antarctic Ice Sheet was drilled here. The deep coring in 2007 reached a depth of 3,405 m (11,171 ft). The ice at the bottom of the hole fell as snow 67,748 years ago.

Fact #10. Marie Byrd Land is home to 18 volcanoes and 30 volcanic centers

The Marie Byrd Land Volcanic Province is a volcanic field, consisting of over 18 large shield volcanoes and 30 small volcanic centers. Volcanism here commenced at least 36 mil years ago with the latest eruption having possibly taken place 2,300 years ago.
