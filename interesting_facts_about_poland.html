Interesting facts about Poland

Interesting facts about Poland presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. Poland is a country in Eastern Europe on the shores of the Baltic Sea, known for its medieval architecture. The city of Krakow is famous for the 14th century Royal Castle on the Wawel Hill, which offers a magnificent view of the medieval old town, where the Cloth Hall, a Renaissance trading building on the Main Market Square, is located. Not far from the city there is the Auschwitz-Birkenau Museum, opened on the site of the Nazi concentration camp Auschwitz, and the Wieliczka Salt Mine, which is a chain of corridors and chambers. The country is also famous for the Dragon's Den, the Bialowieza Forest, the city of dwarfs and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Poland.

Fact #1. The largest castle in the world is located in Poland

Here's the first one of fun facts about Poland. The Castle of the Teutonic Order, located in the town of Malbork, is a 13th century fortress, built by the Teutonic crusader-knights. By land area, the Castle of the Teutonic Order is the largest castle in the world.

Fact #2. There is a tourist attraction in Poland, located 327 m deep

The Wieliczka Salt Mine, located in the town of Wieliczka, Poland, is one of the most popular attractions in the country. The mine is located some 327 m (1072 ft) deep, and consists of numerous chambers and passages of a total length of 287 km (178 mi).

Fact #3. There are 16 UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Poland

Here goes another one of interesting facts about Poland. As of 2020, the country has 16 UNESCO World Heritage Sites and 6 more on the tentative list. Of those 16, one is shared with Belarus, one is shared with Ukraine and one is shared with Germany.

Fact #4. The world's 3rd largest zoological garden is located in Poland

The Wroclaw Zoological Garden is the oldest and the largest zoo in Poland. It was opened in 1865, covers an area of 33 h (82 acres) and is home to 10,500 animals of 1,130 species. In terms of the number of species this zoo is the third largest in the world.

Fact #5. There is a UNESCO forest in Poland with individually named oaks

Here's the next one of fun facts about Poland. Bialowieza Forest is one of the largest remaining parts of the primeval European forests. It's a UNESCO World Heritage Site. What's curious is that it has ancient oaks, at least 10 of which have their own names.

Fact #6. Poland has its own Pompeii

Biskupin is an 8th century fortified settlement, located in central Poland. This is an archaeological site and a museum which is part of national consciousness and the symbol of achievements of the Slavic predecessors, also known as "The Polish Pompeii".

Fact #7. One quarter of the global population of storks live in Poland

Another one of interesting facts about Poland regards storks. The country is known to have the largest stork population in Europe. It's known that almost one quarter of the global population of these birds live and nest in the Polish wetlands.

Fact #8. There is a transboundary UNESCO biosphere reserve in Poland

The Tatra Mountains is a mountain range that parts Poland from Slovakia. Both countries have their own Tatra National Parks and in 1992, both parks on both sides of the border are jointly inscribed in the UNESCO's World Network of Biosphere Reserves.

Fact #9. One of the most popular tourist attractions in Poland is the Dragon's Den

Here's the next one of fun facts about Poland. Smocza Jama is a 276 m (905 ft) long limestone cave, located in Krakow. The name of the cave was first given in 1551 and is derived from the 12th century legend about the Wawel Dragon by Wincenty Kadlubek.

Fact 10. There is a traditional Polish appetizer made of salted sheep milk

Oscypek, is a traditional Polish holiday appetizer. It's a smoked cheese often made of salted sheep milk. Oscypek needs to be made of at least 60% milk of the sheep, must weigh 60 to 80 g (2,1 to 2,8 oz) and have a length of 17 to 23 cm (6,7 to 9 in).
