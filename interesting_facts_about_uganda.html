Interesting facts about Uganda

Interesting facts about Uganda presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. Uganda is a landlocked country in East Africa. The snow-capped Rwenzori Mountains and the large freshwater Lake Victoria are located here. The fauna of Uganda is rich and varied. Here one can meet chimpanzees, as well as see rare species of birds. The famous Bwindi National Park which is home to mountain gorillas can only be reached on foot. The country is also famous for its national bird, UNESCO's tombs, solar parks and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Uganda.

Fact #1. Uganda is named after the largest kingdom in East Africa

Here's the first one of fun facts about Uganda. The country is named after the Buganda kingdom which is the largest of the traditional kingdoms in present-day East Africa. It consists of Uganda's Central Region, including the Ugandan capital Kampala.

Fact #2. The grey crowned crane is the national bird of Uganda

The grey crowned crane is a bird native to eastern and southern Africa. The bird is an omnivore, it occurs in savannah and feeds on aquatic animals. It's 1 m (3,3 ft) tall, weighs 3,5 kg (7,7 lbs) and has a wingspan of 2 m (6,5 ft). This bird is the national bird of Uganda.

Fact #3. Uganda is a landlocked country washed by three lakes

Another one of interesting facts about Uganda regards lakes. Despite being the landlocked country, much of the border of Uganda is lakeshore. Located in the heart of the Great Lakes region, it's surrounded by Lake Edward, Lake Albert and Lake Victoria.

Fact #4. The highest ranked university in sub-Saharan Africa is located in Uganda

Makerere University, located in the city of Kampala is the largest and oldest institution of higher learning in Uganda. In the 2020 U.S. News & World Report ranking, Makerere is the highest ranked university in sub-Saharan Africa outside of South Africa.

Fact #5. Uganda is a popular destination for hikers and climbers

Here's the next one of fun facts about Uganda. The country has many opportunities for hiking and climbing enthusiasts. Among the most visited spots are the Rwenzori Mountains, Mount Gahinga, Mount Sabyinyo, Mount Muhavura, Mount Elgon and not only.

Fact #6. Uganda has its own business innovation tech-hub

There is an innovation hub in Uganda named Hive Colab and launched in 2010 by AfriLabs. It's a collaborative space for tech entrepreneurs, investors, venture capitalists and business angels. It's also an incubation platform intended to assist business activity.

Fact #7. There are 43 languages in Uganda

Another one of interesting facts about Uganda regards its numerous languages. There are as much as 43 languages in active use in Uganda. Of these, 41 are indigenous and 2 are non-indigenous. English and Swahili are the country's official languages.

Fact #8. Football is the national sport in Uganda

The most watched and the most played sport in Uganda is football. The country's national football team, nicknamed "The Cranes" have never qualified for the FIFA World Cup Finals. Their best finish in the African Cup of Nations was second place in 1978.

Fact #9. Uganda has one of the highest diversity of primates in Africa

The next one of fun facts about Uganda regards Kibale National Park. Located near the city of Fort Portal, it has one of the highest diversity of primates in Africa. It's home to a large number of endangered species including the red colobus monkey.

Fact #10. Uganda has its own national dress of men

A kanzu is a white or cream colored ankle length garment robe worn by men in the African Great Lakes region. In Uganda, the kanzu is the national dress of men. Kanzu is a Ganda word of Swahili origin, which literally translates as "robe" or "tunic".
