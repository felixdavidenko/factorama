Interesting facts about Ukraine

Interesting facts about Ukraine presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science and of course people. Ukraine is a large country in Eastern Europe, famous for its Orthodox churches, Black Sea resorts and forested mountains. Kyiv, located in the north, is a financial, cultural and official capital of the country. Eastern Ukraine is a major educational, scientific and industrial region. Western Ukraine is famous for its mountains, beloved by winter pastime enthusiasts. Southern Ukraine is famous for its numerous sea resorts, beloved by summer pastime enthusiasts. The country is also famous for the world’s deepest subway station, the world-famous chicken Kyiv, the booming IT sector and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Ukraine for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. There are over 1,300 monuments to Taras Shevchenko all around the world

The first one of interesting facts about Ukraine regards Taras Shevchenko. He was a Ukrainian poet who fought for the idea of his country's independence. His impact is still so great that today, there are over 1,300 monuments to him all around the world.

Fact #2. Askania-Nova is one of the world's oldest biosphere reserves

Askania-Nova is a biosphere reserve located in the Kherson region, Ukraine. It's one of the world's oldest reserves, being established in 1898. It consists of a zoo, a botanical garden and an open virgin steppes inhabited with numerous rare animal species.

Fact #3. The second-largest city of Ukraine is also known as The First Capital

Another one fun fact about Ukraine regards Kharkiv. Founded in 1654 as a small fortress it grew up to a second-largest city of the country. It was the first capital of Ukraine from December 1919 to January 1934. After, the country’s capital relocated to Kyiv.

Fact #4. Borsch is the national and the most popular dish in Ukraine

Borsch is a Ukrainian sour soup made of beetroots as one of the main ingredients that give the dish its red color. The typical recipe is simple and includes meat (beef, pork, chicken) and vegs (cabbage, potato, tomato). The dish can be served either hot or cold.

Fact #5. The Ukrainian carol Schedryk is sung all around the world

Another one of interesting facts about Ukraine regards "Shchedryk". It's a New Year's song that was arranged by composer Mykola Leontovych in 1916. In 1921 it was adapted by Peter Wilhousky and became known all around the world as the carol "Carol of the Bells".

Fact #6. Traditional Ukrainian Petrykivka painting is a UNESCO Cultural Heritage

There is a unique traditional painting style in Ukraine called Petrykivka. It's a folk painting style, used in decoration of household items with floral ornaments. In 2013 it was inscribed in the UNESCO's list of Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.

Fact #7. The largest ski resort in Eastern Europe is located in Ukraine

Here goes another one of interesting facts about Ukraine. "Bukovel" is the largest ski resort in Eastern Europe, located in western Ukraine. It has a huge skiable area of 220 ha (540 acres) with a total of 63 different runs, equipped with 16 lifts.

Fact #8. The traditional Ukrainian shirt has its own flash mob holiday

"Vyshyvanka" is a traditional national embroidered shirt. Vyshyvanka Day originated in 2006 and takes place annually on the third Thursday of May. On this day Ukrainians all over the world wear these shirts in order to promote their national culture.

Fact #9. There is a railway in Ukraine operated only by children

The next one of fun facts about Ukraine regards railway. A separate railway operated only by children is a phenomenon occurred in the USSR in early 30s as a form of education of future train drivers. Today, there are over 10 such railways in Ukraine.

Fact #10. There are more than 5,000 castles in Ukraine

According to historians, there are over 5,000 castles in Ukraine. Some of them are just remnants, but some are in a good state. The preserved ones are very popular among tourists, such as the Kamianets-Podilskyi Castle, located in western part of the country.
