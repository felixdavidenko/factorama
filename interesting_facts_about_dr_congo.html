Interesting facts about the DR Congo

Interesting facts about the DR Congo presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. The Democratic Republic of the Congo is a state in Central Africa with a population of about 101 million. In terms of the area occupied, it ranks second on the African continent. The official language is French. DR Congo is famous for its UNESCO World Heritage Sites, wildlife animals, mountains, rivers and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about the Democratic Republic of the Congo for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. DR Congo and Zaire in fact are the same country

The first one of fun facts about the DR Congo regards its names. The country is known as The Democratic Republic of the Congo. It's also often referred as the DROC, DR Congo, Congo-Kinshasa, and between 1971 and 1997 the country was known as Zaire.

Fact #2. DR Congo is home to the UNESCO World Heritage Park

Virunga National Park is located in the east of the DR Congo. Its flora contains 2,077 plant species of which 230 are endemic. Its fauna contains 706 bird, 196 mammal, 109 reptile and 65 amphibian species. It's a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1979.

Fact #3. DR Congo is the second-largest country on the African continent

Another one of interesting facts about the Democratic Republic of the Congo regards its territory. The total area of the country is 2,345,409 sq km (905,567 sq mi). By this number, the DR Congo is the 2nd largest in Africa and the 11th largest in the world.

Fact #4. Chicken stewed in palm butter is the national dish in the DR Congo

The national dish in the DR Congo is called "Poulet a la moambe", which means "Chicken in palm butter sauce". This dish is prepared by cooking spiced chicken in palm butter to a stewed state. The dish can be served with hard boiled eggs, potatoes or rice.

Fact #5. DR Congo is the most populous Francophone country in the world

Another one of interesting facts about the DR Congo regards its language. The official language of the country is French. Thus, with a population of over 101 million, DR Congo is the world's most populous Francophone country (outstripping France and Canada).

Fact #6. There are over 50 Patrice Lumumba streets all over the former USSR

Patrice Lumumba was a Congolese politician, known for his struggle for independence and friendship with the USSR. Almost every major city in the Soviet Union had a Patrice Lumumba Avenue. Today there are over 50 such avenues in the post soviet countries.

Fact #7. DR Congo lies on both sides of the Equator

Here goes the next one of fun facts about the DR Congo. The country is located in the middle of the African continent, right where it's crossed by the Equator. So the Equator also halves the DR Congo, making the country to be located on both sides of it.

Fact #8. Football is one of the most popular sports in the DR Congo

There are many equally popular sports in the DR Congo, including football, basketball, rugby, athletics and not only. But football is the most played and the most watched of them. The country has twice won the African Cup of Nations, in 1968 and 1974.

Fact #9. Most of the world's electronics uses tantalum mined in the DR Congo

Another one of interesting facts about the DR Congo regards electronics. The country has over 70% of the world's coltan reserves. The coltan serves as a main source of tantalum which in turn is used in manufacturing electronic components for computers.

Fact #10. DR Congo is home to the world's longest lake

Lake Tanganyika, located in central Africa is the second-oldest, the second-largest and the second-deepest in the world (after Lake Baikal in Russia). This lake is also the world's longest. Its length is 676 km (420 mi) and it has a shoreline of 1,828 km (1,136 mi).
