Interesting facts about Venezuela

Interesting facts about Venezuela presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Venezuela is a country on the north coast of South America known for its incredible natural beauty. There are many resort islands scattered along the Caribbean coast, including Margarita Island and the Los Roques archipelago. In the northwestern part of the country, the famous Andes rise, where the colonial city of Merida is located. The capital of Venezuela, the city of Caracas, is located in the north of the country. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Venezuela for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Christmas in Venezuela is celebrated on roller skates

Here’s the first one of fun facts about Venezuela. In Caracas, every year between 16 and 24 December roads are closed to traffic to let people roller skate to the Christmas mass in the morning. The police are clearing the streets to provide passage for worshippers.

Fact #2. There are over 26 spoken languages in Venezuela

In Venezuela, there are at least 26 languages and dialects used by local citizens. The official country’s language is Spanish. The Constitution of Venezuela dated 1999 declared Spanish and local indigenous languages as official (including Sign Language).

Fact #3. Venezuela is home of everlasting lightning storms

The next one of interesting facts about Venezuela regards a phenomenon, called "Catatumbo Lightning". The continuous storms are the result of collision of wind, heat and moisture that being collected create electrical charges within the clouds.

Fact #4. The Venezuelan Troupal is the country’s national bird

The Venezuelan Troupal, also known as Icterus icterus is the national bird of this country. This is a yellow-orange colored bird with black head, black tail and black'n'white wings. The Troupal's natural habitat are woods of northern and southern Orinoco.

Fact #5. Venezuela is a country that has a national tree

Another one of fun facts about Venezuela regards trees. This country is known to have its own national tree. The Araguaney tree was declared national as far as on 29 May 1945. It blossoms in the first months of the year and can reach 12 m (40 ft) tall.

Fact #6. Pabellon Criollo is considered a national dish in Venezuela

Pabellon Criollo is a traditional dish in Venezuela that is considered national. It's a plate of shredded beef in stew, black beans and white rice. The composition of the dish may vary. It can also be served with plantains, or avocado, or with a fried egg on top.

Fact #7. Venezuela inspired Arthur Conan Doyle to write "The Lost World"

Here’s the next one of interesting facts about Venezuela. It's known that Sir Arthur Conan Doyle wrote "The Lost World" after visiting Venezuela. It's also known that the book was written under the inspiration after visiting the famous Mount Roraima.

Fact #8. The Joropo is a national dance in Venezuela

The Joropo is a musical style accompanying with a dance. It originated in Venezuela and in 1882 it became a national dance. The Spanish word "Joropo" formerly meant a "Party". Today, the word is tightly associated with Venezuela's music and dance.

Fact #9. Venezuela has a tropical climate with only two seasons

Another one of fun facts about Venezuela regards seasons. Most of the country has a rainy season that lasts from May through November. What's curious is that this period is commonly known as winter and the rest of the year is considered summer.

Fact #10. Venezuela is a paradise for wildlife

Venezuela is one of only 17 countries in the world that are known to have "megadiverse" wildlife. It's scientifically proven that over 50% of amphibian species and over 25% of reptilian species that inhabit the country are considered endemic to Venezuela.
