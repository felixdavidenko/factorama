Interesting facts about Chilean Antarctic Territory

Interesting facts about Chilean Antarctic Territory presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the land, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science and of course people. Chilean Antarctic Territory (Spanish: Territorio Chileno Antartico) is a set of territorial and political claims of Chile in Antarctica. Administratively it represents the Antarctic commune (Spanish Antartica) of the Antarctic-Chilena province of the Magallanes-i-la-Antarctica-Chilena region. The territory is famous for having the only international airport in Antarctica, the permanently inhabited town, colonies of earless seals and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Chilean Antarctic Territory.

Fact #1. The Chilean Antarctic Territory is claimed by Chile

Here goes the first one of fun facts about the Chilean Antarctic Territory. This territory has an area of 1,250,257 sq km (482,727 sq mi), it is claimed by Chile and administered by the Cabo de Hornos municipality in the South American mainland.

Fact #2. The Chilean Antarctic Territory houses the research station that once was a navy base

Captain Arturo Prat Base is a Chilean Antarctic research station, named after a naval officer, Captain Arturo Prat. Opened in 1947 by the First Chilean Antarctic Expedition it's the oldest Chilean Antarctic station. Until 2006 it was a base of the Chilean Navy.

Fact #3. The Chilean Antarctic Territory uses the Magallanes Time zone

Another one of interesting facts about the Chilean Antarctic Territory regards time. The territory corresponds geographically to areas UTC-4, UTC-5 and UTC-6. Despite that fact it uses the Magallanes and Chilean Antarctica Time zone UTC-3.

Fact #4. The Chilean Antarctic Territory has a permanently inhabited town

Villa Las Estrellas is a Chilean town on King George Island within the Chilean Antarctic Territory. The town has a summer population of 150 and a winter population of 80. Except for homes, here is a school, hospital, bank, chapel, gym and a souvenir shop.

Fact #5. The Chilean Antarctic Territory houses the only international airport in Antarctica

Here's the next one of fun facts about the Chilean Antarctic Territory. The Teniente Rodolfo Marsh Martin Airport (IATA: TNM) is the only airport in Antarctica that has an IATA code. This is because it is served by various public flights, usually as part of a cruise.

Fact #6. The Chilean Antarctic Territory was once claimed by Spain

The Treaty of Tordesillas, signed in 1494 by Spain and Portugal set their areas of global influence, west and east, respectively, according to a line running from pole to pole that was never demarcated, so the Antarctic areas claimed by Chile today, fell under Spanish influence.

Fact #7. The Chilean Antarctic Territory is a popular tourist destination

Another one of interesting facts about the Chilean Antarctic Territory regards tourism. Today, the development of tourism has increased significantly through the flights handled by Dap Group that depart from Punta Arenas and arrive at King George Island.

Fact #8. The highest peak of the Chilean Antarctic Territory is claimed by Argentina and the UK

Mount Hope is a 3,239 m (10,627 ft) tall mountain, forming the central and highest peak of the Eternity Range in northern Palmer Land, Antarctic Peninsula. The mountain is located within the area claimed by Chile, Argentina and the United Kingdom.

Fact #9. The Chilean Antarctic Territory is inhabited with earless seals

The next one of fun facts about the Chilean Antarctic Territory regards seals. The most abundant species of seals here are earless seals confined to polar climates. Adult ones grow up to 1,17 m (3,8 ft) in length, 45 kg (99 lb) in weight and feed on fish and krill.

Fact #10. Chile's President was the world's first head of state to visit Antarctica

Base General Bernardo O'Higgins Riquelme is a permanently staffed Chilean research station in Antarctica. The base was established in 1948 by the President of the Republic of Chile, Gabriel Gonzalez Videla, the world's first head of state to visit Antarctica.
