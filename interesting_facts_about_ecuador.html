Interesting facts about Ecuador

Interesting facts about Ecuador presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited by) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science and of course people. Ecuador is a country in South America on the Pacific coast, the territory of which is crossed by the equator. The capital of Ecuador, Quito, located in the foothills of the Andes, is famous for its well-preserved Spanish colonial center with richly decorated palaces and temples of the 16th and 17th centuries. The country is also famous for the Incan Temple of the Sun, the Chimborazo mountain, the Panama hat and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Ecuador.

Fact #1. Ecuador is the only country in the world named after a geo location

Here’s the first one of fun facts about Ecuador. The official name of Ecuador is "Republica del Ecuador" which can be literally translated from Spanish as "Republic of the Equator". Ecuador is the only country in the world to be named after its geo location.

Fact #2. Ingapirca is one of the most visited tourist attractions in Ecuador

Ingapirca is a name of the ancient Incan ruins, located in Canar Province, Ecuador. It was a fortress built to resupply the Incan troops. The largest structure of the complex is the Temple of the Sun which served to conduct numerous ritual celebrations.

Fact #3. Ecuador is one of the world's most biodiverse countries

Another one fact about Ecuador regards its biodiversity. Ecuador is one of the 17 countries in the world to have the most biodiverse flora and fauna. The country is home to 16,000 species of plants, 6,000 species of butterflies, 1,600 species of birds and not only.

Fact #4. Mount Chimborazo in Ecuador is the closest to the sun point on Earth

Mount Chimborazo is an inactive volcano, standing 6,263 m (20,548 ft) tall in the Andes, central Ecuador. Due to the unique location along the equatorial bulge, it's the farthest point on Earth from its center. Also, it's the closest point on Earth to the sun.

Fact #5. Ecuador is the world's first country to have legally enforceable rights of nature

The next one of interesting facts about Ecuador regards its nature. The country is known to have unique ecological heritage. Moreover, this is the world's first country to have a constitution with fully recognizable and legally enforceable Rights of Nature.

Fact #6. One of the most popular traditional dishes in Ecuador is Fanesca

Fanesca is a soup traditionally prepared only in the week before Easter. Its main ingredients are milk, pumpkin, 12 different kinds of beans, cod and garnish (eggs, patties). The twelve beans represent the twelve apostles and the cod represents the Jesus himself.

Fact #7. The world's first UNESCO World Heritage Site is located in Ecuador

The next one fun fact about Ecuador regards UNESCO’s heritage list. Quito is the capital of Ecuador. The historic centre of Quito was the first World Heritage Site (along with the historic centre of Krakow, Poland) inscribed on the UNESCO's list in 1978.

Fact #8. The Galapagos Islands are part of the Republic of Ecuador

The Galapagos Islands located 906 km (563 mi) off the coast of Ecuador are an archipelago of volcanic islands famous for their large number of unique and endemic species. This archipelago is one of five UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Ecuador.

Fact #9. The most watched and played sport in Ecuador is football

Another one fact about Ecuador regards its most beloved sport, which is football. The matches of the national team are the most watched sports events in the country. The national football team qualified for the FIFA World Cup finals in 2002, 2006 and 2014.

Fact #10. The national tree of Ecuador is famous for treating malaria

The national tree of Ecuador is Cinchona pubescens. This tree has always been honored for its medical properties. Its bark contains quinine and some other alkaloids that were effective against malaria until 1944, when quinine was synthesized artificially.
