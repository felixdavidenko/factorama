Interesting facts about Fiji

Interesting facts about Fiji presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Fiji is a state in the South Pacific Ocean on the archipelago of the same name, consisting of more than 300 islands. Its appearance is shaped by quaint landscapes, palm-lined beaches and transparent lagoons surrounded by coral reefs. Most of the country's population lives on the two largest islands – Viti Levu and Vanua Levu. On Viti Levu, there is the capital of Fiji, the port city of Suva, where buildings are designed in the British colonial style. The country is famous for its wood-and-straw buildings, ritual dances, island hotels, endemic birds and not only. Fact #1. Fiji consists of more than 330 islands and 500 islets

Here's the first one of fun facts about Fiji. The Republic of Fiji is an island country that consists of over 330 islands, only 110 of which are inhabited. Also, there are 500 more islets, including which, the country's total area is 18,300 sq km (7,100 sq mi).

Fact #2. The national symbol of Fiji is the legendary tear flower

The national symbol of Fiji is a Medinilla waterhousei, which is a species of flowering plant endemic to the island of Taveuni. According to the local romantic legend, tears of the young girl who was forbidden to marry her beloved one became the flower.

Fact #3. Almost 90% of the population of Fiji live on 2 islands

Another one of interesting facts about Fiji regards its population. Almost 87% of the country's total population inhabit the two major islands – Viti Levu and Vanua Levu. Almost three-quarters of Fijians inhabit coasts or the capital city or suburban centers.

Fact #4. The name of Fiji was a result of the mispronunciation

The British explorer and navigator Captain James Cook first met Fijians in Tonga. While Fijians called their home Viti, Tongans in turn called it Fisi. The name Fiji was derived from the Anglicised pronunciation of the mix of these two words.

Fact #5. One of the Fiji's islands is located 450 km apart from the country

Here goes the next one of fun facts about Fiji. The Conway Reef, also known as Ceva-I-Ra Reef, is a 2,5 km (1,5 mi) long coral reef which is part of the republic of Fiji. What's curious is that it's situated as far as 450 km (280 mi) apart from the Fiji islands.

Fact #6. Whale teeth were once an active currency in Fiji

It's known that before contact with Europeans, Fijians have already developed their own monetary system. This system was based on the polished teeth of the sperm whale species. These polished teeth were an active currency among natives.

Fact #7. Tourism is the largest sector of the economy in Fiji

Here goes another one of interesting facts about Fiji. Tourism here is the leading and most profitable economic activity. This industry earns more money than the revenue from the country's two largest goods exports, which are sugar and garments.

Fact #8. An earth oven is still a popular cooking method in Fiji

An earth oven or a cooking pit, known locally as the Lovo is still popular and in active use in Fiji. The lovo is a pit in the ground lined with fire-resistant stones and used to trap heat. Such a pit is most often used for baking, smoking and steaming food.

Fact #9. The highest peak in Fiji serves as a habitat for the endangered Red-throated Lorikeet

Here's the next one of fun facts about Fiji. Mount Tomanivi is an extinct volcano and the country's highest mount standing 1,324 m (4,344 ft) tall. Its slopes are serving as an important habitat for birds, especially critically endangered Red-throated Lorikeet.

Fact #10. Rugby is the national sport in Fiji

Rugby is the most popular and is considered to be the national team sport in Fiji introduced to the country in the 1880s. The Fiji National Rugby Sevens Team has won the Rugby World Cup Sevens two times and the World Rugby Sevens Series three times.
