Interesting facts about the Dominican Republic

Interesting facts about the Dominican Republic presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited by) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science and of course people. The Dominican Republic is a Caribbean state located on the island of Hispaniola. The country is famous for its beaches, resorts and golf courses. Its territory is covered with tropical forests, savannas and mountains, the highest of which is Pico Duarte. The capital, Santo Domingo, has preserved evidence of Spanish rule, presented in the Colonial Zone. The country is also famous for its Punta Cana resort city, Bachata dance and music genre, an oatmeal juice and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about the Dominican Republic.

Fact #1. The Dominican Republic shares the island with another sovereign country

Here’s the first one of fun facts about the Dominican Republic. The country occupies the eastern part of the island of Hispanola, which it shares with Haiti. This makes Hispaniola one of only two Caribbean islands (after Saint Martin), shared by two countries.

Fact #2. The Dominican Republic is home to the largest lake in the Caribbean

Lake Enriquillo is a saline lake, located between the provinces of Independencia and Bahoruco in the Dominican Republic. Its surface area is 375 sq km (144 sq mi), making it the largest lake in the entire Caribbean. It’s located 46 m (151 ft) below the sea level.

Fact #3. Some first structures in the Americas were built in the Dominican Republic

Here’s another one of interesting facts about the Dominican Republic. The country is famous for having some first structures built in the Americas. The country has the first cathedral, the first castle, the first monastery and the first fortress built in the Americas.

Fact #4. The Dominican Republic is the second largest nation in the Antilles

The Dominican Republic is the second largest nation (after Cuba) in the Antilles with total area of 48,671 sq km (18,792 sq mi). Also, the Dominican Republic is the third largest country in the region with a total population of approximately 10,5 million people.

Fact #5. The Dominican Republic was a filming site for one of the Godfather series

The next one of fun facts about the Dominican Republic regards the Godfather series. The second part of the series included the Cuban Revolution scenes. The fact is that these scenes were filmed at the Presidential Palace, located in Santo Domingo.

Fact #6. The largest gold mine in the Americas is located in the Dominican Republic

The Pueblo Viejo is a gold mine, located in the Sanchez Ramirez Province, the Dominican Republic. This gold mine is in fact the largest in the Americas and 8th largest in the world. In 2018, this mine produced approximately 580,000 ounces of gold.

Fact #7. The national music and dance of the Dominican Republic is an Intangible Cultural Heritage

Another one of interesting facts about the Dominican Republic regards Merengue. It’s a genre of music and dance originated here in the 19th century. It was turned national by Rafael Trujillo. Later, it was inscribed in the List of Intangible Cultural Heritage.

Fact #8. Mangu is the most popular dish in the Dominican Republic

Mangu is a Dominican traditional dish often made of boiled plantains mashed with butter. The dish is often served with fried cheese, fried Dominican salami and fried eggs. These three components are often called "Los tres golpes" – "The three hits".

Fact #9. The Dominican Republic is home to the world famous amber stone mosquito

The next one of fun facts about the Dominican Republic regards history. In one of the Jurassic Park series there was shown a prehistoric mosquito inside the amber stone. This in fact is a fossilized tree resin, presented at the Amber Museum in Puerto Plata.

Fact #10. The most popular sport in the Dominican Republic is baseball

Baseball is the most popular and the most played sport in the Dominican Republic. By the number of Professional League Players the country is second only to the USA. In 2013, the national baseball team has won the World Baseball Classic competition.
