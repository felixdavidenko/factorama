Interesting facts about Kenya

Interesting facts about Kenya presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. Kenya is a country on the east coast of Africa, washed by the Indian Ocean. On its territory there are savannas, lakes, the huge Great Rift Valley and mountain ranges. Kenya is home to a wide variety of wildlife including lions, elephants and rhinos. In the capital of the country, Nairobi, tourists are offered to go to the Masai Mara reserve, where more than a million wildebeest migrate annually, and the Amboseli National Park, which offers a magnificent view of the Kilimanjaro volcano. The country is also famous for the cannibal bird, spotted reserve, male voice choir and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Kenya.

Fact #1. Kenya was named after the mountain

The first one of fun facts about Kenya regards its name. An 1882 map drawn by a Scottish geologist Joseph Thompsons, indicated the country's highest peak as Mt. Kenya. Later, the name of the mountain was accepted as the name of the country.

Fact #2. One of the world's best wildlife-viewing sites is located in Kenya

Amboseli National Park is a 8,000 sq km (3,100 sq mi) ecosystem, located in Kajiado County, Kenya. With more than 400 different species of birds and 47 raptor species, this national park is considered one of the best wildlife-viewing sites in the world.

Fact #3. The world's most complete early human skeleton was found in Kenya

Another one of interesting facts about Kenya regards Turkana Boy. It's the name given to fossil of the 11 years old youth who lived about 1,5 million years ago. It was discovered in 1984 and is known as the most complete early human skeleton ever found.

Fact #4. The second highest mountain in Africa is located in Kenya

Mount Kenya, standing 5,199 m (17,057 ft) tall is the highest mountain in Kenya and the second highest in Africa. It's located some 150 km (90 mi) northeast of the capital Nairobi, inside the National Park listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1997.

Fact #5. Lara Croft: Tomb Raider - The Cradle of Life was filmed in Kenya

The next one of fun facts about Kenya regards filming. Lara Croft: Tomb Raider - The Cradle of Life is a 2003 action adventure. Besides the other filming locations, partially, this motion picture was filmed in Kenya at Amboseli and Hell's Gate National parks.

Fact #6. The national bird of Kenya is a cannibal

The lilac-breasted roller is the national bird of Kenya. It inhabits the tops of trees in the areas of up to 2,000 m (6,561 ft) above sea level and feeds with other small birds. The average bird is up to 38 cm (15 in) in length and has a wingspan of up to 58 cm (22,8 in).

Fact #7. Kenya is home to the sudden storm lake

Here's another one of interesting facts about Kenya. Lake Naivasha is a freshwater lake, located at an altitude of 1,884 m (6,181 ft). Its name is derived from the local Maasai name Nai'posha, which means "rough water", due to the frequent sudden storms.

Fact #8. Kenya is one of the world's strongest countries in athletics

Athletics (track and field) is one of the most popular sports in Kenya with plenty of athletes dominating the distance running. The country has regularly produced Olympic champions in various distances, including 800 m, 1,500 m, 5,000 m, 10,000 m and not only.

Fact #10. Kenya has the highest number of Quakers in the world

Quakers belong to a Christian denomination known as the Religious Society of Friends. Quakers are united by their belief in the ability to access the light within, or "that of God in everyone". Kenya has the world's highest number of Quakers with over 145,000 members.
