Interesting facts about the United States

Interesting facts about the United States presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. The United States of America is one of the most well-known and recognizable countries in the world. This country has one of the world's leading economies and one of the most powerful armies. It consists of 50 states inhabited by almost 330 million people. It's also one of the world's most visited countries because it’s home to Hollywood, Las Vegas, Hawaii, 24 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, and many other places, that each year attracts millions of curious people. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about the USA for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. The Liberty Statue was actually built in France

The first of the fun facts about the United States regards the Liberty Statue. The fact is that it wasn’t built in the U.S. It was gifted to the U.S. by France. So in fact, it was built in France, shipped in boxes to the U.S., and only then assembled on Liberty Island.

Fact #2. The USA is home to the Red Cardinal also known as the Angry Bird

The red cardinal (the state bird of 7 U.S. states) is not in vain considered an angry bird. Male species can be really aggressive, especially, when defending their territory. Sometimes, they can even attack first, if they consider some creature as a threat.

Fact #3. Alaska was not always part of the United States

The next one of the interesting facts about the United States regards Alaska. At the beginning of the 19th Century, Alaska was Russian territory. Only after it was sold off to the Americans in 1867 for the cost of $7.2 million, it has become a U.S. territory.

Fact #4. The 5th most visited museum in the world is located in the U.S.

The most visited museum in the U.S. and the fifth most visited museum in the world is the Smithsonian National Air and Space Museum located in Washington D.C. Each year, this museum receives more than 7 million visitors from all around the world.

Fact #5. The USA is home to 24 UNESCO World Heritage Sites

The next fact about the United States regards UNESCO World Heritage Sites. The country is known to have a total of 24 heritage sites. Of these, 12 are natural sites, 11 are cultural and 1 is mixed. Two sites are known as transboundary, shared with Canada.

Fact #6. The Maine Coon cat breed was named after the state where it was bred

The Maine Coon cat breed is considered the oldest in North America. Also known as “The gentle giant”, this cat is the official mammal of the U.S. state of Maine (where it was bred on the Tearbox farm) and one of the most popular cat breeds in the United States.

Fact #7. Washington D.C. was not always the U.S. capital

Another one of the interesting facts about the United States regards Washington D.C. The fact is that it wasn’t always the U.S. capital as many think. It obtained this status only in 1790. Before that, the proud title of the U.S. capital was held by New York City.

Fact #8. The Entertainment Capital of the World is located in the U.S.

Las Vegas, also known as the Entertainment Capital of the World is located in Nevada, USA. This city is renowned all around the world for its casinos, hotels, and nightlife. Las Vegas claims to have the world's largest number of AAA Five Diamond Hotels.

Fact #9. In Kentucky, there is more bourbon than people

Here is the next one of the fun facts about the United States. Kentucky state produces over 4,7 million barrels of bourbon a year. The population of the state is just over 4,3 million people. So in fact, in Kentucky, there is more bourbon than people.

Fact #10. The U.S. flag was not always the way it looks now

Initially, the American flag had only 13 stars, each representing the colonies that declared independence from Great Britain. Each new flag represented the new state. Today, after many changes, the American flag has 50 stars, representing the 50 states.
