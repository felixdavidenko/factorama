Interesting facts about Germany

Interesting facts about Germany presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science and of course people. Germany is a state in Western Europe with forests, rivers, mountain ranges, and beach resorts in the North Sea. The country's history spans over two thousand years. The capital, Berlin, is bustling with culture and nightlife. Many sights of the city, in particular the Brandenburg Gate, remind of the events of World War II. Munich is famous for its Oktoberfest and beer restaurants. The country is also famous for the Berlin Wall, timber-framed architecture, the world’s largest zoo, castles, sausages, cars, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Germany for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Reichstag is one of the most recognizable German landmarks

The first one of fun facts about Germany regards the Reichstag. It’s the meeting place of the Federal Assembly. It was built in 1894, burned in 1933, bombed in 1945, then restored in the 1970s, and rebuilt in the 1990s. Today it’s one of the most popular tourist attractions.

Fact #2. German is the second most widely spoken Germanic language after English

German is one of the most widely used languages in the world. Besides Germany, this language is official in Switzerland, Luxembourg, Liechtenstein, and Austria. Speaking about German in total, it's used all around the world by more than 100 million people.

Fact #3. There are more than 20,000 castles in Germany

Another one of interesting facts about Germany regards castles. This country is home to approx 20 to 25 thousand castles. The most famous of them is the Neuschwanstein castle in southern Bavaria, which is also the most visited. Over 1,3 million people visit it annually.

Fact #4. The world’s biggest zoo is located in Germany

Berlin's Zoological Garden is the biggest zoo in the world if measured by the number of animals. It's home to over 20,000 animals of 1,380 different species. If counting including various parks, reserves, and aquariums, the number of zoo facilities in Germany exceeds 700.

Fact #5. The chancellor of Germany has her own Barbie doll

The next one of fun facts about Germany regards its former Chancellor – Angela Merkel. In 2009, the American toy manufacturing company Mattel decided to celebrate its 50th anniversary in a pretty unusual way. They produced the Barbie doll of Angela Merkel.

Fact #6. Sausages are one of the most beloved foods in Germany

It's considered that around 1,500 different varieties of sausages are produced in Germany. Any gourmet will surely find his favorite. There are boiled, smoked, liverwort, and many other varieties. Sausages are eaten both at celebrations and in the daily diet.

Fact #7. Germany is one of the world’s leading car manufacturers

Here’s another one of interesting facts about Germany. Such famous brands as Audi, BMW, Mercedes, Porsche, Volkswagen, and not only, all belong to the German auto industry. The country is home to Volkswagen Group Corp ranked 7th in the Fortune Global 500 list.

Fact #8. Germany is known to be the land of poets and thinkers

Germany gave the world a lot of persons that we know today as the greatest in their field. Here are some of them. Bach, Beethoven, Goethe, Handel, Schumann, Mendelssohn, Strauss, Wagner, Kant, Hegel, Nietzsche, Schopenhauer. They were all Germans.

Fact #9. Oktoberfest is called October but starts in September

The next one of fun facts about Germany regards Oktoberfest. Most part of this famous beer fest takes place in September. Initially, in 1810 it took place in October, but because it's easier to attract visitors when it's warmer, the starting date was slightly shifted.

Fact #10. The world-famous Devil’s Bridge is located in Germany

One of the world's most picturesque bridges, also known as The Devil's Bridge is located in Gablenz, Germany. The Rakotzbrucke Bridge got its nickname due to the ancient belief that the magical circle in reflection actually represents the hands of the devil.
