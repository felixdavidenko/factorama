Interesting facts about Bolivia

Interesting facts about Bolivia presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science and of course people. Bolivia is a country in the central part of South America. Its relief is very diverse: the Andes, the Atacama Desert and the rainforests of the Amazon basin are located here. The capital of the country, La Paz, is located on the Andean Altiplano plateau with a height of over 3,500 m (11,482 ft) next to the snow-capped Ilimani mountain. Near the border with Peru lies the largest lake on the continent – Titicaca, whose surface resembles polished glass. The country is also famous for the pink lake, the stone tree, the salt flat, the train cemetery and not only. So, get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Bolivia.

Fact #1. Bolivia is the largest landlocked country in the Southern Hemisphere

Here goes the first one of fun facts about Bolivia. This country has no access to the ocean and is known as landlocked. So, with 1,098,581 sq km (424,164 sq mi) of area, Bolivia is the largest landlocked country in the Southern Hemisphere.

Fact #2. More than 17,000 species of plants are growing in Bolivia

It's known that Bolivia boasts more than 17,000 species of seed plants, including 1,500 species of non-vascular land plants, 1,200 species of fern and over 800 species of mushrooms. Also, over 3,000 species of medicinal plants are growing here.

Fact #3. Bolivia is one of the world's most biodiverse countries

Another one of interesting facts about Bolivia regards biodiversity. The country is home to more than 2,900 species of animals, including 1,400 birds, 635 fish, 277 reptiles, 398 mammals, 204 amphibians, and over 3,000 types of butterfly.

Fact #4. There are over 4,000 kinds of potatoes growing in Bolivia

The main sector of the economy of Bolivia is agriculture. Almost 45% of the country's labor force is involved in agriculture. Most workers are involved in subsistence farming. What’s curious is that Bolivia grows more than 4,000 kinds of potatoes.

Fact #5. The world's highest navigable lake is located in Bolivia

Here's the next one of fun facts about Bolivia. Lake Titicaca is a freshwater lake, located on the border between Bolivia and Peru. Because of its elevation of 3,812 m (12,507 ft) above sea level it is known as the world's highest navigable lake.

Fact #6. Bolivia has its own traditional pastry

Saltena is a pastry originated in the early 19th century in the city of Potosi, Bolivia. Saltenas are often filled with meat in a spicy sauce. Thanks to the gelatin, the liquid filling hardens in the fridge and melts when baked, making saltena juicy and savory.

Fact #7. Bolivia is one of the few countries in the world to use reed boats

Another one of interesting facts about Bolivia regards boats. This country is one of the few in the world where reed boats are still in use. These boats are considered the oldest known. The earliest found remains of such a boat are 7,000 years old.

Fact #8. About 70% of the world lithium reserves are concentrated in Bolivia

According to the U.S. Geological Service, Bolivia has about 5,4 million cubic tons of lithium, which estimates from 50% to 70% of all world reserves. Lithium is used in producing batteries for electric vehicles and has a huge technological potential.

Fact #9. One of the most popular tourist attractions in Bolivia is a stone tree

Here's the next one of fun facts about Bolivia. The Eduardo Avaroa Andean Fauna National Reserve is home to the famous Stone tree. It's a standalone rock formation shaped like a 7 m (23 ft) high tree which is one of the country's most popular landmarks.

Fact 10. The highest woodlands in the world are located in Bolivia

Nevado Sajama is the highest peak in Bolivia, standing 6,542 m (21,463 ft) tall. The mountain is famous for being covered with sparse vegetation. But over the heights of 5,000 m (16,000 ft) Polylepis tarapacana trees form woodlands.
