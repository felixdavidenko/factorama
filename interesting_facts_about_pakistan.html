Interesting facts about Pakistan

Interesting facts about Pakistan presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports, and of course people. The Islamic Republic of Pakistan is a state in South Asia, formed in 1947 as a result of the division of the territory of British India. It is washed by the waters of the Arabian Sea in the south, bordered by Iran in the southwest, Afghanistan in the northwest and north, China in the northeast and India in the east. Pakistan is the world's fifth most populous country and the world's second most populous Muslim country after Indonesia. The country is also famous for its antique Asian trade route, one of the largest food streets in Asia, mount K2, jingle trucks, the hour house and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Pakistan for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Pakistan is the world's fifth most populated country

The first one of fun facts about Pakistan regards its population. The Islamic Republic of Pakistan, located in South Asia has a total population of over 212 million people. According to this data, Pakistan is the fifth most populated country in the world.

Fact #2. Pakistan is one of the top freelancing nations in the world

The IT sector of Pakistan is growing fast. The country has more than 82 million internet users, making Pakistan the world's 9th largest population of Internet users. The IT sector employs over 12,000 people, which puts the country among top-5 freelancing nations.

Fact #3. Pakistan is home to the antique Asian trade route

Another one of interesting facts about Pakistan regards the Wakhan Corridor. It's a 350 km (220 mi) long and 13-65 km (8-40 mi) wide pass, separating Pakistan from Tajikistan. This pass has been used as a trade route to and from Asia since antiquity.

Fact #4. The world's deepest sea port is located in Pakistan

The Gwadar Port, located in Balochistan province on the Arabian Sea is the deepest sea port in the world. It has an area of 927 h (2,292 acres) and a total capacity of over 30 million tons of cargo per year. In 2015 it was officially leased to China for 43 years.

Fact #5. One of the world's earliest major cities is located in Pakistan

Here's the next one of fun facts about Pakistan. Mohenjo-daro is an archaeological site, located in the province of Sindh. It was built around 2,500 BCE and is known as one of the world's earliest major cities along with ancient Egypt and Minoan Crete.

Fact #6. There is a lake in Pakistan, named after the Egyptian prince

The Lake Saiful Muluk in the Kaghan Valley, is named after the legendary prince. According to the fairy tale, written by the Sufi poet Muhammad Bakhsh, it was the site, where the Egyptian Prince Saiful Malook fell in love with a princess Badri-ul-Jamala.

Fact #7. Pakistan is literally a land of purity

Here's another one of interesting facts about Pakistan. The name of the country which was coined in 1933 is derived from Urdu and Persian languages and literally means "A land abounding in the pure" or "A land in which the pure abound" accordingly.

Fact #8. One of the largest food streets of Asia is located in Pakistan

Port Grand is a recreational area in Karachi, Pakistan, built along the waterfront of the Native Jetty Bridge. The 1 km (0,6 mi) long bridge was transformed into a 3,716 sq m (40,000 sq ft) food and drink zone. Today it's one of the largest food streets in Asia.

Fact #9. Pakistan is home to the 16th century UNESCO's citadel city

The next one of fun facts about Pakistan regards the Lahore Fort. It's a citadel city, located in the city of Lahore that covers an area of more than 20 h (49 acres). It was built as far as in 1566. In 1981 it was inscribed in the UNESCO World Heritage List.

Fact #10. Pakistan is the first Muslim country to put a satellite into space

Badr-I, the country's first space satellite was built by the Space & Upper Atmosphere Research Commission (SUPARCO) and launched on July 16, 1990. This achievement made Pakistan the first Muslim country to build and launch a satellite into space.
