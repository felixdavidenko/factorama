Interesting facts about Egypt

Interesting facts about Egypt presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, culture, nature, sports and of course people. Egypt is a country in Northeast Africa and the Middle East. Its rich history dating back more than five thousand years is reminded by archaeological sites in the fertile valley of the Nile River, including the pyramids of Giza, the Great Sphinx, as well as the Karnak Temple, the walls of which are dotted with hieroglyphs, and the Valley of the Kings with the tombs of the pharaohs in the Luxor region. The country is famous for the Egyptian Museum, which displays the mummies of the pharaohs and gilded wooden statues of Tutankhamun. Also, Egypt is famous for the Red Sea, camels, UNESCO World Heritage Sites and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Egypt for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Egypt is the world’s most populated Arab country

The first one of fun facts about Egypt regards its population. The country’s population is about 100 million. By this figure, Egypt is the world’s most populated Arab country. Comparing to other countries, Egypt is the 3rd most populated in Africa and 14th in the world.

Fact #2. Egypt is home to seven UNESCO World Heritage Sites

Egypt is known to have seven sites inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. Those are: Abu Mena; Historic Cairo; Thebes and its Necropolis; Memphis and its Necropolis; Nubian monuments from Abu Simbel to Philae; the Saint Catherine area and Wadi Al-Hitan.

Fact #3. It is still unknown how 4,500 years ago the Pyramids were built so accurate

Here’s another one of interesting facts about Egypt. It's believed that Egyptians used two constellations to build and align their famous Pyramids of Giza. The fact is that it's still unknown, how they were aligned in north-south with an accuracy of 0,05 degrees.

Fact #4. Cairo is about to stop being the Egypt’s capital

On March 2015, the Egyptian housing minister Moustafa Madbouly announced that due to rapid population growth, the government decided to build a New Administrative Capital. This new city will be located some 35 km (21,7 mi) east of the present capital city of Cairo.

Fact #5. Cleopatra was not ethnically Egyptian

The next one of fun facts about Egypt regards Cleopatra. Despite being known as Egyptian, in fact, she was Greek. Though she was born in Egypt, Cleopatra traced her family roots to Macedonian Greece. She was a descendant of a Greek general Ptolemy I Soter.

Fact #6. Football is the most popular sport in Egypt

Football in Egypt is the most popular sport by far. The Egyptian national football team "Pharaohs" has won the African Cup of Nations 7 times, in 1957, 1959, 1986, 1998, 2008 and 2010. This achievement made them record holders of most African cup wins.

Fact #7. Social media in Egypt are controlled by the government

Here goes another one of interesting facts about Egypt. The social media in Egypt are closely monitored by the authorities. More than 5,000 active users are subject to the country's media laws, and every new account must be licensed by the government.

Fact #8. Kushari is the national dish in Egypt

In Egypt there are a lot of popular dishes, such as ful medames, mashed fava beans, molokhiya and not only. But the national dish is considered to be kushari. It's a mixture of lentils, noodles and rice, topped with a spicy tomato sauce made using fried onions.

Fact #9. Egyptians considered cats to be sacred animals

The next one of fun facts about Egypt regards cats. It is known that ancient Egyptians considered cats to be sacred animals. Cats in Egypt were treated well and most families kept a cat as a domestic animal. They believed that cats bring good luck and wealth.

Fact #10. In Ancient Egypt, makeup was worn not only by women but also by men

Egyptians believed that special makeup not only protects from the sun, but also has a magical healing effect. That's why they wore a makeup of green (made of copper) and black (made of lead) color. Moreover, both men and women wore such a makeup.
