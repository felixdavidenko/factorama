Interesting facts about Indonesia

Interesting facts about Indonesia presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports, and of course people. Indonesia is a state in Southeast Asia that covers the territory of several thousand volcanic islands. They are inhabited by hundreds of peoples who speak different languages. The capital of Indonesia – a large, picturesque city of Jakarta is located on the island of Java. The country is known for its coral reefs, volcanoes and rare tigers. It's a country of the world's largest Buddhist temple, cockfights, rickshaws and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Indonesia for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Indonesia consists of more than 17,000 islands

Here’s the first one of fun facts about Indonesia. It’s known as the world's largest island country, but it's not a country of just one, ten, fifty, 100 or even 1,000 islands. Also known as the Indonesian Archipelago, it consists of 17,508 officially listed islands.

Fact #2. Java is the world's most populous island

The population of Indonesia is over 267 million people. Java is an island of Indonesia with a population of over 141 million people, which is 56,7% of the country's total population. What's curious is that by land mass it's only the 5th largest island in Indonesia.

Fact #3. There are almost 1,000 local languages in Indonesia

Another one of fun facts about Indonesia regards language. The official language here is Indonesian. Despite the Dutch influence, this language has not taken root here. Most citizens speak one of over 700 local languages while others use over 270 Papuan languages.

Fact #4. Indonesia has only two seasons instead of four

Since Indonesia sits along the equator, its climate is almost the same all year round. The country has no such climate extremes as winter or summer. Indonesia, in fact, has only two seasons – a wet season (November-April) and a dry season (May-October).

Fact #5. Indonesia hosts its own Tour de France

The next one of interesting facts about Indonesia regards cycling. The Tour de Indonesia is an annual professional cycling race, held in Java. What's curious is that this event is inspired by European Tour de France – the world's most prestigious cycling race.

Fact #6. Indonesia is one of the world's most biodiverse countries

Such factors as size of the country, humid tropical climate and lots of islands make Indonesia one of the world's most biodiverse countries. Over 35% of 1,530 species of birds as well as over 40% of 515 species of mammals in Indonesia are endemic.

Fact #7. Rickshaws are common type of public transport in Indonesia

The next one of fun facts about Indonesia regards rickshaws. This is one of those few countries that still actively using such a type of transport. Since man-pulled rickshaws are becoming a thing of the past, they are replaced with cycle, auto or electric ones.

Fact #8. Tumpeng is considered a national dish of Indonesia

Indonesian cuisine is very diverse and full of spicy flavors. There are many regional cuisines and many popular dishes, such as Nasi goreng, Gado-gado, Sate, Soto and not only. However, the Ministry of Tourism declared Tumpeng as a national dish.

Fact #9. Indonesia is home to almost 130 active volcanoes

Here’s another one of interesting facts about Indonesia. The country lies on the Pacific Ring of Fire. It's a place where the Indo-Australian, the Pacific and the Eurasian tectonic plates are pushed between each other. That’s why Indonesia has 127 volcanoes.

Fact #10. Indonesia has its own National Aeronautics and Space Agency

Indonesia has its own space program and the National Institute of Aeronautics and Space. In the 1970s, Indonesia launched its first satellite PALAPA A1 from the Kennedy Space Center in the U.S. As for now, Indonesia has launched 18 different satellites.
