Interesting facts about New Caledonia

Interesting facts about New Caledonia presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. New Caledonia is an overseas territory of France, located on dozens of islands in the South Pacific. It is famous for its lagoons and beaches with palm trees with a variety of flora and fauna. The main island of Grande Terre is surrounded by a barrier reef popular among diving enthusiasts. The territorial capital of Noumea is famous for its French restaurants and luxury shops selling clothes from Parisian couturiers. The country is also famous for the world's largest gecko, the longest coral reef in the world, wood carving, metal huts and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about New Caledonia.

Fact #1. New Caledonia is a Pacific collectivity of France

The first one of fun facts about New Caledonia regards France. New Caledonia is a special collectivity of France, located in the southwest Pacific Ocean, east of Australia, north of New Zealand and some 17,000 km (11,000 mi) from Metropolitan France.

Fact #2. There are two state flags in use in New Caledonia

Up to 2010, the only flag used to represent New Caledonia was the flag of France. In July 2010, the Congress of New Caledonia voted in favor of a wish to fly the Kanak flag. A majority of New Caledonian communes, but not all, today fly both flags.

Fact #3. New Caledonia is an archipelago consisting of archipelagos

Here's another one of interesting facts about New Caledonia. The archipelago, part of the Melanesia subregion, includes the island of Grande Terre, the Loyalty Islands, the Chesterfield Islands, the Belep archipelago, the Isle of Pines, and a few remote islands.

Fact #4. The highest peak of New Caledonia is an important bird area

Mont Panie is a 1,628 m (5,341 ft) tall mountain in the Chaine Centrale range on the island of Grande Terre, New Caledonia. The range is recognized as an Important Bird Area by BirdLife International as it supports unique populations of local bird species.

Fact #5. New Caledonia was named after Scotland

The next one of fun facts about New Caledonia regards its shape. The first European to sight New Caledonia was British explorer Captain James Cook. He named the land "New Caledonia", as the northeast of the island reminded him of Scotland.

Fact #6. More than 30% of the population of New Caledonia is Europeans

The population of New Caledonia is made up of ethnic groups that arrived here in the last 150 years. Those are: Europeans (34,5%, mostly French, German, British and Italian minorities), Polynesians (11,8%), Indonesians (2,6%) and some other ethnic groups.

Fact #7. New Caledonia is considered a biological "Noah's Ark"

Here's another one of interesting facts about New Caledonia. Because of its geographical isolation since the end of the Cretaceous, the territory is a refugium, in effect a biological "Noah's Ark", an island home to both unique living plants and animals.

Fact #8. The society in New Caledonia consists of clans, tribes and chiefdoms

Kanak society in New Caledonia has several layers of customary authority. It consists of 5,000 clans led by clan chiefs and constitute 341 tribes. The tribes are grouped into 57 customary chiefdoms forming the administrative subdivisions of the areas.

Fact #9. Over 60 million years ago New Caledonia was part of Australia

Here's the next one of fun facts about New Caledonia. It's speculated that New Caledonia separated from Australia about 66 million years ago. Subsequently drifting in a north-eastern direction it reached its present position some 50 million years ago.

Fact #10. The world's largest gecko can only be found in New Caledonia

The Isle of Pines is an island in the Pacific Ocean in the archipelago of New Caledonia. The island is rich with wildlife and is home to several unusual creatures, including the New Caledonian giant gecko which is the largest extant gecko in the world.
