Interesting facts about Uruguay

Interesting facts about Uruguay presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. Uruguay is a country in South America known for lush greenery and beaches. In the center of the capital, the city of Montevideo, on Independence Square, there is the Citadel Gate - the surviving part of the fortress wall. From here begins the Old Town with Art Deco and Colonial buildings. Fish rows, marinas and parks are located along the Rambla of Montevideo. The country is also known for its fortresses, the sand-hand, the breast of a virgin mountain and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Uruguay.

Fact #1. The longest continuous sidewalk in the world is located in Uruguay

The first one of fun facts about Uruguay regards the Rambla. The Rambla of Montevideo is an avenue that stretches all along the coastline of Montevideo. With a total uninterrupted length of 22,2 km (13,7 mi), it's the longest continuous sidewalk in the world.

Fact #2. Uruguay is home to one of the world's most important forest reserves

The Lussich Arboretum was founded in 1896 by Uruguayan arboricultorist Antonio Lussich. It has a total area of 192 h (470 acres) and has more than 400 exotic and unique species of trees, the most notable of which are 45 different species of eucalyptus.

Fact #3. One of the most traveler-friendly airports in Latin America is located in Uruguay

Here goes another one of interesting facts about Uruguay. Carrasco International Airport (MVD) is the country's largest airport. With an annual passenger traffic of more than 2 mil, it's also known as one of the most traveler-friendly airports in Latin America.

Fact #4. Uruguay has its own national sandwich

Chivito is a national Uruguayan dish, made of thin slice of cooked beef steak with ham, bacon, cheese, mayo, olives and fried or hard boiled eggs in a bun, accompanied with fried potato. The recipe can include beets, peas, cucumber and not only.

Fact #5. Uruguay is home to the residential building without a lighthouse

Here's the next one of fun facts about Uruguay. Palacio Salvo is a 95 m (312 ft) tall office and residential building first intended to be a hotel. What's also curious is that initially, it had to have a 920 mm (36 in) lighthouse which was replaced by antennas.

Fact #6. The square-tailed bird is the national symbol of Uruguay

The rufous hornero is a medium-sized square-tailed bird with a decurved bill, native to savannas and shrublands of South America. The regular species are up to 20 cm (8 in) long and weigh up to 58 g (2,05 oz). The rufous hornero is the national bird of Uruguay.

Fact #7. There is a national heritage military island in Uruguay

Another one of interesting facts about Uruguay regards Gorriti Island. The island was discovered in 1516 and had a significant military importance. Today it'a a National Heritage island famous among tourists for its preserved abandoned cannons.

Fact #8. Uruguay is home to the largest colony of sea lions in the western hemisphere

Isla de Lobos, located 8 km (5 mi) southeast of Punta del Este are parts of a nature reserve which is a natural habitat for the largest colony of sea lions in the western hemisphere. The total population of the colony accounts for over 250,000 individual species.

Fact #9. Uruguay is home to the breast of a virgin

Here's the next one of fun facts about Uruguay. Cerro Batovi is a 224 m (735 ft) hill, located in the Tacuarembo Department, 25 km (15,5 mi) from the city of Tacuarembo. In Guarani, "Batovi" means "breast of a virgin". It's named so because of its curious shape.

Fact #10. One of the largest sea museums of Latin America is located in Uruguay

The Museum of the Sea, located in La Barra, Uruguay, is a natural history museum. It was opened in 1996, has an area of 2,300 sq m (25,000 sq ft) and consists of 4 large exhibition halls with over 5,000 specimens of marine, pirate, military heritage and not only.
