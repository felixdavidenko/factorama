Interesting facts about Guam

Interesting facts about Guam presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of this territory, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports, and of course people. Guam is an island in Micronesia in the Western Pacific Ocean, which has the status of an unincorporated organized territory of the United States. It is famous for its beaches, Chamorro Aboriginal villages, and Spanish colonial buildings. In the National Historical Park "War in the Pacific", one can learn about the role of Guam during World War II. The island is also famous for its national historical monuments, diving spots, rock art paintings, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Guam for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. The westernmost point of the United States is located in Oceania

Here's the first one of fun facts about Guam. This is an unincorporated territory of the United States located in the western Pacific Ocean. It is also the westernmost point of the U.S. reckoned from its geographic center, located in Belle Fourche, South Dakota.

Fact #2. Guam is the largest island in Micronesia

Micronesia is a subregion of Oceania that consists of thousands of small islands scattered in the western Pacific Ocean. There are approximately 2100 islands, with a total area of 2,700 sq km (1,000 sq mi), the largest of which is Guam, covering 582 sq km (225 sq mi).

Fact #3. People born in Guam are Americans but can't vote in the American presidential elections

The next one of interesting facts about Guam regards its citizens. Those who were born in Guam are American citizens. But what's curious, they can't vote in the U.S. presidential elections, and Guam delegates to the U.S. House of Representatives have no vote either.

Fact #4. There is a landmark in Guam that became famous due to the folktale

Two Lovers Point is a seaside cliff in Tamuning, Guam. It's a National Natural Landmark closely associated with the folktale of two lovers who leaped to their deaths from the 110 m (370 ft) high cliff. Today it's Guam's most famous landmark and a popular wedding spot.

Fact #5. Guam is the only natural habitat for the Mariana flying fox

Another one of fun facts about Guam regards the Mariana fruit bat. Also known as the Mariana flying fox, this megabat has an average weight of up to 500 g (1,1 lb), and a forearm length of 15,6 cm (6,1 in). It can be found only in the Mariana Islands including Guam.

Fact #6. There is a dam in Guam inscribed on the National Register of Historic Places

The Fonte River Dam is a 46 m (150 ft) long, and 7,3 m (24 ft) high concrete dam, located on the Fonte River in Guam. It was built in 1910 by the U.S. Navy in order to provide a water supply to Hagatna. In 2014 it was listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

Fact #7. Guam was settled as far as 3500 years ago

Another one of interesting facts about Guam regards its indigenous citizens also known as the Chamorro people. They are related to the Austronesian peoples of Indonesia, Micronesia, and Polynesia. The Chamorro people are known to settle the island some 3500 years ago.

Fact #8. The marinated meat wrapped in a tortilla is a national dish of Guam

Kelaguen is an indigenous people's dish eaten both as a side dish and a main course. A pickling marinade of lemon juice with onions and spices is used to marinate cooked chicken, fish, or beef. The dish is served cold and eaten with rice, wrapped in a warm corn or flour tortilla.

Fact #9. America's day begins in Guam

The next one of fun facts about Guam regards its unofficial motto - "Where America's day begins". It refers to the island's proximity to the International Date Line, an internationally accepted demarcation serving as the boundary between one calendar day and the next.

Fact #10. There is a popular submerged WWII relic in Guam

The Agat World War II Amtrac is an underwater relic of WWII, located about 460 m (1,510 ft) off Agat Invasion Beach at a depth of 15 m (50 ft). This is the almost intact remains of an amphibious tracked landing vehicle that participated in the 1944 Battle of Guam.
