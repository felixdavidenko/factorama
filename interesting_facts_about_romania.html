Interesting facts about Romania

Interesting facts about Romania presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, sports, science and of course people. Romania is a country in southeastern Europe with the famous historical region of Transylvania surrounded by the Carpathian Mountains. It is home to the medieval city of Sighisoara and many fortified churches and castles, including the cliff-top Bran Castle, where the legendary prince Dracula is believed to have lived. The country is also famous for the hidden gem, the best road in the world, the city with eyes, the bridge of lies and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Romania.

Fact #1. The tallest rock relief in Europe is located in Romania

The first one of fun facts about Romania regards the rock sculpture of Decebalus near the city of Orsova. It's a colossal rock carved face of the last king of Dacia, Decebalus. At 55 m (180 ft) in height and 25 m (82 ft) in width it's the tallest rock relief in Europe.

Fact #2. There is a car manufacturer company in Romania named after its historic region

Automobile Dacia S.A. is a Romanian car manufacturer founded in 1966 and named after the historic region of the same name. Dacia was the land that constitutes the present day Romania. It was inhabited by the Dacians and existed between 82 BC until 106 AD.

Fact #3. The name of Romania is the Latin adjective

Another one of interesting facts about Romania regards its name. The name of the country is derived from the Latin adjective Romanus. Romanians are a people that were living mostly in Central and South Eastern Europe and using a Romance language.

Fact #4. One of the earliest European human remains were found in Romania

Pestera cu Oase is a system of 12 karstic caves, located near the city of Anina, Romania. Literally translated as "The Cave with Bones'' it's a site, where in 2002, some 37 to 42,000 years old remains of one of the earliest modern human populations were found.

Fact #5. There is a star, named after the highest peak of Romania

Here's the next one of fun facts about Romania. Moldoveanu Peak, located in the Southern Carpathians is the country's highest peak, standing 2,544 m (8,346 ft) tall. In 2019 it was selected as the name for the XO-1 star, located 536 light-years away.

Fact #6. Sarmizegetusa Regia in Romania is often confused with Sarmizegetusa Ulpia

Sarmizegetusa Regia is an archaeological site, often confused with Ulpia Traiana Sarmizegetusa, built by Roman Emperor Trajan. Ulpia was discovered earlier and was mistakenly considered the Dacian capital, which led to many biased historic conclusions.

Fact #7. The second largest river delta in Europe is located in Romania

Here’s another one of interesting facts about Romania. The Danube Delta, the greater part of which lies in Romania, is the second largest river delta in Europe with a total area of 5,165 sq km (1,994 sq mi) and the best preserved river delta on the continent.

Fact #8. Sarma is so popular in Romania that is considered a national dish

Sarma, or the Cabbage Rolls is a popular Romanian dish often made of minced pork, rice, eggs, onion, thyme and dill rolled in a cabbage leaf. The dish is so popular that it is considered national in Romania and is often served for Easter and Christmas meals.

Fact #9. There is a national treasure museum in Romania

The next one of fun facts about Romania regards the National Museum of Romanian History, located in Bucharest. It has a surface area of 8,000 sq m (86,111 sq ft), 60 exhibition rooms and some specimens of the Pietroasele treasure, jewelry and not only.

Fact #10. There is a popular prison attraction in Romania

Fagaras Citadel is a historic monument, located in the city of Fagaras, Romania. It was built in 1310 for defense purposes. From 1948 to 1960 it was used as a prison. Today it's a popular tourist attraction that funсtions as a museum and houses various artifacts.
