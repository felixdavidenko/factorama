Interesting facts about Adelie Land

Interesting facts about Adelie Land presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of this territory, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, culture, economy, science, nature, and of course people. Adelie Land – a sector of Antarctica, which France claims, is the administrative district of its Southern and Antarctic Territories. Adelie Land is bordered on both sides by the Australian Antarctic Territory, namely Clary Coast (part of Wilkes Land) to the west and George V Land to the east. This land is known for the story of its name, for the penguin of the same name, for the Antarctic meteorite, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Adelie Land for children, adults, the elderly, and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Adelie Land is a French part of the continent of Antarctica

The first one of interesting facts about Adelie Land regards France, which administered this territory since 1955 along with the other four districts of French Southern and Atlantic Lands. France has applied here the Antarctic Treaty System rules ever since 1961.

Fact #2. Adelie Land predominantly consists of glaciers with minor hills and rocks

Adelie Land has a shore length of about 350 km (220 mi) and its inland part extends about 2,600 km (1,600 mi) toward the South Pole. Most part of Adelie Land is a snow-covered glacier and its total area is approximately 432,000 sq km (167,000 sq mi).

Fact #3. The population of Adelie Land may vary depending on the season

Here’s another one of fun facts about Adelie Land. This land has a permanent "Dumont d'Urville Station", which is the only inhabited place on Adelie Land. What's curious is that the number of its inhabitants may vary from 25 during winter to 78 during summer.

Fact #4. The first meteorite found in Antarctica was found on Adelie Land

On December 5, 1912, in Antarctica was found a meteorite. It was found by Francais Bickerton, who was a member of Sir Douglas Mawson's Australasian Antarctic Expedition. This meteorite was decided to be named after Adelie Land, where it was found.

Fact #5. The name of Adelie Land is the name of its explorer's wife

The next one of interesting facts about Adelie Land regards its name. The coast of Adelie Land was discovered by the French explorer Jules Dumont d'Urville (1790-1842) in 1840. He decided to name the land after his wife, Adelie Dumont d'Urville (1798-1842).

Fact #6. Adelie Land was a filming site for the Oscar-winning movie about penguins

"The March of the Penguins" is a 2005 French documentary directed, produced, and co-written by the National Geographic Society. The documentary was shot around the Dumont d'Urville Station and won the 2006 Oscar as the Best Documentary Feature.

Fact #7. The Adelie penguin is named after Adelie Land

The next one of fun facts about Adelie Land regards penguins. One of the most common species of penguin that can be found in Antarctica is the Adelie penguin. This penguin was first discovered in 1840 by Jules Dumont d'Urville and was named after Adelie Land.

Fact #8. The Dumont d'Urville was not the first research station on Adelie Land

France is known to have only one research base on Adelie Land. Partially that's true. The fact is prior to the Dumont d'Urville Station (est. 1956) there was the Port-Martin Station (est. 1950). But it was destroyed by a fire and abandoned in 1952.

Fact #9. It takes 7 tractors to deliver supplies to the research station

Here’s another one of interesting facts about Adelie Land. All the supplies are transported here by the cargo ship and then by tractors directly to the station. It takes 7 tractors and a team of up to 10 people to deliver an average payload of 150 tons to the camp.

Fact #10. Adelie Land is a popular tourist destination

Tourism in Antarctica is becoming more and more popular over the years. Sea tours started in the 1960s. Air flights started in the 1970s. Most part of visitors comes here by ship cruises during the summer season, which lasts from November to March.
