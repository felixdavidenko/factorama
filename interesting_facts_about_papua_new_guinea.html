Interesting facts about Papua New Guinea

Interesting facts about Papua New Guinea presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Papua New Guinea is a country in the Southwest Pacific Ocean, occupying the eastern part of the island of New Guinea and several islands nearby. It's famous for its diverse flora, fauna, and coral reefs with a huge variety of fish. The interior of the country is home to granite Wilhelm Mountain and dense rainforests. Here are a lot of villages of local tribal communities, many of which use their own languages. The country is also famous for the Sing-sing gathering, tropical snowfalls, a single UNESCO World Heritage Site, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Papua New Guinea for children, adults, the elderly, and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Papua New Guinea is one of the world's most rural countries

The first one of interesting facts about Papua New Guinea regards its low urbanization. This country is known as one of the most rural in the world. With a total population of almost 9 million people, only about 13% of them are living in urban areas.

Fact #2. Bird-of-paradise is the national symbol of Papua New Guinea

The Raggiana bird-of-paradise, also known as Kumul or Cenderawasih is a large bird endemic to Papua New Guinea. In 1971 this bird has become the national symbol of the country and since then has been depicted both on the national emblem and the national flag.

Fact #3. Papua New Guinea occupies the Bird's Tail Peninsula

The next one of fun facts about Papua New Guinea regards its shape. The island of New Guinea is considered to be in the shape of a bird. Thus, the island is divided into the Bird's Head Peninsula in the northwest and the Bird's Tail Peninsula in the southeast.

Fact #4. The only World Heritage Site in Papua New Guinea is a former swamp

The Kuk Swamp, located in the Waghi Valley is archaeological evidence of the ancient agricultural drainage systems, used in the cultivation of bananas, sugar cane, taro, and some other crops. In 2008 the Kuk Swamp became a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Fact #5. In Papua New Guinea you can buy a bride for seashells

Here's the next one of interesting facts about Papua New Guinea. A seashell was once a country's currency, but since 1933 it's not. However, in some cultures, there's a wedding ritual when a groom must bring a certain amount of seashells as a bride price.

Fact #6. Papua New Guinea was once ruled by the UK and Germany

In 1884, the southern part of the country controlled by the UK was named "The Territory of Papua". The northern part of the country controlled by Germany was named "German New Guinea". That's why today the country is known as "Papua New Guinea".

Fact #7. The traditional Papuan drum is partially made of snakes

Another one of fun facts about Papua New Guinea regards Kundu. It's a traditional Papuan drum. The size of such a drum can vary from the palm-size to the human-size. What's curious, it's made of wood but its membrane is made of snake's or lizard's skin.

Fact #8. Papua New Guinea has a popular drink made of roots used in herbal medicine

Kava is one of the most popular non-alcoholic drinks in Papua New Guinea. First of all, kava is a local crop, often used in herbal medicine. The drink is popular because it's made of roots that cause sedative, anesthetic, and even euphoria effects.

Fact #9. The climate in Papua New Guinea is frosty-hot

The next one of interesting facts about Papua New Guinea regards climate. The country's climate is tropical but very diverse. In lowlands, the average temperature can reach 32 deg C (90 deg F) but in the highlands, it can be as low as 15 deg C (59 deg F).

Fact #10. The New Guinea crocodile can be found nowhere else except here

The New Guinea crocodile is a small species of a crocodile named after the world's only habitat where it was discovered. It is much smaller than the saltwater crocodile that can also be found here. Its natural habitat is a swamp. Its hunting time is night.
