Interesting facts about Algeria

Interesting facts about Algeria presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. Algeria is a state in North Africa located between the Mediterranean Sea and the Sahara Desert. Various empires have left their mark here. Roman ruins can be seen in the coastal town of Tipaza. In the capital of the country, the city of Algiers, there are monuments from the Ottoman Empire, such as the Ketshava Mosque of 1612 in the Kasbah mountain quarter with narrow streets and stairs. The Neo-Byzantine-style Cathedral of Our Lady of Africa reminds that there was once a French colony here. The country is also famous for the great eastern sea of sand, the national symbol deflecting the evil eye, the mosque with the world's tallest minaret and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Algeria.

Fact #1. Algeria is the tenth-largest country in the world

The first one of interesting facts about Algeria regards its territory. With an area of 2,381,741 sq km (919,595 sq mi), Algeria is the tenth-largest country in the world. Also this country is the largest by area in the African Union and in the Arab world.

Fact #2. One of the tallest bridges in the world is located in Algeria

The Sidi Rached Viaduct is a road viaduct that crosses the Rhummel gorges in Constantine, Algeria. Its length is 450 m (1,480 ft) with 27 arches including 1 of 70 m (230 ft). It was inaugurated in 1912 and at that moment, it was the tallest bridge in the world.

Fact #3. One of the best preserved Roman ruins in North Africa is located in Algeria

Here's the next one of interesting facts about Algeria. Djemila is a village famous for some of the best preserved Roman ruins in North Africa, including a theatre, foras, temples, arches, basilicas and not only. In 1982, Djemila became a World Heritage Site.

Fact #4. The traditional Algerian wedding dress is an intangible heritage of humanity

The chedda of Tlemcen is a traditional Algerian dress in velvet and gold thread, decorated with pearls, necklaces and meskia. This is the country’s most expensive wedding dress. In 2012 it was inscribed in the list of the intangible heritage of humanity.

Fact #5. There is a mountain range in Algeria named after the nomads

The next one of fun facts about Algeria regards the Ouled Nail mountain range. The range is named after a confederation of nomadic and semi-nomadic tribes. All of these tribes claim to hail from Arab ancestors that arrived in the mountains in the 11th century.

Fact #6. Algeria is home to more than 220 artiodactyl species

There are more than 220 artiodactyl species in Algeria, many of which are of great economic importance to humans. Among the most distinctive are vulnerable barbary sheep, endangered rhim gazelle, critically endangered dama gazelle and not only.

Fact #7. The name of Algeria is derived from the name of its capital

Another one of interesting facts about Algeria regards its name. The People's Democratic Republic of Algeria is named after the city of Algiers which in turn derives from the Arabic al-Jaza'ir (The Islands), employed by medieval geographers, including al-Idrisi.

Fact #8. Algeria is one of the top-10 countries in the world by the amount of natural gas reserves

Algeria has the 10th largest reserves of natural gas in the world. This country is also the 6th largest gas exporter in the world. According to the U.S. Energy Information Administration, Algeria has about 4,5 trillion cubic meters of proven natural gas reserves.

Fact #9. Algeria has its own Triumphal Arch

Here goes the next one of fun facts about Algeria. The Arch of Trajan is a triumphal arch in the city of Timgad. It's a 12 m (39,3 ft) tall arch built as far as 100 AD. It's one of the most popular tourist spots that in 1982 was inscribed in the World Heritage List.

Fact #10. Couscous is the national dish of Algeria

Couscous is a North African dish of crushed durum wheat semolina that is often cooked in a spicy or mild broth or stew and generally served with vegs (carrots, potatoes, turnips) and some meat (chicken, lamb, mutton). It is the national dish of Algeria.
