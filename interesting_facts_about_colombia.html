Interesting facts about Colombia

Interesting facts about Colombia presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, culture, economy, science, nature, sports, and of course people. Colombia is a country in the northwest of South America, shaped by tropical forests, the Andes Mountains, and numerous coffee plantations. The country’s capital Bogota is famous for its colorful and vivid murals. Cartagena on the Caribbean coast is one of the most visited tourist destinations in Colombia, famous for its numerous landmarks and the oldest film festival in Latin America. The country is also famous for its rich wildlife, sweet alcohol, passionate salsa, diverse food, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Colombia for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Colombia has over 300 beaches on 2 coastlines

The first one of fun facts about Colombia regards its unique location. It’s the only country on the South American continent that has coastlines both on the Caribbean Sea and the Pacific Ocean. The number of beaches on both sides of the country exceeds 300.

Fact #2. Colombia had different names at different times

Colombia once was the New Kingdom of Granada (since 1499); the United Provinces of New Granada (since 1810); the Grenadine Confederation (since 1858); the United States of Colombia (since 1863) and finally, the Republic of Colombia (since 1886).

Fact #3. Colombia is one of the world’s most biodiverse countries

Here goes the next one of interesting facts about Colombia. This country is one of the Top-3 world's most biodiverse countries. To be precise, it keeps the second position after only Brazil. Colombia also has the world's highest number of anteaters.

Fact #4. Colombia is home to the river, known as the Liquid Rainbow

In Colombia, there is a multi-colored river called Cano Cristales, also known as the "River of five colors" or the "Liquid Rainbow". Beginning at the end of July and through November, the river's bed changes color between yellow, red, green, blue, and even black.

Fact #5. Colombia is one of the world’s leading countries by the number of holidays

So, the next one of fun facts about Colombia is that this country has a lot of holidays. To be precise, there are 18 national holidays throughout the year. By this number, Colombia is the second country in the world after India, with its 21 national holidays.

Fact #6. Graffiti in Colombia is not just permitted but also appreciated

The capital of Colombia - Bogota is a Mecca for those who love graffiti. And what's curious, instead of getting rid of street artists, the government tolerates their activity and even encourages it. That’s the reason why there are so many beautiful murals.

Fact #7. The most consumed dish for the breakfast in Colombia is a milk soup

Another one of interesting facts about Colombia regards one of its dishes. The dish is called Changua. It’s a milk soup with an undercooked egg. Served in a bowl and garnished with scallion, this dish is the most consumed for the breakfast in this country.

Fact #8. “One hundred years of solitude” is a world bestseller written by Colombian

Colombia gave the world one of the most talented writers of all time. Gabriel Garcia Marquez in 1982 was awarded the Nobel Prize in literature. One of his most significant novels "One hundred years of solitude" has been translated into 37 languages.

Fact #9. Colombia is the world’s leading producer of emeralds

The next one of fun facts about Colombia regards gemstones. This country is the world's leading producer of emeralds. It’s well known, that up to date the finest quality emerald ever was found right here, in Colombia, in one of more than 150 mines.

Fact #10. Cartagena is one of the most visited cities in Colombia

The most beloved Colombian city by tourists is Cartagena. The most eye-catching sites here are Plaza de la Aduana, Palacio de la Inquisicion, Convento de la Popa, Castillo de San Felipe, the Clock Tower, and this is not a full list of Cartagena's landmarks.
