Interesting facts about Spain

Interesting facts about Spain presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science and of course people. Spain is a European country located on the Iberian Peninsula. The territory of Spain is divided into 17 autonomous regions. In the capital of the country, Madrid, there is the Royal Palace and the Prado Museum, which houses works of European masters. In Segovia, you can visit the medieval castle (Alcazar) and see the well-preserved Roman aqueduct. Barcelona is the capital of the autonomous community of Catalonia. The appearance of this city is determined by the numerous fanciful creations of the architect Antoni Gaudi, including the world famous Sagrada Familia. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Spain for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. One of the world famous landmarks of Spain is still unfinished

The first one of fun facts about Spain regards the Sagrada Familia. It’s a Roman Catholic basilica, designed by Antoni Gaudi. At the time of his death (in 1926) it was only a quarter ready. Its construction continues and is expected to be finished by 2026.

Fact #2. The cultural symbol of Spain originated as an advertising image

The Osborne Bulls are silhouetted images of bulls erected by the Osborne Company in 1956 all over Spain to advertise their brandy. In 1994 alco-ads were prohibited but bulls were left standing. Soon, they have become a cultural symbol of the country.

Fact #3. Spain is the world's third country by the number of UNESCO World Heritage Sites

The next one interesting fact about Spain regards UNESCO World Heritage Sites. There are 48 such sites in Spain. 42 are cultural sites, 4 are natural and 2 are mixed. By the number of heritage sites, Spain is third country in the world after China (55) and Italy (55).

Fact #4. The national dish of Spain, in fact, is not national

Paella is a rice dish, originated in Valencia, Spain. What's curious is that most non-Spaniards consider it a national dish of Spain, while Spaniards themselves know that this dish is not national but regional. Valencians believe it to be their identifying symbol.

Fact #5. Spain is the only European country to have a physical border with Africa

The next one of fun facts about Spain regards its exclaves. There are three Spanish exclaves, located on the African continent. These are Ceuta, Melilla and Penon de Velez de la Gomera. Thus, Spain is the only European country to border with Africa.

Fact #6. Spain is one of the most biodiverse European countries

Spain has about 60 to 70 thousand species of animals including those that are endemic only to this country, such as the Iberian ibex. It's a species of ibex consisting of four subspecies. Of these four subspecies, two can still be found and two are now extinct.

Fact #7. One of the world famous party destinations is located in Spain

Another one of fun facts about Spain regards the Balearic Islands. It’s an archipelago, located in the Mediterranean Sea. It consists of Mallorca, Menorca, Formentera and Ibiza. The latter is a world famous party destination, along with Goa in India.

Fact #8. The most popular sport in Spain is football

Football has been popular in Spain since the beginning of the 20th century. The national football team won the UEFA Championship several times, in 1964, 2008 and 2012. Both FC Barcelona and Real Madrid are considered the world's most successful clubs.

Fact #9. Spanish is the world's second most spoken native language

The next one of interesting facts about Spain regards its language. Spanish language originated in the Iberian Peninsula of Europe. Today, it's a global language with over 483 million native speakers all over the world, mainly in Spain and Latin America.

Fact #10. Almost 30% of the territory of Spain is parks and reserves

In association with a European Union initiative, 27% of the territory of Spain is national parks and wildlife reserves. The country has almost 9,000 species of plants. Mainland Spain has 820 endemic species of plants and the Balearic Islands have about 100.
