Interesting facts about Italy

Interesting facts about Italy presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science and of course people. Italy is a Mediterranean country in southern Europe with a long coastline that has had a tremendous impact on Western culture and cuisine. The capital of the country, Rome, is home to the Vatican, as well as world-famous museums and ancient ruins. Other major cities in Italy are Florence, where you can see masterpieces of the Renaissance, such as the statue of David by Michelangelo and the Duomo, designed by the architect Brunelleschi, as well as the city of canals, Venice and the fashion capital Milan. Italy is also known for its supercars, landmarks, cuisine and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Italy for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Italy is world famous for its explorers

Here’s the first one of interesting facts about Italy. The world's famous explorers Christopher Columbus, Amerigo Vespucci and Marco Polo were all Italian. The first and the second one discovered the Americas. The third one is famous for exploring the East.

Fact #2. The age of Rome is more than 2,800 years

Roman mythology dates the founding of Rome at 753 BC. Its history spans 28 centuries. In the 8th century it became the capital of the Papal States. In 1871, Rome became the capital of the Kingdom of Italy. In 1946, Rome became the Italian Republic.

Fact #3. Over 1 million Euros are thrown in the Trevi Fountain annually

The next one of fun facts about Italy regards Trevi Fountain. This fountain is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Rome. It's counted that about €3,000 of change is thrown in it daily. That's about a million Euros a year that then donated to charity.

Fact #4. There are over 34 spoken dialects in Italy

The official language in Italy is Italian (obviously). But also, there are approximately thirty four related dialects, so not even all the locals speak proper Italian. Moreover, some of the older people can't speak "Basic" or "Common" Italian, but only the local dialect.

Fact #5. The Volt unit was invented by an Italian physicist

The next one of interesting facts about Italy regards electricity. Many know that "Volt" is the derived unit for electric potential. But not many know that it's named after the Alessandro Volta, an Italian physicist, chemist and pioneer of electricity and power.

Fact #6. The Leaning Tower is not the only leaning structure in Pisa

The Leaning Tower of Pisa is the most famous, but not the only leaning structure in this city. There are also: San Nicola church (located half a mile south) and San Michele degli Scalzi church (two miles east). The latter boasts a significant 5-degree tilt.

Fact #7. Italy is home to Europe's only three active volcanoes

Here’s another one of fun facts about Italy. The country is home to three active volcanoes. Mount Vesuvius is located 9 km (5,6 mi) east of Naples. Mount Etna is located on the east coast of Sicily. Mount Stromboli is located on an island in the Tyrrhenian Sea.

Fact #8. The most popular sport in Italy is football

Italians love different sports, but the most beloved is football. The national football team has won the FIFA World Cup four times (1934, 1938, 1982, 2006). Italians have also won the European Championship (1968) and the Olympic Gold Medal (1936).

Fact #9. The Lamborghini Company produces not only supercars, but also tractors

The next one of interesting facts about Italy regards tractors. The world's famous Lamborghini brand (established in 1963) is known to be supercars Manufacturer Company. But few people know that the company also produces tractors (since 1948).

Fact #10. The Grey Wolf is considered a national animal of Italy

There are a lot of debates held in Italy about the official national animal. Despite having no official national animal, the unofficial symbol of the country is the Grey Wolf. It's also known as the Apennine Wolf that lives in the Italian Mountains.
