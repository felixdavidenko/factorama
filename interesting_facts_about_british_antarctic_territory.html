Interesting facts about British Antarctic Territory

Interesting facts about British Antarctic Territory presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of this territory, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, culture, economy, science, nature, and of course people. The British Antarctic Territory, also called BAT is a sector of the Antarctic continent claimed by the United Kingdom. This territory includes the Antarctic Peninsula, the South Shetland Islands, South Orkney Islands and some other smaller islands. The British Antarctic Territory is home to the world's fastest swimming penguin, the endemic flowering plant, two research stations, a couple thousand starfish and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about the British Antarctic Territory for children, adults, the elderly, and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Antarctica is divided between several countries including the UK

Here's the first one of interesting facts about the British Antarctic Territory. While many consider Antarctica as a no man's land, in fact, its territory is divided between several countries. The British Antarctic Territory is claimed by the United Kingdom.

Fact #2. The British Antarctic Territory has a native flowering plant

The British Antarctic Territory has very poor vegetation, but at the same time it's a place of growth of the Antarctic pearlwort. It's a flowering plant, native to this Antarctic region. It grows up to 5 cm (2 in) tall, and looks like bush with yellow flowers.

Fact #3. The disputed Antarctic Peninsula is part of the British Antarctic Territory

The next one of fun facts about the British Antarctic Territory regards the Antarctic Peninsula. Despite being part of the BAT, it is actually a subject of dispute between Argentina, Chile and the UK. Though, all these claims have no international recognition.

Fact #4. The British Antarctic Territory is home to the fastest swimming penguins

The British Antarctic Territory is home to the Gentoo penguin species. On average these penguins grow up to 90 cm (35 in) and reach a max weight of 8,5 kg (19 lb). These are the world's fastest swimming penguins that can reach the speed of 36 km/h (22 mph).

Fact #5. The British Antarctic Territory's islands are no man's islands

Here goes the next one of interesting facts about the British Antarctic Territory. The South Shetland Islands are claimed by the UK. But according to the Antarctic Treaty, their sovereignty is neither disputed nor recognized. So they are free for use by any country.

Fact #6. The British Antarctic Territory is inhabited by less than 200 people

The British Antarctic Territory has two permanently staffed research stations. The Rothera Research Station is staffed with 100 people. The Halley Research Station is staffed with 70 people. These are summer numbers, which in winter are significantly lower.

Fact #7. The British Antarctic Territory is densely populated with starfish

Here's another one of fun facts about the British Antarctic Territory. The South Orkney Islands in the Southern Ocean are a natural habitat for starfish colonies. With increasing water depth, the number of starfish increases from several ones to thousands.

Fact #8. The British Antarctic Territory is a popular tourist destination

The Goudier Island which is part of the British Antarctic Territory is home to the historic base at Port Lockroy. This base, staffed by the UK Antarctic Heritage Trust is one of the most visited sites on the whole continent. It receives more than 10,000 tourists per year.

Fact #9. The British Antarctic Territory is home to the A-38 iceberg

Another one of interesting facts about the British Antarctic Territory regards its Filcher-Ronne Ice Shelf. This shelf became famous in October 1998, when the huge A-38 iceberg broke off of it. The size of this iceberg was 150 km (93 mi) by 50 km (31 mi).

Fact #10. Part of the British Antarctic Territory is named after Queen Elizabeth

The total area of the British Antarctic Territory is 1,709,400 sq km (660,000 sq mi). In 2012, a 437,000 sq km (169,000 sq mi) triangular section of this territory in the south was named Queen Elizabeth Land in order to honor her Diamond Jubilee.
