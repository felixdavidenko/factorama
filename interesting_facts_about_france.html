Interesting facts about France

Interesting facts about France presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science and of course people. France is a country in Western Europe, which is home to medieval towns, alpine villages, and the beaches of the Mediterranean. Paris, the capital of the state, is famous for its fashion houses, the oldest art museums, including the Louvre, and landmarks such as the Eiffel Tower and the Arc de Triomphe. France hosts the Tour de France and the 24 Hours of Le Mans races. The country is home to Notre Dame, the Mont Blanc, the Avenue des Champs-Elysees and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about France for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. France is the world’s most visited country

The first one of interesting facts about France regards tourism. The world's most popular tourist destination is France, Europe. It’s counted that more than 710 million tourists arrive in Europe annually. The most number - 89,4 million goes to France.

Fact #2. The Eiffel Tower is one of the world’s most recognizable landmarks

For the vast majority of people all around the world, the Eiffel Tower is a symbol of France. Since France is the world's most visited country, it's not a surprise that this is the country's most visited landmark. The tower attracts more than 7 million visitors per year.

Fact #3. France produces nearly 1,200 different varieties of cheese

Another one of fun facts about France regards its cheese traditions. The found records about goats’ cheese are dating about 500 AD. The ancient records about Roquefort cheese are date 1070 and the records about Emmental cheese are dating 1290.

Fact #4. The 24 Hours of Le Mans race is held not on the track, but on public roads

The world-famous motorsport endurance race takes place on The Circuit de la Sarthe in Le Mans, France. Its length is 13,626 km (8,467 mi). The circuit consists of race-specific sections combined with public roads, available for public use throughout the year.

Fact #5. There are a lot of Triumphal Arches around the world

The next one of interesting facts about France regards triumphal arches. The fact is that The Arc de Triomphe in Paris is not the one and only. There are a lot of them around the world - in Moscow, London, Rome, Bucharest, Delhi, Pyongyang and not only.

Fact #6. France holds the record for most Nobel Prizes won in literature

France gave the world some of the most influential writers and thinkers. Among them: Descartes in the 17th century; Voltaire in the 18th; Flaubert in the 19th; Sartre in the 20th. France holds the record for the most number (15) of Nobel Prizes won in Literature.

Fact #7. Le Tour de France is the world’s most prestigious cycling competition

Another one of fun facts about France regards the Tour de France. There are 180 riders, competing in 21 stages and covering 3,500 km (2,200 mi). What’s curious is that among the pro-riders, winning this race is more valued than winning the Olympic Gold medal.

Fact #8. The highest peak of the Alps is located in France

The Alps are known to be Europe's highest and most extensive mountain range system. The Alps are stretching through several European countries, but the highest peak - Mont Blanc is located in France. The mountain is 4,808 meters (15,774 feet) high.

Fact #9. Almost 8 million people in France do not use French

The next one of interesting facts about France regards language. For 88% of citizens French is the first language. But almost 12% (8 million people) speak other dialects and languages, such as Alsacian, Basque, Breton, Catalan, Occitan, and Flemish.

Fact #10. The Michelin Guide was initially intended not for gourmets but for motorists

The world-famous Michelin Guide was initially a guide not on restaurants, but on gas stations, repair shops, hotels, and cafes. The idea of this guide, created by the tire company Michelin, was to increase the demand for cars by encouraging people to travel.
