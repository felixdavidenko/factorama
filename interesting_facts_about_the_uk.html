Interesting facts about the United Kingdom

Interesting facts about the United Kingdom presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science and of course people. Great Britain (officially the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland) is an island state in northwestern Europe, consisting of England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland. In England, the homeland of Shakespeare and the Beatles is the capital of the state London - the world business and cultural center. The country is also known for its Neolithic Stonehenge, the world's oldest subway system, universities in Oxford and Cambridge, and not only. Fact #2. Big Ben in fact is not a tower

Big Ben is one of the world's most recognizable landmarks of the UK. But most people think that Big Ben is a tower. In fact, Big Ben is the nickname for the Great Bell of the striking clock. In turn, the official name of the tower, housing Big Ben is the Clock Tower.

Fact #3. The world's oldest metro system is located in the UK

Here goes another one of interesting facts about the United Kingdom. The London Underground, also known as the Tube is the oldest metro system in the world. Opened in 1863, today, it has 270 stations, 400 km (250 mi) of the route, and carries 5 million passengers a day.

Fact #4. English language in the UK has 40 different dialects

English is de facto the official language in the UK. It's spoken by 59,8 million citizens or 98% of the population. However, there are almost 40 different dialects of this language in the UK. So even using the same language, two may not understand each other.

Fact #5. The annual amount of tea consumed in the UK is over 36 billion cups

The next one of fun facts about the United Kingdom regards tea. Tea plays an important role in the country's cultural tradition. It's counted that on average, almost 55,9 million citizens drink 1,78 cups of tea a day. That's about 99,6 million cups a day and 36 billion a year.

Fact #6. The UK is home to one of the world's busiest airports

Heathrow Airport located 23 km (14 mi) west of Central London is the busiest airport in Europe by passenger traffic and the world's second busiest airport by international passenger traffic. In 2019 it carried a recording 80,8 million passengers on 475,861 flights.

Fact #7. The Wimbledon tennis tournament is the oldest in the world

Another one of interesting facts about the United Kingdom regards tennis. Wimbledon is the world's oldest and the most prestigious tennis tournament played at the All England Club, London. The tournament was founded in 1877 and is played outdoors on grass courts.

Fact #8. Sir Winston Churchill earned fame escaping the prison

In 1899, Churchill sailed to Africa to serve as a war correspondent for the Morning Post. In October, on the way to the conflict zone, his train was besieged and he was imprisoned. In December, he escaped the prison, made it to safety, and earned instant fame.

Fact #9. In the UK you can actually buy a telephone booth

The next one of fun facts about the United Kingdom regards telephone boxes. Today, because of mobile phones many of those old phone booths became redundant. But according to "Adopt a kiosk" scheme, anyone can buy one and make it useful for the community.

Fact #10. The UK is home to the world's only rotating boat lift

The Falkirk Wheel is a rotating boat lift located in Falkirk, Scotland. The wheel is named after the town and connects the Forth and Clyde Canal with the Union Canal. Its height is 35 m (115 ft), and each of its gondolas carries some 300 tons of water.
