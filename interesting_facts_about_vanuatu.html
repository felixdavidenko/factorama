Interesting facts about Vanuatu

Interesting facts about Vanuatu presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Vanuatu is a state in the South Pacific Ocean, located on a chain of 80 islands, stretching for 1,300 kilometers (807 miles). The islands are popular among scuba divers for their coral reefs, underwater caves and wrecks, including the President Coolidge troop carrier, wrecked during World War II. Vanuatu is also famous for its cap shaped island, endemic birds, cargo cult and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Vanuatu for children, adults, the elderly, and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Vanuatu was once called Australia

Here goes the first one of fun facts about Vanuatu. The first Europeans to visit the archipelago were a Spanish expedition led by navigator Fernandes de Queiros. He claimed the archipelago for Spain and named the country "La Australia del Espiritu Santo".

The history of Vanuatu before the arrival of Europeans is almost obscure because of the lack of convincing written evidence. However, archaeological evidence gathered since the 1980s promotes the theory that Vanuatu was inhabited some 3,000 years ago.

Fact #3. There is a church in Vanuatu dedicated to the sacred heart of Jesus

Another one of interesting facts about Vanuatu regards the Cathedral of the Sacred Heart which is a modern Roman Catholic cathedral in Port Vila. The Church is dedicated to the sacred Heart of Jesus and is the seat of the local Roman Catholic Diocese.

Fact #4. There is a cave in Vanuatu with 3,000 years old rock paintings

Fels Cave, located on the island of Lelepa, is a very popular tourist attraction famous for having numerous rock drawings of up to 3,000 years old. Since 2008, the cave has been part of Chief Roi Mata's Domain, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Fact #5. Vanuatu is home to 15 endemic bird species

The next one of fun facts about Vanuatu regards birds. It's known that there are 79 native bird species in Vanuatu, 15 of which are endemic. Among the most famous are the buff-bellied monarch, vanikoro flycatcher, yellow-fronted white-eye and not only.

Fact #6. There is a cap shaped grave island in Vanuatu

Eretoka is a small island in Shefa Province of Vanuatu. The center of the island forms a cap of coral lime and is 90 m (295 ft) high, while the "brim" is no higher than 5 m (16 ft) high. A powerful 16th-17th century chief Roy Mata was buried on the island.

Fact #7. The number of Americans in Vanuatu was once larger than the number of natives

Here's another one of interesting facts about Vanuatu. During the Second World War US troops were stationed on the islands. At the peak of the deployment, there were about 50,000 Americans, outnumbering the country's population of about 40,000.

Fact #8. The national dish in Vanuatu is often made of a bat

Laplap is the national dish of Vanuatu, usually made by grating breadfruit, bananas and yam roots into a vegetable paste. The paste is then wrapped in banana leaves and cooked in an oven. Laplap can be added with meats like beef, pork, chicken or a bat.

Fact #9. Only 1% of the land of Vanuatu is arable

Here's the next one of fun facts about Vanuatu. The total area of Vanuatu is 12,274 sq km (4,739 sq mi) of which the land area is roughly 4,700 sq km (1,800 sq mi). It's estimated that only 1,64% of the country's land is arable and 10,25% is devoted to crops.

Fact #10. A cargo cult originated in Vanuatu

A cargo cult is a belief system in which adherents perform various rituals which as they believe will bring them supplies. It gained popularity in the 1940s, after American troops were stationed in Vanuatu and supplied with an enormous amount of cargo.
