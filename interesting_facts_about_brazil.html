Interesting facts about Brazil

Interesting facts about Brazil presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Brazil is a country in South America that extends from the Amazon River basin in the north to the vineyards and huge Iguazu Falls in the south. The city of Rio de Janeiro, symbolized by the 38-meter-high statue of Christ the Redeemer on the top of Mount Corcovado is famous for its lively Copacabana and Ipanema beaches, as well as for its large-scale carnival and not only. The country is also famous for its rich wildlife, vivid favelas, numerous airports, UNESCO World Heritage Sites, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Brazil for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Brazil is a venue for the world’s biggest festival

Here’s the first one of fun facts about Brazil. The world's most famous Brazilian Carnival in Rio de Janeiro is officially registered in the Guinness Book as the biggest in the world. For locals, it’s the most important and the most expensive event of the year.

Fact #2. One of the world’s famous beaches is located in Brazil

The Copacabana beach in Rio is known for many reasons. Because it’s the longest (4 km / 2,5 mi) city beach, because of its famous promenade with calçadão pattern, and because it’s a place of the most famous New Year's Eve beach party with fireworks.

Fact #3. Favelas are one of the world-renowned symbols of Brazil

The next one of interesting facts about Brazil regards its colorful shantytowns, called "Favelas". One of the most visited of such towns is Santa Marta located in Rio de Janeiro. This place is known to be visited by Madonna, Beyonce, and Michael Jackson.

Fact #4. The highest point of Brazil is located in the restricted area

The highest mountain in Brazil is the Pico da Neblina, located near the border with Venezuela. Its height is almost 3,000 meters (9,823 ft). Because of its location within the Pico da Neblina National Park, access to the mountain is allowed only with a special permit.

Fact #5. A large and rare Lear's macaw parrot is endemic only to Brazil

Here's the next one of interesting facts about Brazil. Lear's macaw, often called the Indigo macaw is a large parrot endemic only to Brazil. This rare bird of 75 cm (30 in) in height and 950 g (2 lb) in weight is endangered and accounts for about 1,000 individuals.

Fact #6. Over 1 million tourists visit the underground Brazilian cathedral

One of the most visited landmarks in Brazil is the Cathedral of Brasilia. It consists of 16 columns and a 20 meter (66 ft) bell tower. Most of the cathedral is located below the ground so visitors are able to enter it only through the underground tunnel.

Fact #7. Brazil has the world’s most recognizable motto

Another one of fun facts about Brazil regards its national motto - "Ordem e Progresso". The fact is that it’s located directly on the flag, so it turns out that thanks to such an original solution, not only the flag itself but also the motto is both world-famous.

Fact #8. The largest river in the world is located in Brazil

Brazil is home to the Amazon River which is known as the world's largest. Per one second it discharges almost 210,000 cubic meters of water into the Atlantic Ocean. It also has the world's largest drainage basin with an area of 7,050,000 sq km (2,720,000 sq mi).

Fact #9. Brazil is one of the world’s largest coffee exporters

The next one of interesting facts about Brazil is that this country for more than 150 years has been the world's largest coffee exporter. Nowadays this figure has fallen a little, but anyway, it's highly likely that your morning coffee was made of Brazilian beans.

Fact #10. The world’s symbol of Christianity is located in Brazil

The statue of Christ the Redeemer is located in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Built in 1931 of soapstone, it has a height of 38 m (98 ft) and weighs 635 tons. This statue is a world's symbol of Christianity and is considered one of the "New 7 Wonders of the World".
