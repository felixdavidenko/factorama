Interesting facts about Samoa

Interesting facts about Samoa presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the state, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. Samoa is a state in Polynesia, occupying the western part of the archipelago of the same name. Many Samoan islands are famous for their reef-fringed beaches and rainforest hinterland with canyons and waterfalls. Among them are Upolu, where most of the country's population lives, and Savaii, one of the largest islands in the South Pacific. The state is also famous for the mother temple of the pacific islands, one of the world's top spots for surfing, crab-eating macaques and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Samoa.

Fact #1. Samoa was once referred to as the navigators land

Here goes the first one of fun facts about Samoa. Because of the Samoans' highly developed seafaring skills, pre-20th-century European explorers often referred to the entire island group (including the American Samoa) as the "Navigator islands".

Fact #2. Samoa was inhabited as far as 3000 years ago

Samoa was discovered and settled by the Samoans' Lapita ancestors. New Zealand scientists have dated the earliest human remains found in Samoa to between roughly 2900 and 3500 years ago. The scientists' findings were published in 1974.

Fact #3. The cave pool is one of the most popular swimming spots in Samoa

The next one of fun facts about Samoa regards Piula Cave Pool. It's a natural freshwater pool beneath the historic Methodist Chapel at Piula. Formed by a natural spring flowing out of a cave, it's a popular swimming spot for both foreigners and locals.

Fact #4. The national symbol of Samoa is an endangered pigeon

The tooth-billed pigeon is a large pigeon found only in Samoa. It's a medium-sized 31 cm (12 in) long dark bird with reddish feet and red bare skin around the eye. It's the national bird of Samoa inscribed in the IUCN Red List because of ongoing habitat loss.

Fact #5. Samoa has its own traditional type of a hut

Here's another one of interesting facts about Samoa. A beach fale is a simple thatched hut in Samoa. The word fale is the local word for any type of building. Beach fale are usually located along the coast, owned by local families and rented out for extra income.

Fact #6. There is a village of international importance in Samoa

Uafato is a village on the north east coast of Upolu Island in Samoa which is a unique cultural and conservation area. The village is a centre for traditional wood carving where visitors can watch carvers making bowls, war clubs and other local crafts.

Fact #7. There is an inhabited rim of an extinct volcano in Samoa

The next one of fun facts about Samoa regards Apolima which is a rim of an extinct volcanic crater and the smallest of the four inhabited islands of the country. This island has one village settlement, called Apolima Tai, with a total population of 75.

Fact #8. One of the most important customs in Samoa is ritual drinking

The 'ava ceremony is a solemn ritual in which a ceremonial beverage is shared to mark different important occasions in Samoan society. One of the most important occasions for the ceremony is during the bestowal of matai chiefly titles.

Fact #9. One of the world's top surfing destinations is located in Samoa

Another one of interesting facts about Samoa regards surfing. Aganoa Beach located on the east coast of Savai'i island is renowned for its incredible waves. The waters off the beach were the venue for the surfing competition at the 2007 South Pacific Games.

Fact #10. There is a nexus between two worlds in Samoa

The Fagaloa Bay – Uafato Tiavea Conservation Zone located on eastern Upolu Island is a nexus between the natural and cultural worlds: the tropical rainforest and local Samoan culture. It was inscribed in the UNESCO’s Tentative List in 2006.
