Interesting facts about Canada

Interesting facts about Canada presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Canada is a North American country stretching from the US border in the south to the Arctic Circle in the north. The largest cities are populous Toronto, the center of the film industry Vancouver located on the west coast, French-speaking Montreal and Quebec, and the capital of the country Ottawa. A large part of Canada is occupied by areas with wildlife, including the Banff National Park in the Rocky Mountains with many lakes. This is the country of ice hockey, maple syrup, Niagara Falls, grizzly bear, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Canada for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. The maple leaf is the most widely recognized national symbol of Canada

The first one of fun facts about Canada regards the maple leaf. It was chosen as a national symbol as far as in the early 18th Century. Described as "The king of the forest", the leaf represents its different tree species native to every Canadian province.

Fact #2. Canada was named so because of a misunderstanding

It is believed that when a French explorer Jacques Cartier came to the new world and met natives, there was small talk between them. They invited him to their "Kanata", which means "Village". But the explorer assumed that it was the name of the country.

Fact #3. The largest Canadian city is not the country’s capital

The next one of interesting facts about Canada regards its capital. The largest Canadian city is Toronto (pop. 2,731,571). Because of that, foreigners often consider it a country's capital. But it's not. In fact, the country’s capital is Ottawa (pop. 934,243).

Fact #4. The Rocky Mountaineer is the only all-daylight journey train in the world

The famous Canadian journey-train Rocky Mountaineer stops overnight in each destination. Thanks to this, travelers don't miss any detail of the picturesque landscape and wildlife. This is the world's only journey-specific train to offer such an opportunity.

Fact #5. Canada holds the record for most Olympic gold medals won

Another one of fun facts about Canada regards the Olympic Games. Canada holds the record for the most number of gold medals ever won during the Winter Olympics. The record of 14 Gold medals was set at the 2010 Winter Olympic Games in Vancouver.

Fact #6. Winnie the Pooh was a real Canadian bear

It is known that in 1915, a bear cub named Winnipeg was shipped from Canada to the London Zoo. In London, there was a boy - Christopher Robin Milne, who loved to visit Winnie. His love inspired his father A. A. Milne to write the book "Winnie-the-Pooh".

Fact #7. There are more than 30,000 lakes in Canada

Here goes another one of interesting facts about Canada. The country is home to 31,752 lakes (with an area larger than 3 sq km). 560 of these lakes have a surface area larger than 100 sq km (38,6 sq mi). Almost 9% of the country’s area is covered with lakes.

Fact #8. The Canadian Formula 1 Grand Prix is held on the island

The annual Canadian Grand Prix of Formula One is held at the Circuit Gilles Villeneuve. The circuit in turn is situated on the Notre Dame Island in Montreal. This is a man-made island created in 10 months for Expo-67 to celebrate Canada's centennial.

Fact #9. The Royal Canadian Mounted Police started with only 9 officers

The next one of fun facts about Canada regards law and order. The Mounted Police were created in 1873 with only 9 officers. In 1920, they united with the Dominion Police and became the Royal Canadian Mounted Police, which today has about 30,000 officers.

Fact #10. Canada is home to one of the world’s oldest National Parks

Banff National Park (est. 1885) is one of the oldest in the world and the oldest in Canada. It's located in Alberta's Rocky Mountains and encompasses 6,641 sq km (2,564 sq mi) of mountainous terrain with picturesque mountains, lakes, glaciers, and ice fields.
