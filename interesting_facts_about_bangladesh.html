Interesting facts about Bangladesh

Interesting facts about Bangladesh presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports, and of course people. Bangladesh is a state in South Asia located east of India and washed by the Bay of Bengal. Bangladesh has a rich fauna and an abundance of waterways. The main rivers – Padma (Ganges), Meghna and Jamuna – form fertile plains. In the southern part, on the coast, there is the huge Sundarban mangrove forest, part of which belongs to Eastern India. This area is home to Bengal tigers. The country is also famous for its mosques, historic monuments, celebrations and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Bangladesh for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Bangladesh is one of the world's most densely populated countries

Here's the first one of fun facts about Bangladesh. The People's Republic of Bangladesh has a total population of over 164 million people. With a total area of 148,460 sq km (57,320 sq mi), it's also one of the most densely populated countries in the world.

Fact #2. The Sixty Dome Mosque in Bangladesh is a mosque but not of a sixty dome

The Sixty Dome Mosque is the country's largest mosque from the sultanate period (1204-1576). It was completed in 1459 and what's curious, it consists not of 60, but of 77 domes arranged in 7 rows of 11 and 1 dome on each corner, making the total of 81 domes.

Fact #3. Bangladesh is washed by the largest bay in the world

Here goes another one of interesting facts about Bangladesh. The south of the country is washed by the Bay of Bengal which is part of the Indian Ocean. With a total area of 2,600,000 sq km (1,000,000 sq mi) it's the world's largest water region called a bay.

Fact #4. The largest fruit of all trees is the national fruit of Bangladesh

The jackfruit is a species of tree, widely cultivated in tropical regions all around the world. It bears the largest fruit of all trees, reaching 55 kg (120 lbs) in weight, 50 cm (20 in) in diameter and 90 cm (35 in) in length. This is the national fruit of Bangladesh.

Fact #5. The longest natural sea beach in the world is located in Bangladesh

The next one of fun facts about Bangladesh regards Cox's Bazar Beach, located at Cox's Bazar. With a width of 400 m (1,300 ft) and a total length of 150 km (93 mi) it's the world's longest natural sea beach and the country's top tourist destination.

Fact #6. There is a temple in Bangladesh known as Goddess of Dhaka

Dhakeshwari National Temple is a state-owned Hindu temple in Dhaka, which is also the country's national temple, the country's largest Hindu temple and the most important Hindu place of worship. The name "Dhakeshwari" literally means "Goddess of Dhaka".

Fact #7. There is a World Heritage Site in Bangladesh, containing 4 other World Heritage Sites

Another one of interesting facts about Bangladesh regards the Sundarbans. It's a mangrove area that includes 4 UNESCO World Heritage Sites at once: Sundarbans National Park, Sundarbans West, Sundarbans South and Sundarbans East Wildlife Sanctuaries.

Fact #8. One of the most visited landmarks in Bangladesh is an unfinished fort

Lalbagh Fort located in the city of Dhaka is an unfinished 17th century fort complex. Its construction started as far as in 1678 and was never completed. Today it's one of the most popular and the most visited landmarks among both local and foreign tourists.

Fact #9. There is a social fest in Bangladesh often held on circumcision

The next one of fun facts about Bangladesh regards a social fest called Mezban. In Persian "Mezban" means host and "Mezbani" means hosting a feast for the guests. It's held on special occasions, such as buying a new house, marriage or even circumcision.

Fact #10. There is a broken brick wall memorial in Bangladesh

Martyred Intellectuals Memorial in Dhaka is built in memory of the victims of the Bangladesh Liberation War of 1971. Its main element is a 17,7 m (58 ft) high, 0,9 m (3 ft) thick and 115,8 m (380 ft) long wall, broken at two ends to represent grief and sorrow.
