Interesting facts about India

Interesting facts about India presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports, and of course people. India is a huge country in South Asia with a diverse relief: both the mountain peaks of the Himalayas and the coast of the Indian Ocean are located on its territory. The country's history goes back five millennia. In northern India, there are architectural monuments of the Mughal era, including the Red Fort, the Jami Masjid, and the Taj Mahal. The city of Varanasi is known as a place of pilgrimage for Hindus who bathe in the Ganges, and Rishikesh is known as a yoga center and starting point for trekking in the Himalayas. The country is also famous for its deities, spicy food, Goa, Gandhi, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about India for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. India is the world’s second most populated country

The first one of interesting facts about India regards its population. India has the second-largest number of citizens in the world after only China. The population of the country is 1,39 billion and it's estimated that in a few years India will surpass China.

Fact #2. India is the second English speaking country in the world

Due to the large number of citizens and the huge influence of England in the past, India is the world's second country by the number of English speakers. About 125 million people in India are speaking this language and the figure is growing constantly.

Fact #3. In the Indian culture, Mehendi is most often used as a wedding ritual

Here’s another one of fun facts about India. The henna painting, known as Mehendi is a wedding ritual that represents the holy bond of marriage and love between the spouses and their parents. The darker the color of the henna, the stronger their love is.

Fact #4. Taj Mahal is the most recognizable landmark in India

The Taj Mahal is one of the world's most famous Indian landmarks. This mausoleum built in 1653 is located in the Indian city of Agra. In 1983 it was inscribed in the UNESCO World Heritage List. Today, the annual number of its visitors exceeds 8 million people.

Fact #5. The Indian city of Varanasi was founded about 5,000 years ago

The next one of interesting facts about India regards Varanasi. It is believed that this city is one of the oldest inhabited places in the world. Due to Indian Mythology, the city of Varanasi (or Baranas) was founded by Lord Shiva about 5,000 years ago.

Fact #6. One of the most common forms of public transport in India is a tuk-tuk

India is one of the few countries in the world to use tuk-tuk for public transportation. What's curious is that this type of vehicle is also known as Auto-rickshaw. The wagon pulled by a man is called a rickshaw, but the wagon pulled by the engine is called a tuk-tuk.

Fact #7. The world's largest bust sculpture was built to inspire people towards inner well-being

Another one of interesting facts about India regards the Adiyogi statue in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu. It's a 34 m (112 ft) tall, 45 m (147 ft) long, and 25 m (82 ft) wide statue of Shiva made of steel. It was established to inspire people towards inner well-being through yoga.

Fact #8. India has many popular dishes but no national

India is known to have a lot of popular dishes, such as bhajiya, biryani, khichdi, daal, roti, and many others. But even despite that fact, there is no dish that has been declared the official national. Khichdi is the most popular dish, but it's not national.

Fact #9. Cows in India are considered sacred animals

The next one of fun facts about India regards cows. In Hinduism, it's believed that cows are sacred animals. Due to this belief, the local constitution has a number of rules that protect cows. So in India, it's illegal to slaughter cows, sell them and eat their meat.

Fact #10. India is one of the strongest countries in Field Hockey

Cricket is the most popular sport in India. Kabaddi is the most popular indigenous sport in the country. Whatever it be, field hockey is considered the most successful sport for India. The Indian men's team has won an amazing eight Olympic gold medals.
