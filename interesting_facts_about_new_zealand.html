Interesting facts about New Zealand

Interesting facts about New Zealand presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. New Zealand is a state in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, located on two large islands with volcanoes and glaciers separated by the Cook Strait. The country's capital, Wellington, is located on the North Island. The colorful nature of New Zealand – the fjords and South Lakes of the South Island and Mount Victoria, Peter Jackson captured in the film trilogy "The Lord of the Rings". The country is also famous for its endemic penguins, world's safest airlines, "Haka" war dance and a Kiwi bird but not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about New Zealand for children, adults, the elderly, and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. New Zealand consists of 2 big and 600 small islands

Here’s the first one of fun facts about New Zealand. While many know that this is an island country, few people know that in fact it consists of two islands – the North and the South ones. What's more curious is that there are also 600 smaller islands.

Fact #2. New Zealand's English is almost similar to Australian English

The predominant language in New Zealand is English, which is spoken by over 95% of population and almost similar to Australian English. The local indigenous language Maori (declared official in 1987) is spoken by approximately 4% of the population.

Fact #3. The New Zealand's Southern Alps range has 18 peaks over 3,000 m

Another one of interesting facts about New Zealand regards the Southern Alps. It’s a mountain range that sits almost all along the New Zealand's South Island. This mountain range spans about 500 km (310 mi) and includes 18 peaks over 3,000 m (9,800 ft).

Fact #4. The kiwi bird is a national symbol of New Zealand

Kiwi birds are well known all around the world. These are flightless birds endemic to New Zealand which also are the symbol of this country (first appeared on the 1886 badges). The largest species are 45 cm (18 inch) tall and weighs about 3,3 kg (7,3 lbs).

Fact #5. Local citizens in New Zealand are called Kiwis

The next one of fun facts about New Zealand regards its citizens. Locals in this country have their own distinctive nickname – Kiwis. This nickname is derived from the national bird of the same name. It's considered a symbol of pride and not offensive at all.

Fact #6. A meringue cake is considered a national dish in New Zealand

A meringue cake, also known as Pavlova has a crisp crust and soft marshmallow centre, often topped with cream and garnished with some fruits and berries. This cake is considered a symbol of national cuisine of both New Zealand and Australia.

Fact #7. The Lord of the Rings series were filmed in New Zealand

Here goes the next one of interesting facts about New Zealand. All three parts of The Lord of the Rings series were filmed in national parks and conservation areas of New Zealand. Moreover, all three parts of The Hobbit film series were also filmed here.

Fact #8. New Zealand gave the world one of the most influential rocket scientists

William "Bill" Pickering was a rocket scientist from New Zealand, who headed Jet Propulsion Lab (JPL) and was a senior NASA luminary in space exploration. His group flew Explorer 3, Pioneer, Ranger and Surveyor missions to the Moon, Venus and Mars.

Fact #9. A Maori war dance "Haka" is part of national identity of New Zealand

The next one of fun facts about New Zealand regards sports. The country's most popular sport is rugby. The national "All Blacks" team is famous not only for winning three World Cups, but also for performing a traditional Maori war dance called "Haka".

Fact #10. The silver fern tree is a national plant of New Zealand

Alsophila dealbata, also known as the silver fern is a species of fern tree, endemic to New Zealand. The average height of the plant is over 12 m (39 ft) and the trunk's diameter is up to 450 mm (17,7 in). This plant represents the country since 1880s.
