Interesting facts about Nigeria

Interesting facts about Nigeria presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Nigeria is a state in West Africa. In terms of population, it surpasses all other African countries. The area of the territory of Nigeria is 923,768 sq km (356,669 sq mi). According to this indicator, the country ranks 14th in Africa and 31st in the world. The capital – Abuja, is located in the center of the country, about 700 km (435 mi) from the sea. Until December 12, 1991, the capital was Lagos. In 2014, Nigeria, Africa's leading oil producer, became the largest African economy. The country is also known for its numerous ethnic groups and languages, film industry, space satellite, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Nigeria for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Nigeria is one of the world’s most populated countries

Here is the first one of fun facts about Nigeria. This country is the 7th most populated in the world. In 2000 it had a population of 125 million people. In 2010 it already had 160 million. And, due to the recent population census, Nigeria is home to over 206 million people.

Fact #2. There are more than 250 ethnic groups in Nigeria

Since Nigeria is a highly populated country, it's also home to a great number of ethnic groups. It's counted that there are over 250 of them. More than 70% of people belong to Hausa, Yoruba, and Igbo groups. Up to 30% belong to Edo, Ijaw, Kanuri, and others.

Fact #3. There are more than 520 spoken languages in Nigeria

Here goes another one of interesting facts about Nigeria. Because of a huge population, the number of spoken languages in Nigeria is simply unbelievable – more than 520. The official is English, but the most common are Yoruba, Ijaw, Fulfulde, Ogoni, and Edo.

Fact #4. The Twin Capital of the World is located in Nigeria

In Nigeria, there is a town of Igbo-Ora, known as the Twin Capital of the World. The town has earned such a reputation due to the high birth rate of twins. Locals suppose that Yams and Okra leaves in food are the cause of that, but scientifically it's not proven.

Fact #5. Nigeria has its own local Hollywood

The next one of fun facts about Nigeria regards the country's film industry. Everyone knows what Hollywood is. Some people know what Bollywood is. And only a few people know (until now) that in Nigeria, there is a local film industry, called Nollywood.

Fact #6. The national animal of Nigeria is the Eagle

The national animal of Nigeria is the eagle that belongs to the Accipitridae family. Some species are golden or black. Some have short wings and long tail. The bird has various symbolic meanings, but the most common are freedom, power, and judgment.

Fact #7. The most populated city of Africa is located in Nigeria

Here goes the next one of interesting facts about Nigeria. Lagos is the most populated city in Nigeria. Its population is over 14 million. What's curious is that it's not the country's capital, it's a financial center. The capital is the city of Abuja with 3,2 million.

Fact #8. Football is considered a national sport in Nigeria

Football is very popular in Nigeria. The country has its own Premier League. The Nigeria national football team has won the World Cup six times. They won the African Cup of Nations three times and once won the gold medal at the 1996 Summer Olympics.

Fact #9. Palm wine is one of the most popular drinks in Nigeria

Here’s another one of fun facts about Nigeria. Palm wine is an alcoholic drink, made of sap from various species of a palm tree, collected from its cut flower. Due to the yeasts, the sap begins fermenting, and within 2 hours, turns into a wine of 4% alcohol content.

Fact #10. The richest person in Africa is from Nigeria

Aliko Dangote is a billionaire businessman from Nigeria, known as the founder of Dangote Group. He was born in 1957 in Kano, Kano State. In 1981 he founded the Dangote Group which now is one of the largest industrial conglomerates in Africa.
