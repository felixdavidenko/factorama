Interesting facts about Turkey

Interesting facts about Turkey presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. Turkey is a state on the border of Europe and Asia, whose culture combines ancient Greek, Persian, Roman, Byzantine and Ottoman traditions. Sprawling across the Bosphorus, cosmopolitan Istanbul is home to the famous Hagia Sophia with its soaring dome and Christian mosaics, the majestic 17th-century Blue Mosque and the 1460s Topkapi Palace, the former residence of the Ottoman sultans. The country is also famous for its hot air-ballooning region, cross-continental swim race, ghost city of castles and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Turkey.

Fact #1. Turkey is a transcontinental country straddling both Europe and Asia

Here's the first one of fun facts about Turkey. The Republic of Turkey is a transcontinental country straddling Southeastern Europe and Western Asia. It's bordered on its northwest by Greece and Bulgaria. On its southeast it's bordered by Iraq, Iran and not only.

Fact #2. The world's oldest known megalithic ruins are located in Turkey

Göbekli Tepe is a Neolithic archaeological site in Southeastern Anatolia, Turkey. The artificial mound has a height of 15 m (50 ft) and is about 300 m (1,000 ft) in diameter. Its stone pillars are the world's oldest known megaliths, dated around 9,500 BC.

Fact #3. There is a tower in Turkey heavily featured in pop culture

Another one of interesting facts about Turkey regards the world famous Maiden's Tower in Istanbul. It was featured in the James Bond films, in the American show The Amazing Race 7, in the computer game Assassin's Creed: Revelations and not only.

Fact #4. One of the world's top hot air-ballooning destinations is located in Turkey

Cappadocia is a historical region in Central Anatolia famous for its sedimentary rocks which eroded into hundreds of spectacular pillars and minaret-like forms. This region is one of the top destinations in the world for hot-air ballooning, available in Göreme.

Fact #5. There is a transcontinental metro system in Turkey

The next one of fun facts about Turkey regards the Istanbul Metro. The metro system is connected with the underground systems of the Marmaray tunnel which connects Europe and Asia, hence, the Istanbul Metro is a transcontinental metro system.

Fact #6. There is a mosque in Turkey that once was a Christian church

Hagia Sophia is a late antique place of worship in Istanbul. Built in 537 it was the largest Christian church of the eastern Roman Empire. In 1453 it was converted to a mosque. In 1935 it became a museum. In 2020 it was reclassified as a mosque.

Fact #7. The "Evil eye" is one of the most popular Turkish souvenirs

Here's another one of interesting facts about Turkey. The nazar is an eye-shaped amulet believed to protect against the evil eye. It's often made of glass featuring concentric circles in blue, white and black color. The nazar is a popular choice of souvenir for tourists.

Fact #8. One of the earliest known international peace treaties was found in Turkey

Hattusa is the former capital of the Hittite Empire in the late Bronze Age. One of the most important discoveries at the site has been the royal archives of clay tablets. A copy on display in the UN in New York is one of the earliest known international peace treaties.

Fact #9. Oil wrestling is the national sport of Turkey

The next one of fun facts about Turkey regards oil wrestling. It’s also known as grease wrestling because the wrestlers douse themselves with olive oil. They also wear a type of hand-stitched leather trousers traditionally made of water buffalo hide.

Fact #10. There is the ghost city of castles in Turkey

Burj Al Babas is a residential village near the town of Mudurnu, Turkey. The houses are designed to resemble mini castles. Construction began in 2014, but eventually stalled. As of 2019, 587 of the planned 732 castles were started but none were finished.
