Interesting facts about Haiti

Interesting facts about Haiti presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Haiti is a country in the Caribbean, occupying the western part of the island of the same name and bordering the Dominican Republic. The Republic of Haiti won independence from France in 1804 in the course of the successful Haitian revolution and became the second independent state in America after the United States. It's the only independent state in America where the French language predominates, and one of the two, along with Canada, where it's the state language. The country is famous for its mountaintop fortress, the carnival, rum and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Haiti for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Haiti is the most populous country in the Caribbean Community

The first one of fun facts about Haiti regards its population. According to the U.N. data, the population of Haiti is 11,4 million people. This country is the most populous in the Caribbean Community and the 2nd most populous in the Caribbean after Cuba.

Fact #2. The Haitian Creole language is derived from French

There are two official languages in Haiti – French and Haitian Creole. French is spoken by over 42% of Haitians, but what's curious is that Haitian Creole vocabulary is 90% derived from French. So despite some grammar features, they are pretty the same.

Fact #3. People that discovered Haiti thought that they had discovered India

Here's another one of interesting facts about Haiti. It's known that the first Europeans arrived here on 5 December 1492. It happened during the first voyage of Christopher Columbus. What's curious is that initially he thought that he had discovered India.

Fact #4. Haiti is home to the largest mountaintop fortress in the Western Hemisphere

The Citadelle Laferriere, or simply the Citadel is a 19th century fortress that sits on top of the Bonnet a l'Eveque mount in Nord, Haiti. Being known as an icon of the Caribbean nation, in 1982 this fortress was inscribed in the UNESCO World Heritage List.

Fact #5. The name of Haiti is derived from its landscape

The next one of fun facts about Haiti regards its name. The country is known to have mountainous landscape. The name of the country is derived from the indigenous Arawak place-name called "Ayti", which can be literally translated as "The Mountainous land".

Fact #6. Haitian rum is known all over the world

One of the most popular and one of the most consumed alcoholic beverages in Haiti is rum. This beverage is considered the nation's finest and most famous exports according to international standards. The local rum is popular all over the world.

Fact #7. Haiti is one of the most biologically significant countries of the West Indies

Another one of interesting facts about Haiti regards its flora. According to the World Conservation Monitoring Centre, Haiti is one of the most biologically significant countries of the West Indies with over 5,600 plant species, 36% of which are endemic.

Fact #8. The Haitian patty is one of the best-known Haitian appetizers

The Haitian patty is a popular baked pastry filled with meat. Usually it's made of ground beef, turkey, chicken, herring or cod packed within a crispy crust. The Haitian patty is often served with spices and seasonings, as well as slaw, vinegar or vegetables.

Fact #9. The most popular sport in Haiti is football

Here’s the next one of fun facts about Haiti. Despite the growth in popularity of basketball, football is still the most popular sport here. In 1974, the national team was only the 2nd Caribbean team to make the World Cup. In 2007, they won the Nations Cup.

Fact #10. Hibiscus is considered the national flower of Haiti

Hibiscus is known as national but not official flower of Haiti. There are many reasons why this flower is considered national. The flower has medicinal use and rich religious history, plus, it can be found everywhere and it's much beloved by the people of Haiti.
