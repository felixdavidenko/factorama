Interesting facts about Belgium

Interesting facts about Belgium presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. Belgium is a country in Western Europe known for its medieval towns and Renaissance buildings. In the north of the country, in Flanders, they speak Dutch, and in the south, in Wallonia, they speak French. The east of Belgium is home to a large German-speaking community. The country's capital, Brussels, which speaks both Dutch and French, is renowned for its ornate guild houses on the Grand Place and elegant Art Nouveau buildings. The country is also famous for Brussels sprouts, bicycle races, World Heritage boat lifts and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Belgium.

Fact #1. There's a gate in Belgium that once was a prison, a customs, a granary and a church

Here's the first one of fun facts about Belgium. The Halle Gate is a medieval fortified city gate built in 1381 in Brussels. While the other gates were demolished, this one survived. At different times it served as a prison, a customs, a granary and a Lutheran church.

Fact #2. There are 6 different governments in Belgium

The governing system in Belgium is complex and made up of 6 different governments. The Belgian Federal, the Flemish, the Walloon, the French community, the German community governments and the government of the Brussels-Capital region.

Fact #3. One of the most popular tourist spots in Belgium is an artificial waterfall

Another one of interesting facts about Belgium regards the Coo Waterfall. It's not the highest but one of the best known waterfalls in Belgium. It was created in the 18th century by local monks who cut through a bend in the river to feed a watermill.

Fact #4. The Royal Museum of Fine Arts of Belgium was founded by Napoleon Bonaparte

The Royal Museum of Fine Arts of Belgium was founded in 1801 by Napoleon Bonaparte. In 1927 it was renamed to its current name: the Royal Museums of Fine Arts of Belgium. There are 6 of them, situated in the downtown Royal District, in Brussels.

Fact #5. Brussels sprout was named after the capital of Belgium

The next one of fun facts about Belgium regards the Brussels sprout. These species of cabbage first appeared in northern Europe during the 5th century, later being cultivated in the 13th century near Brussels, Belgium, from which they derived their name.

Fact #6. Belgium hosts some of the world's most prestigious classic cycle races

The classic cycle races are the most prestigious one-day professional road cycling races in the international calendar. Belgium hosts such classic cycle races as Gent-Wevelgem, Tour of Flanders, La Fleche Wallonne, Liege-Bastogne-Liege and not only.

Fact #7. One of the most beautiful squares in Europe is located in Belgium

Another one of interesting facts about Belgium regards the Grand Square. It's the central square and the most memorable landmark in Brussels. The Grand Square is one of the most beautiful squares in Europe and a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1998.

Fact #8. The highest point of Belgium serves as a transmitter between England and Germany

Signal de Botrange is the highest point in Belgium located at an altitude of 694 m (2,277 ft) above sea level. In 2013, a 50 m (160 ft) tower with parabolic antennas was agreed to provide data between London Stock Exchange and Frankfurt Stock Exchange.

Fact #9. Belgian waffles were introduced to the world in 1964

The next one of fun facts about Belgium regards its waffles. Belgian waffles were originally showcased in 1958 at Expo 58 in Brussels by a Belgian named Walter Cleyman. These waffles started gaining world popularity after the 1964 New York World's Fair.

Fact #10. Belgium hosts one of the most popular races on the F1 calendar

The Belgian Grand Prix is part of the Formula One World Championship. The race course is one of the oldest among all others. It was built in 1921. Due to the scenic landscape and historical background this race is one of the most popular on the F1 calendar.
