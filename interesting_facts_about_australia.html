Interesting facts about Australia

Interesting facts about Australia presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, and of course people. Australia is a country occupying the continent of the same name, which is washed by the Indian and Pacific Oceans. On the coasts of the continent, there are such large cities as Sydney, Brisbane, Melbourne, Perth, and Adelaide. The capital of Australia – Canberra is located far from the sea. The country's main attractions are the Sydney Opera House, the Great Barrier Reef, and Australia’s Big Things but not only. The country is also famous for kangaroos, the pink lake, the Australian Open, and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Australia for children, adults, the elderly, and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. Australia is both a country and a continent

Here’s the first one of fun facts about Australia. Australia (the country) is located in Australia (the continent). Experts often disagree about is the country a continent and vice versa. So very often, the country and the continent are not quite the same.

Fact #2. There is no official language in Australia

Despite being known as an English-speaking country, this language in Australia has no official status. English in this country is considered the national language only de facto, since European settlement. However, it's used by more than 73% of the population.

Fact #3. In Australia, summer is winter and winter is summer

Here’s another one of interesting facts about Australia. The country is located in the Southern Hemisphere, where the seasons are inverted in relation to the Northern Hemisphere. So in this country, winter is Jun, Jul, Aug, and summer is Dec, Jan, and Feb.

Fact #4. The Sydney Opera House is one of the world's most recognizable landmarks

The Sydney Opera House is a truly heritage structure. In 2003 it was inscribed in the New South Wales Heritage Register. In 2005 it was inscribed on the Australian National Heritage List. In 2007 it was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List.

Fact #5. There are twice as many kangaroos as inhabitants in Australia

The next one of fun facts about Australia regards kangaroos. It is known that the population of the country is just over 25 million. It's also known that there are over 50 million kangaroos in Australia. So there are twice as many kangaroos as inhabitants.

Fact #6. Almost every park in Australia has grills for making steaks

Barbecue, also called "Barbie" or "BBQ" is a very popular summer pastime in Australia. Lots of coin-operated or free public barbecues can be found in parks all around the country. So in Australia, barbecue can fairly be called an element of cultural tradition.

Fact #7. The world’s longest barrier fence is located in Australia

Here goes another one of interesting facts about Australia. The Dingo Fence, also called the Dog Fence was built in 1885 to protect the sheep flocks from wild dingoes. This fence is considered the world's longest barrier fence. Its length is 5,614 km (3,488 mi).

Fact #8. Australia gave the world 16 Nobel Prize winners

Since 1915 there have been 16 Australians who won the Nobel Prize. Half of these prizes (8) have been awarded in the field of Physiology or Medicine. Other prizes have been awarded in the field of Physics (4), Literature (2), Chemistry (1), and Peace (1).

Fact #9. One of the most visited landmarks in Australia is the pink lake

The next one of fun facts about Australia regards Lake Hillier which is a saline lake, located on the south coast of the western part of the country. The lake is worldwide famous for its pink color. Red algae and bacteria are the reason why the lake is pink.

Fact #10. The Australian Alps receive more snow than the Swiss Alps

The Australian Alps is the highest mountain range in the country with multiple peaks exceeding 2,000 m (6,600 ft) above sea level. Above 1,400 m (4,600 ft) snow may persist for 4-6 months, making this country beloved by those who prefer winter sports.
