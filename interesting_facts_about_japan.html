Interesting facts about Japan

Interesting facts about Japan presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports, and of course people. Japan is an island nation in the Pacific Ocean, famous for its densely populated cities, imperial palaces, national parks, temples and sanctuaries. The Shinkansen high-speed rail network is represented on all the main islands of the Japanese archipelago: Kyushu with the subtropical beaches of Okinawa, Honshu with Tokyo and Hiroshima with a memorial to the victims of the atomic bombing, and Hokkaido with ski resorts. The country's capital, Tokyo, is the center of Japanese pop culture, known for its skyscrapers and shops. This is a country of Torii gates, Sakura trees, Samurai warriors and not only. Here you will find amazing, funny and of course interesting facts about Japan for children, adults, the elderly and in general everyone who is interested in the world that surrounds us and the facts that it is filled with. Enjoy reading.

Fact #1. There are over 32,000 Torii gates in Japan

The first one of fun facts about Japan regards Torii gates. A Torii is a traditional Japanese gate that marks the transition from the mundane to the sacred. There are over 32,000 Torii gates in Japan, 10,000 of which belong to the Fushimi Inari Shrine.

Fact #2. Japan is one of the largest island countries in the world

Most people know that Japan is an island country. Little less people know that Japan consists of 5 main islands. Few people know that there are 6,852 islands in total. And very few people know that approximately 430 of those islands are inhabited.

Fact #4. In Japan, tattoos are considered rude and offensive

In Japan, Tattoos are stigmatized for being associated with gangs and crime. Regardless of who you are and where did you come from, if having a tattoos, it's better to cover them. Otherwise, you will not be able to visit pools, gyms, beaches and some cafes.

Fact #5. There are over 3,8 million vending machines in Japan

The next one of interesting facts about Japan regards vending machines. They are everywhere and they are selling everything, from snacks and drinks to crabs and noodles. Japan has the world's largest number of vending machines - over 3,8 million.

Fact #6. Koi is considered a national fish in Japan

Koi, also known as Brocaded Carp is a colored variety of the Amur Carp, usually kept for decorative purposes in special koi-ponds. This fish has important symbolic meaning in Japanese literature, culture and art. It's a symbol of luck, wealth and prosperity.

Fact #7. To take a nap at work is a usual thing in Japan

Here goes the next one of fun facts about Japan. The Japanese are known for their diligence and hard work. Since their working day is long, employers try to let their employees recharge and avoid fatigue. So, work naps are becoming a common thing in Japan.

Fact #8. More than 1,500 earthquakes happen in Japan each year

The seismic activity level in Japan is very high. From year to year this figure may vary, but it's estimated that there are over 1,500 earthquakes that happen in Japan each year. Fortunately, most of them are insignificant shakes, but few ones can be really devastating.

Fact #9. The Sakura Tree is a symbol of hope in Japan

Another one of interesting facts about Japan regards Sakura. This is a national tree in Japan. Such a tree is not just a symbol of spring, it's a symbol of renewal and hope. There is also a custom in Japan called "Hanami", which is a picnicking beneath a Sakura tree.

Fact #10. The national Japanese beverage is Sake

Sake is a national Japanese alcoholic beverage made by fermenting polished rice. Unlike wine, in which alcohol is produced by fermenting sugar, sake is produced by a brewing process, where starch is converted into sugars, which ferment into alcohol.
