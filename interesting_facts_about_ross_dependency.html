Interesting facts about Ross Dependency

Interesting facts about Ross Dependency presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the land, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science and of course people. The Ross Dependency, a sector of Antarctica claimed by New Zealand, is part of the Kingdom of New Zealand. The area is named after James Clark Ross, the British sailor who discovered the Ross Sea. It's famous for the southernmost sea in the world, the world's smallest baleen whales, the floating airfield, the Antarctic labyrinth and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about the Ross Dependency.

Fact #1. Ross Dependency is a New Zealand's part of the continent of Antarctica

Here's the first one of interesting facts about Ross Dependency. This is a region of Antarctica claimed by New Zealand. The Dependency is named after the British Royal Navy officer and polar explorer James Clark Ross who discovered the Ross Sea.

Fact #2. Ross Dependency is home to the floating airfield

Williams Field, also called the Willy Field (NZWD) is a United States Antarctic Program airfield, located some 11 km (7 mi) from Ross Island. It has two runways lying on 8 m (25 ft) of snow over 3 m (10 ft) of ice, floating over 550 m (1,800 ft) of water.

Fact #3. The southernmost sea in the world is located in Ross Dependency

Another one of fun facts about Ross Dependency regards its sea. The Ross Sea is a deep bay of Antarctica's Southern Ocean, named after James Ross. It has an area of 637,000 sq km (246,000 sq mi) and is the southernmost sea in the world.

Fact #4. Ross Dependency is home to the world's smallest baleen whales

Ross Sea is home to about 21,000 individual species of Antarctic minke whales. First described in 1870, such a whale grows up to 11 m (32 ft) long and weighs up to more than 10 tons. Minke whales are known as the smallest baleen whales in the world.

Fact #5. Ross Dependency is home to the Antarctic Labyrinth

Here goes the next one of interesting facts about Ross Dependency. The Labyrinth is an extensive and deeply eroded flat area, located in Victoria Land. The area was named so because of the dolerite which during erosion formed in a labyrinth shape.

Fact #6. Ross Dependency is the second smallest Antarctic claim

Ross Dependency by land area is the second smallest Antarctic claim, compared to other countries claims. Most of the land mass claimed by New Zealand as Ross Dependency is either in the Ross Sea, in the Ross Ice Shelf or the Antarctic Ocean.

Fact #7. Ross Dependency has the ice shelf about the size of France

The next one of fun facts about Ross Dependency regards its ice shelf. The Ross Ice Shelf with an area of 500,809 sq km (193,363 sq mi) is known as the largest ice shelf of Antarctica. Being about 800 km (497 mi) across, it's about the size of France.

Fact #8. The southernmost active volcano on Earth is located in Ross Dependency

Mount Erebus is the sixth-highest ultra mountain on the continent, the second highest volcano in Antarctica and the southernmost active volcano on Earth. It's height is 3,794 m (12,448 ft) and it has been active since approx 1,3 million years ago.

Fact #9. Ross Dependency has an island considered to be part of the mainland

Another one of interesting facts about Ross Dependency regards the mainland island. Ross Island is located in the Ross Sea off the coast of Victoria Land. Because of the permanent presence of the ice sheet it's considered to be part of the mainland.

Fact #10. The largest settlement in Antarctica is located in Ross Dependency

The McMurdo Station, located on the south tip of Ross Island is a United States Antarctic research station. It consists of more than 85 buildings and is inhabited with about 1,260 residents. This station is known as the largest settlement in Antarctica.
