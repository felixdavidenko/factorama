Interesting facts about Thailand

Interesting facts about Thailand presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports, and of course people. Thailand is a state in Southeast Asia with numerous tropical beaches, luxurious royal palaces, ancient ruins, and richly decorated Buddhist temples. In Bangkok, the country's capital, quiet residential areas and iconic temples line the canals alongside ultra-modern skyscrapers. The country is also famous for the world-famous floating and railway markets, the beer bottle temple, the fighting fish, and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about Thailand.

Fact #1. Thailand and Siam are the same country

The first one of fun facts about Thailand regards its name. According to Bowring Treaty, the name Siam had an official status until 1939 when it was changed to Thailand. Thailand was reverted to Siam from 1946 to 1948, after that it was again reverted to Thailand.

Fact #2. There is a festival in honor of the city pillar in Thailand

Intakhin is the name of the city pillar of Chiang Mai. The pillar was erected by King Mangrai at the founding of the city on April 12, 1296. During the Intakhin Festival which lasts eight days, offerings of flowers, candies, and incense are made to this city pillar.

Fact #3. Thailand is a land of people

Another one of interesting facts about Thailand regards the etymology of its name. According to the scholars, Thai simply means “people”, since in rural areas the word “Thai” was used instead of the usual Thai word “Khon” for people. Thus Thailand is a land of people.

Fact #4. There are more Thai dishes on the list of the "World's 50 Best Foods" than any other

In 2017, in an online poll of 35,000 people worldwide by CNN Travel, 7 Thai dishes got into the “World's 50 Best Foods” list. These were: tom yam (4th), pad thai (5th), som tam (6th), massaman curry (10th), green curry (19th), Thai fried rice (24th), and Nam to mu (36th).

Fact #5. Thailand is the golf capital of Asia

The next one of fun facts about Thailand regards golf. The country is home to more than 200 world-class golf courses nationwide. Thailand attracts golfers from all around the world and hosts PGA tournaments. Because of that, it has been called the golf capital of Asia.

Fact #6. The world's largest parliament building is located in Thailand

The Sappaya-Sapasathan is the meeting place of the National Assembly of Thailand. It houses offices, museums, a convention center, a banquet hall, and a seminar room. With a total area of 424,000 sq m (4,563,898 sq ft) it is the world’s largest parliament building.

Fact #7. Robots in Thailand are widely used for the treatment purposes

Another one of interesting facts about Thailand regards the industry of medical robots, widely used in diagnosis, surgery, and rehabilitation. In 2017, a reputable Ramathibodi Hospital in Bangkok successfully performed the first robot-assisted brain surgery in Asia.

Fact #8. There are three seasons in Thailand

Thailand is divided into three seasons. The monsoon season (mid-May to mid-October) is the wettest period of the year. Winter (mid-October to mid-February) has dry weather with mild temperature. Summer (mid-February to mid-May) has a long period of warm weather.

Fact #9. There are about 20,000 edible insect farms in Thailand

The next one of fun facts about Thailand regards edible insects which are common in this country. Many markets in Thailand sell deep-fried grasshoppers, crickets, termites, silkworms, ant eggs, etc. There are about 20,000 insect farms in 53 of the country's 76 provinces.

Fact #10. The biggest motorcycle event in Asia takes place in Thailand

Phuket Bike Week is the biggest motorcycle event in Asia. Bikers from all over the world join this event every year. The event highlights include bike parades “Ride for Peace”, Miss Phuket Bike Week competition, exhibitions, contests, live entertainments, and not only.
