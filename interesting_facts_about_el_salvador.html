Interesting facts about El Salvador

Interesting facts about El Salvador presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. El Salvador is a small country in Central America, famous for its beaches on the Pacific coast, surfing spots and mountain ranges. The Trail of Flowers, a famous tourist route, passes through coffee plantations, rainforests with waterfalls and mountain towns, including Juayua, where the Gastronomic Festival is held, and Ataco, famous for its colorful wall paintings. In the country's capital, San Salvador, surrounded by picturesque volcanoes, numerous museums and the National Theater are worth visiting. The country is also famous for its archaeological sites, terrestrial ecosystems, turtle species, hammocks and not only. So, get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about El Salvador.

Fact #1. El Salvador is the smallest country in Central America

The first one of fun facts about El Salvador regards its size. The Republic of El Salvador is a country in Central America washed by the Pacific Ocean. It encompasses the area of 21,041 sq km (8,124 sq mi) and is known as the smallest country in the region.

Fact #2. El Salvador was named after Jesus Christ

Explorer Pedro de Alvarado named the new province San Salvador, which means Saint Savior that is Jesus Christ. The Provincia De Nuestro Senor Jesus Cristo, El Salvador was later shortened to the Republic of El Salvador and finally settled as El Salvador.

Fact #3. The earliest artifacts from Mesoamerica were found in El Salvador

The next one of interesting facts about El Salvador regards Tazumal. It's a Maya archaeological site in the department of Santa Ana. Metal artifacts excavated from this site are dated to the 8th century AD and considered the earliest found in Mesoamerica.

Fact #4. The first top-class airport in the isthmus is located in El Salvador

El Salvador International Airport (SAL), located in the city of San Luis Talpa is the 1st Category-1 certified airport in the isthmus. It’s also the 2nd in the region with the most competitive infrastructure and the 3rd best airport in the Caribbean.

Fact #5. The turquoise-browed bird is the national symbol of El Salvador

Another one of fun facts about El Salvador regards the Turquoise-browed motmot. Also known as Torogoz, it's a colorful 34 cm (13 in) long forest bird that weighs up to 65 g (2,3 oz). In 1999 it was officially declared the country's national bird.

Fact #6. El Salvador produces over 7 million tons of sugarcane annually

One of the most developed sectors of the economy of El Salvador is agriculture. The country annually produces over 7 million tons of its main crop sugarcane, in addition to some smaller amounts of such agricultural products as maize, beans, coffee, etc.

Fact #7. The most active volcano in Central America is located in El Salvador

Here goes the next one of interesting facts about El Salvador. The country is home to Izalco stratovolcano. It has continuously erupted at least 51 times since 1770. Because of that it earned its famous nickname "Lighthouse of the Pacific".

Fact #8. Each room of the National Palace of El Salvador has its own color

The National Palace of El Salvador has four main rooms, each having its own specific color and purpose. The Yellow Room for the President; the Pink Room for the Supreme Court; the Blue Room for the Legislature; the Red Room for receptions.

Fact #9. El Salvador is also known as the country of the 40 minutes

Another one of fun facts about El Salvador regards its size. Because of a small size, the country is often referred to as "The country of 40 minutes". In less than 40 minutes you can get from the capital to any point: beaches, mountains, reserves, etc.

Fact #10. More than 86% of the population of El Salvador is Mestizos

More than 86% of the population of El Salvador identify themselves with Mestizos ancestry. Almost 13% of Salvadorans are ethnically Spanish. There are also citizens of Irish, English, German, Swiss, French, Italian, Hungarian and Polish descent.
