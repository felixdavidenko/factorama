Interesting facts about the Philippines

Interesting facts about the Philippines presented here are related to the most diverse topics of both the modern life and the past of the country, including (but not limited to) its history, heritage, nature, culture, science, sports and of course people. The Philippines is a state in Southeast Asia in the western part of the Pacific Ocean, consisting of more than 7000 islands. Manila, the capital of the country, is known for its waterfront and centuries-old Binondo Chinatown. The old walled city is located in the Intramuros area. Here you will find the Church of St. Augustine, built in the Baroque style in the 17th century, and Fort Santiago, a famous fortress and military prison. The country is also famous for its own cradle of civilization, one of the world's largest butterflies, soot fest and not only. So get ready to explore some interesting and fun facts about the Philippines.

Fact #1. The Philippines consists of 7,641 islands

Here goes the first one of fun facts about the Philippines. The Republic of the Philippines is an archipelagic country, located in Southeast Asia. It consists of about 7,641 islands categorized under three geographical regions: Luzon, Visayas and Mindanao.

Fact #2. Philippines was named after the Prince of Asturias

During his expedition in 1542, Spanish explorer Ruy Lopez Villalobos named the islands "Felipinas", after the Prince of Asturias Philip II of Spain. The full name of the Republic of the Philippines was included in the 1935 constitution as the name of the country.

Fact #3. The Philippines is washed by the sea of the same name

Another one of interesting facts about the Philippines regards the Philippine Sea. It's a marginal sea of the Western Pacific Ocean, located east of the Philippine archipelago (hence the name) that occupies the area of about 5 mil sq km (2x106 sq mi).

Fact #4. There is a fortress in the Philippines declared the shrine of freedom

Fort Santiago is a defense fortress built in 1593 in the city of Manila. The fort is of a triangular form and has a perimeter of 620 m (2,030 ft). Today it's one of the country's most important historical sites which in 1950 was declared as a Shrine of Freedom.

Fact #5. The deepest oceanic trench on Earth is located in the Philippine Sea

Here's the next one of fun facts about the Philippines. The prominent feature of the Philippine Sea is the presence of several deep sea trenches, including the Mariana Trench. It's the deepest oceanic trench on Earth with a max depth of 10,984 m (36,037 ft).

Fact #6. The largest of the extant eagles in the world is the national bird of the Philippines

The Philippine Eagle is an endangered species of eagle endemic only to the Philippines. It's considered the largest of the extant eagles in the world. Among the world's rarest and most powerful birds it has been declared the national bird of the Philippines.

Fact #7. The Philippines is one of the most populated countries in the world

Here goes another one of interesting facts about the Philippines. With a population of over 109 mil people, this country is one of the world’s most populated. As of 2020, it's the 8th most populated country in Asia and the 12th most populated in the world.

Fact #8. The earliest found document in the Philippines is the copper acquittance

The Laguna copperplate is an official document (acquittance) inscribed in the Shaka year 822, written in Old Malay using the Kawi script. It was found in 1989 and deciphered in 1992. It's the earliest known calendar-dated document used in the Philippines.

Fact #9. People in the Philippines don't eat with chopsticks

Here’s the next one of fun facts about the Philippines. Unlike many Asians, most people here don't eat with chopsticks. Even despite the rice being the primary staple food, the main pairing of utensils seen at the dining table are spoon and fork, not chopsticks.

Fact #10. The Philippines has its own cradle of civilization

The Tabon Caves are a complex of 215 caves on Palawan Island, in the Philippines. Because of the oldest human bones found in the Philippines this site is also known as "The Cradle of Civilization". In 2011 the complex was declared the National Cultural Treasure.
